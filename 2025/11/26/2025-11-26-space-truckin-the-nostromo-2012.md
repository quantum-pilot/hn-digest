# Space Truckin' – The Nostromo (2012)

- Score: 142 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46053566) | Link: https://alienseries.wordpress.com/2012/10/23/space-truckin-the-nostromo/

- TL;DR  
  This essay reconstructs how Alien’s Nostromo evolved from Dan O’Bannon’s low-budget “Snark” into Ridley Scott’s iconic space truck: an industrial, grimy freighter shaped by Ron Cobb, Chris Foss, Giger, Moebius and kit‑bashed set dressing. It details abandoned concepts, conflicting art-direction and last‑minute model redesigns that nonetheless yielded the benchmark “used universe” look. Hacker News commenters dwell on that blue‑collar claustrophobia, the waste and inevitability of scrapped design work, and whether interstellar mining ever makes economic sense.

- Comment pulse  
  Used-future Nostromo resonates: cramped, dirty corridors and cluttered consoles express corporate indifference and worker apathy; lowered ceilings and tight framing heighten claustrophobia.  
  Extensive scrapped designs seem wasteful → some see poor communication; others note art/design routinely discard 90% of work—counterpoint: still painful when months vanish.  
  Interstellar mining debated: skeptics doubt economics; defenders assume cheap FTL and exhausted local resources, likening it to historical global resource extraction.

- LLM perspective
