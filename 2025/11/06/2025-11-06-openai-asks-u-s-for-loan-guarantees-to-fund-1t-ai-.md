# OpenAI asks U.S. for loan guarantees to fund $1T AI expansion

- Score: 184 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=45830380) | Link: https://investinglive.com/stock-market-update/icymi-openai-asks-us-for-loan-guarantees-to-fund-1-trillion-ai-expansion-20251105/

- TL;DR
  OpenAI’s CFO said the company is exploring U.S. government “backstops” to reduce borrowing costs for a massive compute buildout, fueling reports of $1T-scale ambitions amid Oracle/SoftBank megaprojects. Commenters note Sam Altman disputes datacenter guarantees, saying discussions focus on government-owned infrastructure or fab-related guarantees, not an OpenAI bailout. Debate centers on moral hazard and 2008-style risks vs. industrial policy that could be structured as limited first/second‑loss tranches. Others question whether $1T on datacenters beats alternatives like grid, fabs, or biopharma investment.

- Comment pulse
  - Clarification: Altman says no guarantees for OpenAI datacenters; prefers government-owned AI infra; limited fab-related guarantees discussed, no formal application → article’s $1T claim questioned.
  - Risk socialization: loan guarantees shift downside to taxpayers, echoing Fannie/Freddie—counterpoint: a sector-wide mezzanine backstop with first/second-loss tranches could cap public exposure.
  - Opportunity cost: $1T on fast-depreciating compute vs buying years of global wafer-fab tools, grid upgrades, or biopharma R&D with broader public returns.
