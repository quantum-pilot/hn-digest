# Petition to formally recognize open source work as civic service in Germany

- Score: 491 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46078770) | Link: https://www.openpetition.de/petition/online/anerkennung-von-open-source-arbeit-als-ehrenamt-in-deutschland#petition-main

### TL;DR
Petition in Germany seeks to classify open-source work as officially recognized volunteering (Ehrenamt), on par with club, youth, or rescue services. Supporters argue FOSS underpins critical infrastructure, is mostly unpaid, and deserves tax-advantaged expense reimbursements, simpler non-profit status, and liability protections. Discussion centers on edge cases: not all OSS is beneficial, bureaucratic “good OSS” certification, potential corporate misuse to disguise paid work, and incentive distortions. Others note civic-service rules already require vetted non-profit hosts, making abuse and casual GitHub coding unlikely to qualify.

### Comment pulse
- Open source isn’t always public good → bureaucratic “good OSS” certification would reward paperwork, not impact — counterpoint: enforcement flaws shouldn’t block creating support structures.  
- Tax and labor abuse fears → companies might label directed feature work as “volunteering”; any incentives risk Hacktoberfest-style spam unless eligibility and caps are strict.  
- Design details matter → civic-service status likely flows via approved non-profit host orgs; debate over prioritizing maintainers vs contributors and excluding pet or unmerged work.

### LLM perspective
- View: Treat digital commons like physical ones, but tie recognition to demonstrable public-interest impact and transparent governance, not license alone.  
- Impact: If crafted well, policy could reduce maintainer burnout, stabilize critical infrastructure projects, and normalize funding travel, hosting, and coordination costs.  
- Watch next: Look for German ministries consulting OSS foundations, draft criteria for eligible organizations, and pilots that reimburse expenses without resembling employment.
