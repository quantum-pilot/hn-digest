# I am stepping down as the CEO of Mastodon

- Score: 305 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=45969909) | Link: https://blog.joinmastodon.org/2025/11/my-next-chapter-with-mastodon/

TL;DR
- Eugen Rochko is resigning as Mastodon’s CEO after a decade, transferring trademarks and assets to the Mastodon non-profit and moving into an advisory role. He cites burnout, intense scrutiny, and a desire for stronger institutional guardrails so the project outlives its founder. Hacker News reactions mix alarm over losing a visionary leader with praise for the governance move, debates about toxicity in user communities, and reflections on Mastodon’s calmer, non-algorithmic alternative to enshittified, influencer-driven social platforms.

Comment pulse
- Loss of founder seen as dire → Commenters call Rochko indispensable, fear stagnation—counterpoint: Mastodon’s decentralization and non-profit structure could enable leadership succession.
- Governance model praised → Commenters contrast Mastodon’s asset-transfer structure with messy control fights at WordPress or RubyGems, yet still scrutinize board independence and affiliations.
- Community and product split-view → Some laud Mastodon’s calm, non-algorithmic, anti-influencer design; others note toxic interactions, low engagement, uneven moderation driving leaders and users away.
