# CachyOS: Fast and Customizable Linux Distribution

- Score: 251 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46095585) | Link: https://cachyos.org/

### TL;DR
CachyOS is an Arch-based Linux distro that chases maximum performance and usability: it ships CPU-tuned kernels (BORE and other schedulers), LTO/PGO/BOLT-optimized packages, and an online installer where you pick desktop environment, filesystem, boot manager, and gaming stack. HN users like it as a fast, beginner-friendly Arch derivative, especially with KDE and Nvidia, plus one-click gaming helpers. Criticism centers on broken i3/X11 setups, concerns about experimental patches, and debate over whether “gaming/customizable” branding really distinguishes it from mainstream distros.

### Comment pulse
- Arch benefits without Arch install → Users report fast, stable systems, easy online installer, and one-click gaming setup—counterpoint: most mainstream distros are already highly customizable.  
- Rough edges for niche WMs → Several report broken i3/X11 installs and linking errors, blaming bit-rotting X11 stacks and CachyOS’s experimental patches increasing bug risk.  
- Gaming-focused distros debate → Some like tuned gaming kernels, schedulers, Proton forks; others argue base distros should fix defaults and avoid fragile, hard-to-maintain custom stacks.  

### LLM perspective
