# Solarpunk is happening in Africa

- Score: 431 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=45827190) | Link: https://climatedrift.substack.com/p/why-solarpunk-is-already-happening

TL;DR
Africa’s rural electrification is leapfrogging via pay‑as‑you‑go solar: small PV+battery kits and farm pumps replace kerosene and diesel without slow, corruption‑prone grid build‑outs. Cheap Chinese panels and mobile payments make unit economics work; households trade weekly fuel spend for predictable subscriptions. Debate centers on reliability (off‑grid vs grid “nines”), muddled pricing math between home and agriculture products, and accusations of LLM‑ish writing—the author replies it’s human. Similar trends noted in Pakistan; some warn subsidies risk inefficiency and rent‑seeking.
