# The realities of being a pop star

- Score: 87 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46016613) | Link: https://itscharlibb.substack.com/p/the-realities-of-being-a-pop-star

## TL;DR
Charli XCX writes a candid essay on pop stardom as both fantasy and farce: intoxicating parties, free luxury, adoring fans, and godlike moments on stage, contrasted with endless liminal spaces, projection, misogyny, and the pressure to stay inside a marketable “box.” She pushes back on being seen as a shallow party girl, reflects on staying grounded amid yes‑people, and rejects the idea that pop stars must be moral role models, arguing that art’s value is in fantasy, performance, and escape.

## Comment pulse

- Fame looks glamorous, but outcomes skew bleak → money mismanagement, broken families, endless reunion tours, and loneliness; the stage high rarely compensates for long-term costs.  
- Industry economics are brutal → gross numbers hide debt and fragility; like athletes and mid-tier authors, most “famous” artists end up financially precarious—counterpoint: top 0.1% still do extremely well.
