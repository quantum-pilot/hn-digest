# How the UK lost its shipbuilding industry

- Score: 212 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=45871141) | Link: https://www.construction-physics.com/p/how-the-uk-lost-its-shipbuilding

- TL;DR
  - Britain once led shipbuilding by keeping capital and management light and leaning on skilled, unionized labor for bespoke ships. After WWII, the market pivoted to welded, block-built, standardized, larger vessels and fixed-price contracts—favoring big, modern yards. UK firms, constrained by cramped sites, demarcated labor, weak planning, scarce finance, and rational risk aversion, fell behind despite reports, consolidation, subsidies, and nationalization. Global competition intensified (Japan→Korea→China). By 2023 the UK built no commercial ships. HN debates management vs unions, strategic self-sufficiency vs subsidies, and notes defense work persists.
