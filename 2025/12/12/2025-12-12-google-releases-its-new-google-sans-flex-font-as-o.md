# Google releases its new Google Sans Flex font as open source

- Score: 226 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46246802) | Link: https://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2025/11/google-sans-flex-font-ubuntu

- TL;DR  
  Google has open-sourced its new “next‑generation brand typeface” Google Sans Flex under the SIL Open Font License. It’s a highly flexible variable sans-serif with axes for weight, width, optical size, slant, stroke grade, and rounded terminals, tuned for screens and Android-style UI work. The article shows it as a clean Ubuntu system font but notes Linux desktops barely exploit variable-font magic. HN readers like the licensing and flexibility, but criticize weak character disambiguation and debate better fonts for coding.
