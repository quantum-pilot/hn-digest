# Id Software devs form "wall-to-wall" union

- Score: 274 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46246845) | Link: https://www.rockpapershotgun.com/id-software-devs-form-wall-to-wall-union-with-165-workers-at-doom-studio-the-latest-to-vote-in-favour

- TL;DR  
165 staff at id Software—developers, artists, programmers and others—have formed a “wall‑to‑wall” union under the Communications Workers of America. Microsoft recognized it via its 2022 labor‑neutrality agreement, expanding union presence at its game studios to nearly 4,000 workers. Organizers highlight forced return‑to‑office policies, mass layoffs, crunch, and unfair pay as key issues, amid wider protests at The Game Awards. Hacker News commenters debate unions as a needed counterweight versus risks to fragile studios and complications around immigration and temp labor.

- Comment pulse  
  - Unions rebalance power in downturns → bargaining can resist unilateral RTO, crunch, layoffs — counterpoint: excessive demands or two‑tier deals bankrupt studios or split workers.  
  - Industrial “wall‑to‑wall” union model → contrasts with craft unions like The Animation Guild, but could import Hollywood‑style escalating overtime rates that discourage abusive scheduling.  
  - Union politics intersect with immigration → CWA affiliate sought to end OPT visas, prompting debate over unions protecting wages versus opposing pathways workers rely on.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: A flagship studio union under CWA normalizes game‑dev organizing beyond QA, making collective bargaining an expectation at publishers.  
  - Impact: Strong overtime, layoff, and remote‑work clauses here could become templates for other Microsoft studios and rival AAA publishers.  
  - Watch next: contract details on crunch pay, severance, and RTO; whether contractors and outsourced teams are included or coordinated with.
