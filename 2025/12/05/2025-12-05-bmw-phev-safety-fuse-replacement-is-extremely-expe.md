# BMW PHEV: Safety fuse replacement is extremely expensive

- Score: 418 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46155619) | Link: https://evclinic.eu/2025/12/04/2021-phev-bmw-ibmucp-21f37e-post-crash-recovery-when-eu-engineering-becomes-a-synonym-for-unrepairable-generating-waste/

## TL;DR

BMW’s plug‑in hybrids use a “safety fuse” (pyrofuse) integrated into a sealed, cryptographically locked battery control module (iBMUCP). Even minor impacts can trigger it, disabling the high‑voltage system. Because the module is welded shut, tightly software‑locked, and requires expensive OEM tools plus risky reprogramming to re‑commission, the practical fix is replacing the entire unit for ~€4–5k, with a real risk of accidentally bricking the whole battery. The author argues this design boosts costs and waste, undermining EV eco claims; commenters broaden it to German/modern car engineering, safety/liability, and right‑to‑repair concerns.
