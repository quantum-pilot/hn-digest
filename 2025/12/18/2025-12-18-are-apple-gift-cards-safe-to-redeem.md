# Are Apple gift cards safe to redeem?

- Score: 487 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46313061) | Link: https://daringfireball.net/linked/2025/12/17/are-apple-gift-cards-safe-to-redeem

### TL;DR
A developer’s Apple account was completely locked after he tried to redeem a seemingly tampered $500 Apple gift card, cutting him off from iCloud and all media purchases. After days of press attention and escalation to Apple Executive Relations, the account was restored—but Apple has not explained the underlying cause, the decision process, or why the response was so extreme. Commenters worry this reveals how fragile crucial cloud accounts are and how opaque fraud systems can arbitrarily erase digital lives.

---

### Comment pulse

- Gift cards behave like semi-anonymous cash → manufactured-spend schemes show how legitimate use is hard to distinguish from fraud, so fraud filters get very aggressive.  
- Centralized accounts are critical infrastructure now → losing Apple/Google access affects communication, ID, photos, and purchases, yet support remains “it’s just an online account” level.  
- Tension: stop fraud vs protect users → stricter defenses may require invasive identity checks and more surveillance—counterpoint: clearer rights and appeal processes could mitigate harm without maximal KYC.  
- Users don’t really “own” digital purchases → licenses can vanish with an account; some want law to treat purchases as property, others prefer a formal “digital rights” framework.
