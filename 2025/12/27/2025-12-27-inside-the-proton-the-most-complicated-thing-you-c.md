# Inside the proton, the ‘most complicated thing you could possibly imagine’ (2022)

- Score: 116 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46398666) | Link: https://www.quantamagazine.org/inside-the-proton-the-most-complicated-thing-imaginable-20221019/

### TL;DR
Protons aren’t simple three‑quark marbles but quantum objects whose “appearance” changes with the energy you probe them with. Classic SLAC experiments saw three valence quarks; HERA revealed a seething sea of gluons and short‑lived quark–antiquark pairs, as described by quantum chromodynamics. A new machine‑learning analysis of 50 years of data finds evidence for a persistent charm quark–antiquark component, making the proton occasionally a five‑quark molecule. This matters for LHC predictions, cosmic‑ray neutrinos, and future Electron‑Ion Collider studies.
