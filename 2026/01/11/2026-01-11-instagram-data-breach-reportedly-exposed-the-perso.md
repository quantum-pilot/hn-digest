# Instagram data breach reportedly exposed the personal info of 17.5M users

- Score: 185 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46576337) | Link: https://www.engadget.com/cybersecurity/an-instagram-data-breach-reportedly-exposed-the-personal-info-of-175-million-users-192105616.html

### TL;DR
Malwarebytes claims a dataset of 17.5M Instagram users’ details (usernames, emails, phone numbers, physical addresses, etc.) is being sold on the dark web, tying it to a 2024 Instagram API exposure. Many users simultaneously reported waves of password reset emails. Instagram counters that there was no breach of its systems, only an issue that let an external party mass-trigger password reset emails, now “fixed.” HN commenters are largely skeptical of a real leak, citing weak evidence and long‑standing password‑reset abuse.

---

### Comment pulse
- “Leak” skepticism → Anyone can trigger resets via public usernames; this looks like nuisance traffic, not proof of compromised Instagram data — counterpoint: if emails were breached elsewhere, resets could enable takeovers.  
- Incentives question → Security vendors benefit from labeling scraped/public or guessed data as “breaches,” which plays well with nontechnical executives and media.  
- Evidence gap → Malwarebytes’ claim lacks technical detail; commenters suspect scraped data, old credential lists, or third‑party app leaks rather than a core Instagram breach.
