# ‘ELITE’: The Palantir app ICE uses to find neighborhoods to raid

- Score: 331 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46637127) | Link: https://werd.io/elite-the-palantir-app-ice-uses-to-find-neighborhoods-to-raid/

## TL;DR
404 Media reports that Palantir’s ELITE app lets ICE visualize “targets” on a map, score address confidence, and pull detailed immigrant dossiers. Ben Werdmuller argues this enables large-scale racialized raids and forces Palantir employees to confront their complicity in abusive enforcement. Hacker News debates whether blame lies mainly with Palantir or with immigration policy and data-hoarding agencies, how much ethical nuance around ICE is still tenable, and whether other tech firms should actively hire away Palantir engineers.

## Comment pulse
- Some see Palantir as mundane vendor; claim danger is agency policy and data access — counterpoint: building and selling such tools is itself culpable.
- Debate over “nuance”: one side posits ethical ICE mandate but bad methods; critics say the mandate itself is unjust, and nuanced stances are socially unrewarded.
- Some urge companies to offer explicit off-ramps for Palantir engineers; others distrust applicants who stayed despite knowing the mission, preferring to signal ethics via projects.
