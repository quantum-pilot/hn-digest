# DHS keeps trying and failing to unmask anonymous ICE critics online

- Score: 156 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46768081) | Link: https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2026/01/instagram-ice-critic-wins-fight-to-stay-anonymous-as-dhs-backs-down/

### TL;DR
DHS repeatedly tried to use a customs subpoena statute to force Meta to reveal the identities behind Instagram/Facebook “community watch” accounts that track ICE operations and post agents’ faces, plates, and locations. An ACLU-backed plaintiff showed these accounts mostly shared rights info and alerts, not threats, and once sued to quash, DHS abruptly withdrew its demands—again, as in prior ICE-watch cases. This is a tactical win for online anonymity, but hinges on Meta notifications and access to lawyers, leaving a gray zone for less-resourced critics. HN commenters focus on free-speech risks, apparent growing impunity for ICE, and the inevitability of large-scale surveillance profiling.
