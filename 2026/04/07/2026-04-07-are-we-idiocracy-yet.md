# Are We Idiocracy Yet?

- Score: 590 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47672818) | Link: https://idiocracy.wtf/

## TL;DR
An interactive satire builds an “Idiocracy proximity index,” lining up scenes from Mike Judge’s film with present-day news: reality-TV-style politics, corporate capture of essentials, pain-as-entertainment, garbage crises, AI-driven healthcare misfires, history distortion, and IQ/education decline. The joke is that 2505 looks uncomfortably like 2024. HN discussion centers on how often people miss the satire and identify with the targets, the film’s awkward eugenics framing versus cultural explanations, and why the movie now plays more as tragedy than comedy.

## Comment pulse
- Idiocracy as misunderstood satire → many viewers identify with the “idiots” and feel insulted; similar misreadings cited for Starship Troopers and Onion pieces.  
- Eugenics vs education → critics dislike Idiocracy’s “breeding makes us dumber” premise, prefer stories like Don’t Look Up — counterpoint: movie targets misinformation, not genetics.  
- From comedy to tragedy → late viewers find it too bleak to enjoy; others say permissive culture lets people indulge childish desires without guardrails.
