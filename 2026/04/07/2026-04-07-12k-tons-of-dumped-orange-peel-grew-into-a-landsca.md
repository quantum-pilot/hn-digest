# 12k Tons of Dumped Orange Peel Grew into a Landscape Nobody Expected (2017)

- Score: 259 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47677142) | Link: https://www.sciencealert.com/how-12-000-tonnes-of-dumped-orange-peel-produced-something-nobody-imagined

### TL;DR
In the 1990s, ecologists and a Costa Rican juice company dumped 12,000 tons of orange peels onto 3 hectares of severely degraded pasture bordering a national park. Within months, the peels decomposed into rich soil; 16 years later, the site had become dense forest with 176% more above‑ground biomass, richer soils, and higher tree diversity than an untreated control. The project was halted early by a competitor’s lawsuit, yet still demonstrated how targeted use of organic waste can cheaply restore ecosystems and sequester carbon.
