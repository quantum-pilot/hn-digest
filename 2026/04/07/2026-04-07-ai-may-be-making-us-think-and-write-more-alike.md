# AI may be making us think and write more alike

- Score: 210 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47673541) | Link: https://dornsife.usc.edu/news/stories/ai-may-be-making-us-think-and-write-more-alike/

- TL;DR  
USC researchers argue that widespread LLM use is standardizing language and reasoning, shrinking cognitive diversity and nudging people toward WEIRD, linear “chain-of-thought” styles. Individuals may generate more polished, detailed ideas with AI, but groups using LLMs converge on fewer, less-creative solutions as they accept “good enough” suggestions. They call for training on more globally diverse texts and reasoning patterns, plus more mindful use. HN commenters add fears of secretive “dark forest” communities and AI-flattened workplace communication.

- Comment pulse  
  - Knowledge will go dark → People hoard tricks to avoid LLM training, retreat into closed human-only groups — counterpoint: secrets leak; many still share publicly.  
  - AI as median mind → LLMs feel above-average for novices but shallow for experts, encouraging “good enough” solutions and eroding value of weird, bad attempts.  
  - Behavioral reshaping at work → Some managers outsource emails and reviews to LLMs, creating flattering but inaccurate feedback and pressure to adopt generic, LLM-like phrasing.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Homogenization risk is real but gradual; social norms can push back, especially once “LLM voice” becomes uncool or untrusted.  
  - Impact: Education, management, and policymaking may over-index on AI-generated prose, sidelining unconventional thinkers who don’t match the emerging standardized register.  
  - Watch next: Long-term measures of language change in heavy LLM users, plus benchmarks rewarding diverse reasoning styles, not single solution.
