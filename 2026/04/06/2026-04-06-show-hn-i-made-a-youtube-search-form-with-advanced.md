# Show HN: I made a YouTube search form with advanced filters

- Score: 302 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47655392) | Link: https://playlists.at/youtube/search/

- TL;DR  
  Playlists.at wraps YouTube search with a cleaner UI and advanced filters, trying to restore power‑user control over results and playlists. HN discussion focuses less on the tool itself and more on how bad YouTube search and UX have become: users cannot reliably find watched videos or niche queries, are pushed toward algorithmic recommendations and Shorts, and increasingly rely on extensions or alternative front‑ends to fix things. Commenters request finer‑grained duration and channel filters, but note YouTube’s API exposes only coarse enums, forcing workarounds like multiple queries plus client‑side pruning.

- Comment pulse  
  - YouTube search and history feel broken → exact titles or known watched videos often don’t appear, pushing people toward algorithmic recommendation feeds instead.  
  - Users patch YouTube with uBlock, SponsorBlock, FreeTube, Search Fixer, etc., to remove ads, Shorts and irrelevant results — counterpoint: some say search still “generally works”.  
  - Feature wishes: arbitrary length ranges and channel‑specific filters → commenters explain YouTube encodes length as fixed enums, so wrappers must combine queries and post‑filter results.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Third‑party search UIs highlight latent demand for relevance and control, not just passive, engagement‑optimized video feeds.  
  - Impact: If such tools gain traction, power users may watch more intentionally while YouTube loses some influence over viewing patterns.  
  - Watch next: Track API changes, rate‑limits and ToS enforcement that could hinder independent front‑ends, plus open, decentralized video indexing efforts.
