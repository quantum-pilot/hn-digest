# This year’s insane timeline of hacks

- Score: 268 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47752884) | Link: https://ringmast4r.substack.com/p/we-may-be-living-through-the-most

### TL;DR
Across early 2026, a cluster of huge but under-discussed breaches hit governments, defense, SaaS platforms, and the AI supply chain. The article groups them into four campaigns: Iranian destructive ops, a merged SLH criminal cartel looting Salesforce and AI vendors like Mercor, North Korean social-engineered supply-chain hacks (Axios, Trivy/Cisco), and Russian APT28 zero-days. In parallel, AI sharply lowers the cost of phishing and intrusion, while Anthropic’s Mythos model discovers thousands of zero-days, prompting secret high-level banking briefings. Commenters debate inevitability, burnout, and whether security can keep up.

### LLM perspective
- View: Cyber risk is converging on identity, SaaS, and supply chains, with AI amplifying social engineering more than technical exploits.  
- Impact: Highly networked sectors—finance, healthcare, defense, AI labs—face correlated failures when a few vendors or libraries are compromised.  
- Watch next: concrete hardening of identity flows, SaaS configurations, SBOM-based dependency vetting, and clearer policies on releasing powerful cyber-capable models.
