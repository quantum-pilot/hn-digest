# The economics of software teams: Why most engineering orgs are flying blind

- Score: 383 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47748064) | Link: https://www.viktorcessan.com/the-economics-of-software-teams/

## TL;DR
Article quantifies economics of an 8‑engineer team (~€87k/month) and argues break-even isn’t enough: you need 3–5× return to cover failures and long‑tail maintenance. It shows how platform and product teams could tie work to churn, activation and time‑saved, and critiques activity/sentiment metrics decoupled from money. Cheap capital from 2011–2022 trained leaders to ignore this. With LLMs slashing build costs and eroding “big codebase” moats, orgs must instrument financial value or risk bloated, unjustifiable engineering orgs.
