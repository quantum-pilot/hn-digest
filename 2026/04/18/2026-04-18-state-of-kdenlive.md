# State of Kdenlive

- Score: 324 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47815118) | Link: https://kdenlive.org/news/2026/state-2026/

## TL;DR
- Kdenlive’s 2026 report highlights a stability‑first year: AI‑driven background/object removal, 3× faster audio waveforms, revamped mixer/markers/titler, a new docking system, welcome screen, and better monitors. Next up: monitor mirroring, animated transition previews, multi‑clip speed changes, timeline ergonomics, 10/12‑bit color, OpenFX, a dopesheet with per‑parameter keyframing, and a Windows Store build. Behind 11.5M+ downloads stand just two core devs and ~€9k/year in donations; HN feedback ranges from crash/UX complaints and 2× playback/titling gaps to strong praise as a Resolve alternative.

## Comment pulse
- UX and crashes → Some quit Kdenlive as unstable and convoluted vs commercial editors—counterpoint: others report recent releases stable and capable for everyday 4K work.  
- Sweet‑spot editor → Many praise Kdenlive as more capable than iMovie yet less intimidating than DaVinci Resolve, integrating nicely with OBS and Audacity.  
- AI‑assisted fixes → One user used Claude to find slow paths hurting large projects, but fears AI PR backlash; others propose draft PRs or bug‑plus‑patches.
