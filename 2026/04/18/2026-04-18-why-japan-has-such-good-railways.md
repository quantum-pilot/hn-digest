# Why Japan has such good railways

- Score: 282 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47815395) | Link: https://worksinprogress.co/issue/why-japan-has-such-good-railways/

### TL;DR
Japan’s world-class railways are explained by institutions and policy, not “rail-loving” culture. Since early 20th century, private rail companies have combined trains with housing, retail, leisure, and real estate, capturing land value around stations. Liberal zoning and land readjustment let them continually densify station areas and fund new lines. Cars must largely pay their own way via tolled roads and privatized parking, keeping rail competitive. Privatizing Japanese National Railways into vertically integrated JR firms, with light but targeted regulation and capital subsidies, completed a highly productive, expandable system.

### LLM perspective
- View: The core exportable idea is aligning rail profits with urban land value, not copying Shinkansen technology.  
- Impact: Cities that liberalize zoning and reform parking/road finance can make dense rail corridors fiscally and politically viable.
