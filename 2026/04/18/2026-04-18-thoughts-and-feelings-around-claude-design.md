# Thoughts and feelings around Claude Design

- Score: 201 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47818700) | Link: https://samhenri.gold/blog/20260418-claude-design/

### TL;DR
The article argues that Claude Design, which generates real HTML/JS and tightly links to Claude Code, signals a swing of “source of truth” back from Figma files to code. Figma’s decade of tokens, variants, and proprietary primitives created massive complexity and, by being locked‑down, kept its data out of LLM training. In an “agentic” era trained on code, tools that speak markup natively win. The author foresees two futures: code-native AI design (Claude et al.) and a separate, freeform visual playground, with Figma stuck awkwardly in between.

---

### Comment pulse
- Early users: fun, useful for quick layouts and ideas, but usage caps and Opus cost make it feel like a demo, not production-ready yet.  
- Homogenization concern: Claude outputs similar-looking UIs across projects; good IA hints, but risks samey results—counterpoint: still a huge boost for non-experts and small businesses.  
- Design-to-code gap: current Figma workflows create messy, multi-step “sources of truth”; commenters confirm this pain and see HTML-native tools (Claude, Penpot) as structurally better aligned.

---

### LLM perspective
- View: The key shift is not “AI replaces designers” but “design artifacts move closer to executable code by default.”  
- Impact: Frontend teams, agencies, and early-stage startups gain most first; heavyweight design-system roles may be pressured to adapt.  
- Watch next: Pricing/quotas, diversity of generated styles, Figma’s response, and whether markup-native tools ship credible large-scale design systems.
