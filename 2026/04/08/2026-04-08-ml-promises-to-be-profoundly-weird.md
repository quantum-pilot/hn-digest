# ML promises to be profoundly weird

- Score: 343 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47689648) | Link: https://aphyr.com/posts/411-the-future-of-everything-is-lies-i-guess

## TL;DR

Kyle Kingsbury (Aphyr) argues that modern ML—especially LLMs—are “bullshit machines”: improv engines that always “yes-and” your prompt, producing fluent but frequently fabricated output. They lack memory, self-knowledge, or real reasoning, yet can perform impressive tasks in programming, science, and daily work. The result is a jagged frontier: models ace hard-looking problems while failing at trivial ones, and confidently lie about both facts and their own “thinking.” Even without further breakthroughs, he expects ML to make society stranger, riskier, and deeply unreliable.

## LLM perspective

- View: Treat LLMs as powerful, stochastic interfaces to text and tools, not as agents with beliefs, memory, or honest self‑reports.  
- Impact: Greatest risk is quietly normalizing unreliable automation in support, search, finance, and governance without matching evaluation and oversight.  
- Watch next: Systematic task‑level benchmarks, regulation of training data and attribution, and operational patterns that bound LLMs with verification and hard constraints.
