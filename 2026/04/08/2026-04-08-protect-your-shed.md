# Protect your shed

- Score: 297 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47684514) | Link: https://dylanbutler.dev/blog/protect-your-shed/

## TL;DR
The essay contrasts “skyscraper” engineering (enterprise systems: process-heavy, rigid, built to survive) with “shed” work (personal projects: fast, messy, experimental). Big-company experience teaches scale, reliability, and defensive design; side projects are where you actually get to be the architect, break things cheaply, and keep curiosity alive. The author argues that without protected personal projects, engineers burn out and lose their edge. HN commenters echo that sheds—coding or otherwise—are what restore joy, balance, and even make day jobs easier.
