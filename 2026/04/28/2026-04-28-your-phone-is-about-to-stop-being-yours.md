# Your phone is about to stop being yours

- Score: 895 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47935853) | Link: https://keepandroidopen.org/en/

### TL;DR

Google’s 2026 Android “developer verification” plan will require almost all app developers—Play Store or sideloaded—to register with Google, pay a fee, submit government ID, disclose signing keys, and list app IDs. Unregistered apps will be silently blocked on certified devices, with only a buried, 9‑step “advanced” sideloading flow plus 24‑hour delay as an escape hatch. Critics say this centralizes global app distribution, threatens F‑Droid and indie/open‑source apps, enables censorship, and retroactively turns Android phones into locked appliances. HN debates whether this actually makes iOS more attractive, how much typical users value openness, and whether the campaign overstates the impact of the advanced flow.
