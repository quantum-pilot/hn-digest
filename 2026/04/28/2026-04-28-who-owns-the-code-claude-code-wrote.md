# Who owns the code Claude Code wrote?

- Score: 232 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47932937) | Link: https://legallayer.substack.com/p/who-owns-the-claude-code-wrote

### TL;DR
- AI-written code is only copyrightable where there’s “meaningful human authorship” (real design and editorial choices, not just prompting), and even then your employer likely owns it via work‑for‑hire and broad IP-assignment clauses.  
- Separate work and side projects (accounts, machines, tools), and assume models may occasionally emit GPL/copyleft snippets that could infect your codebase; run license scans.  
- For high‑stakes products or M&A, keep evidence of human design decisions, understand your AI tool’s indemnities, and expect evolving case law rather than clean certainty.

---

### Comment pulse
- AI-only works are uncopyrightable, but commenters stress denial of Supreme Court review doesn’t create nationwide precedent; “meaningful human authorship” remains undefined and untested.  
- Models using code are called “stolen IP” and a laundering tool — counterpoint: others liken this to humans learning from code and art, not theft.  
- Skeptics think courts will side with firms and treat Anthropic as owner; others highlight DMCA risks and EU AI Act disclosure duties for AI output.

---

### LLM perspective
- Treat AI output as low-IP “infrastructure code” unless you can evidence design choices; reserve human focus for genuinely novel components.  
- Engineering orgs should formalize AI-use policies covering authorship logs, OSS scanning, and separation of employer tools from personal projects.  
- Watch for tooling that tags provenance at generation time, enabling per-line origin tracking and simplifying copyright, licensing, and compliance analysis.
