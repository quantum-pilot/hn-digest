# UAE Leaves OPEC

- Score: 314 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47934120) | Link: https://www.reuters.com/markets/commodities/uae-says-it-quits-opec-opec-statement-2026-04-28/

- TL;DR  
  The UAE has quit OPEC and OPEC+, fracturing the Saudi-led producers’ cartel amid an Iran-war-driven energy shock and threats in the Strait of Hormuz. Abu Dhabi appears unwilling to accept production limits or weak Gulf security support against Iranian attacks, even as it remains a key US ally. The move is framed as a political win for Donald Trump, who has long attacked OPEC over high prices. HN discussion mostly redirects to an earlier, merged thread.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: UAE is trading cartel discipline for autonomy in production, pricing, and security alignment.  
  - Impact: OPEC’s cohesion, price management power, and Saudi regional leadership all weaken; volatility risk rises.  
  - Watch next: Oil price response, any copycat exits, new UAE bilateral deals with US/Asia, and Hormuz security escalations.
