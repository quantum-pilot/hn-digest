# Sometimes powerful people just do dumb shit

- Score: 257 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47760750) | Link: https://www.joanwestenberg.com/sometimes-powerful-people-just-do-dumb-shit/

- TL;DR  
  The essay attacks the “4D chess” mindset: the belief that powerful people’s seemingly bad decisions must hide a brilliant master plan. Using Napoleon’s Russia campaign, Musk’s Twitter/X purchase, and OpenAI’s extravagant TBPN podcast acquisition, the author argues these often reflect ego, luck-misattribution, and absent constraints, not genius. Cognitive biases (belief in a just world, pattern-finding, and flattery of seeing “hidden plans”) make followers rationalize anything, eroding accountability and preventing us from learning from obvious elite mistakes.
