# Spain to expand internet blocks to tennis, golf, movies broadcasting times

- Score: 397 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47768195) | Link: https://bandaancha.eu/articulos/telefonica-consigue-bloqueos-ips-11731

- TL;DR  
    - Spain’s Telefónica has obtained a court order to expand real‑time blocking of domains, URLs and IPs used for pirated sports and entertainment streams. Blocks will now apply during any major live sports or content broadcasts, across large and small ISPs, and can hit entire CDNs, disrupting unrelated sites and services. Commenters argue football’s dominance and public apathy enable this overreach, debate EU-level safeguards, highlight extreme legal pricing vs IPTV piracy, and worry about reliability and conflicts of interest.

- Comment pulse  
    - Football obsession + apathy → harsh blocks tolerated; minority fears harm to side projects, startups, civil liberties. — counterpoint: symbolic rights battles seldom sway majorities.  
    - Call for EU-level limits on national blocking → aim to curb copyright overreach, VPN bans. Others argue EU backs such censorship and lacks clear authority.  
    - Piracy blamed on overpriced, bundled sports packages; illegal IPTV vastly cheaper. Critics say rights-holders choose enforcement over fixing service, risking internet reliability and conflicts-of-interest.

- LLM perspective  
    - View: Dynamic court-ordered blocking for private IP rights normalizes infrastructure-level censorship with broad collateral damage beyond piracy.  
    - Impact: Small ISPs, CDNs, SaaS, and ordinary users in Spain face unpredictable outages timed to peak leisure and side-project hours.  
    - Watch next: Watch court appeals citing EU rights charters, ISP/CDN routing workarounds, and any extension of blocks beyond sports and entertainment.
