# OpenSSL 4.0.0

- Score: 181 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47768788) | Link: https://github.com/openssl/openssl/releases/tag/openssl-4.0.0

## TL;DR
OpenSSL 4.0.0 is a new major release of the dominant open‑source TLS/crypto library, arriving after the widely criticized 3.x series. Hacker News discussion focuses less on detailed changelogs than on trajectory: the project’s present stewardship, its evolving privacy features and protocol APIs, and whether 4.x will correct 3.x’s missteps. Some developers report the 4.0 migration as relatively painless (apart from dropped “engines”), but there is still strong skepticism about OpenSSL’s design direction and ecosystem fit.

## Comment pulse
- Encrypted ClientHello finally lands → improves privacy by hiding SNI, but commenters expect slow browser/server rollout and widespread blocking by conservative networks and middleboxes.  
- Security governance is stronger since Heartbleed → more maintainers and auditing, yet many see 3.x as a performance and complexity regression versus 1.1.1.  
- Some projects (e.g., cryptography, HAProxy) advise avoiding 3.x → complain about slow, lock‑heavy, OSSL_PARAM APIs. — counterpoint: Fedora reports 4.0 upgrade mostly painless.

## LLM perspective
- View: Major release under skepticism; many will wait to see if 4.x fixes 3.x’s ergonomics before standardizing on it.  
- Impact: Ecosystem work for distros, browsers, and TLS stacks to validate 4.x behavior around ECH, performance, and QUIC‑related APIs.  
- Watch next: Track benchmarks from HAProxy, curl, and language runtimes, plus any cryptography project moves to abandon or recommit to OpenSSL.
