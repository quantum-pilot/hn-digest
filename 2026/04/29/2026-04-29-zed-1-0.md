# Zed 1.0

- Score: 1454 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47949027) | Link: https://zed.dev/blog/zed-1-0

- TL;DR  
  Zed 1.0 marks the “real” launch of a GPU‑driven, Rust-based editor that avoids Electron, aiming for game-like responsiveness and deep control over the stack. It’s positioned as AI-native: multiple parallel agents, keystroke-level edit predictions, and an Agent Client Protocol to plug in Claude Agent, Codex, Cursor, etc. Upcoming DeltaDB (CRDT-based) powers multi-human/agent collaboration. HN discussion praises performance and remote workflows, but raises serious concerns over telemetry, broad data rights, arbitration, and other business-terms tradeoffs.

- Comment pulse  
  License and telemetry worry users → broad rights over “Customer Data” plus “telemetry” that may include AI training and mandatory arbitration feel risky for proprietary code.  
  Performance and UX win fans → many report replacing VSCode, JetBrains, and Sublime for everyday work, citing speed, stability with huge files, and strong Vim mode.  
  Remote development traction → people use Zed heavily over SSH and with services like exe.dev, seeking a lighter, more reliable alternative to VSCode Remote-SSH.

- LLM perspective  
  View: Zed exemplifies “AI-native, GPU-native” tooling—handcrafted stack to tightly integrate agents and collaboration.  
  Impact: Strong pull for teams needing fast editors plus compliant, controllable AI workflows; ToS may push privacy-sensitive orgs away.  
  Watch next: Clear telemetry controls, self-hosted/airgapped modes, and DeltaDB-based multi-agent sessions will determine enterprise and OSS adoption.
