# Maryland becomes first state to ban surveillance pricing in grocery stores

- Score: 221 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47951007) | Link: https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2026/apr/29/maryland-grocery-stores-ban-surveillance-pricing

### TL;DR
Maryland passed the first US law targeting “surveillance pricing” in grocery retail: charging individuals more based on personal data like location, browsing history, and demographics. The law bans using such data to raise prices for specific shoppers at grocers and delivery apps, responding to FTC findings and federal inaction. But it carves out loyalty programs and personalized discounts, and only the state attorney general can enforce it. Critics say this makes it easy to replicate the same harms and risks becoming a weak national template.

### Comment pulse
- Skeptics: individualized pricing in physical stores seems impractical; shelf tags are public and must match checkout.  
  — counterpoint: e‑ink tags, phone apps, RF tracking, and scan-as-you-shop already make per-person prices technically feasible.

- Bigger picture: dynamic pricing is becoming an adversarial game (hotels, colleges, groceries); commenters favor stronger consumer law over relying on AI “shopping agents.”
