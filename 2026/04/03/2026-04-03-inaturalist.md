# iNaturalist

- Score: 310 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47629433) | Link: https://www.inaturalist.org/

### TL;DR
iNaturalist is a citizen‑science platform and mobile app where anyone can photograph organisms, get identifications from AI plus experts, and automatically contribute observations to biodiversity databases like GBIF. The HN discussion highlights side projects and visualizations built on its data, and the kid‑friendly Seek app. Commenters also flag privacy risks from precise home‑location clustering and suggest safer defaults, while others credit the app with deepening their knowledge of local species during the pandemic.

### Comment pulse
- API and ecosystem are developer-friendly → unauthenticated reads, permissive CORS, and bulk exports power visualizations, games, custom maps, bots, and research workflows.  
- Citizen-science observations have real impact → research-grade records flow into national databases, prompting invasive-species responses and enriching biodiversity baselines — counterpoint: traditional recorders doubt data quality.  
- Location precision worries users → backyard clusters can reveal residences; commenters want clearer onboarding, coarser defaults, and per-user randomization to protect non-technical participants.
