# Marc Andreessen is wrong about introspection

- Score: 366 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47627056) | Link: https://www.joanwestenberg.com/marc-andreessen-is-wrong-about-introspection/

### TL;DR
The article rebuts Marc Andreessen’s claim that introspection is a 20th‑century Freudian invention and a kind of modern pathology. It cites long traditions of self-examination from Socrates, the Stoics, Augustine, Mencius, and Shakespeare to show introspection is ancient and foundational to civilization. The author argues Andreessen’s “zero-introspection” stance conveniently clears away questions of meaning, leaving only “more/faster/bigger” as goals. Without introspection, we misjudge human flourishing—just as social platforms optimized metrics while degrading users’ inner lives. HN comments mostly see Andreessen as overconfident, lucky, and emblematic of money-worship.
