# Critics say EU risks ceding control of its tech laws under U.S. pressure

- Score: 212 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47625244) | Link: https://www.politico.eu/article/fatal-decision-eu-slammed-for-caving-to-us-pressure-on-digital-rules/

### TL;DR
- The European Commission plans an EU–US “dialogue” on enforcing the Digital Services Act and Digital Markets Act, amid strong US pressure to ease the impact on American tech giants. MEPs and German lawmakers say this risks giving Washington and Big Tech indirect influence over EU enforcement and turning digital regulation into a trade bargaining chip, undermining EU sovereignty. Hacker News discussion expands this into a broader critique of the EU’s opaque institutions and of decades of ineffective fines on US platforms.

### Comment pulse
- EU critics → Commission and Council seen as opaque, self-serving bureaucracy undermining Parliament; chat control, age verification, trade deals show contempt for citizens’ rights and farmers.  
- Enforcement fatigue → Years of EU fines haven’t changed Big Tech; some urge bans or fast-escalating penalties until operating in Europe stops being profitable.  
- Realpolitik view → Cross-border tech laws inevitably involve US–EU negotiation; excluding the US is unrealistic—counterpoint: critics fear this ‘dialogue’ trades away enforcement for tariff concessions.

### LLM perspective
- View: The clash is less about specific laws than about who speaks for European digital sovereignty: Commission, Parliament, or Washington.  
- Impact: Weakening DSA/DMA enforcement would benefit Meta, X, Apple, Google; costs fall on EU citizens, smaller platforms, and regulators’ credibility.  
- Watch next: Whether Parliament demands transparency conditions on any US ‘dialogue’, or instead pushes anti-coercion tools and rigid enforcement guidance.
