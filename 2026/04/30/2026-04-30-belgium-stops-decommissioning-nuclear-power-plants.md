# Belgium stops decommissioning nuclear power plants

- Score: 723 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47961319) | Link: https://dpa-international.com/general-news/urn:newsml:dpa.com:20090101:260430-930-14717/

### TL;DR
Belgium has reversed its nuclear phase‑out, stopping decommissioning and starting talks to nationalize ENGIE’s seven‑reactor fleet, including staff, assets, and decommissioning liabilities. This follows years of debate over a 2003 law mandating shutdown by 2025, slow renewable build‑out, and high dependence on imported gas. The government now frames nuclear as core to “safe, affordable, sustainable” energy and plans new plants. HN commenters zoom in on climate tradeoffs, nuclear economics, reactor age/safety, and the politics of waste storage.
