# How Mark Klein told the EFF about Room 641A [book excerpt]

- Score: 394 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47965060) | Link: https://thereader.mitpress.mit.edu/the-whistleblower-who-uncovered-the-nsas-big-brother-machine/

- TL;DR  
  - EFF director Cindy Cohn recounts how, in 2006, retired AT&T technician Mark Klein walked into EFF’s San Francisco office with schematics proving NSA mass domestic internet surveillance. Klein described Room 641A and a fiber splitter duplicating all backbone traffic into an NSA-controlled room, effectively erasing the post‑9/11 “wall” between foreign intelligence and domestic data. His documents enabled the Hepting v. AT&T lawsuit and triggered tense DOJ claims the materials might be classified, leaving EFF lawyers fearing prosecution as they challenged unconstitutional spying.

- Comment pulse  
  - Surveillance “wall” wasn’t real → Commenters say NSA–FBI separation was violated long before 9/11; NDAs, parallel construction, and ECHELON obscure domestic spying origins.  
  - Case outcome and now → Congress retroactively immunized telecoms; commenters think programs expanded since, citing secret FISA reauthorizations and Ron Wyden’s “worse than Snowden” warnings.  
  - Tech vs democracy anxiety → Some argue pervasive surveillance makes liberal governance unsustainable; others say it’s sustainable but demands continuous cultural resistance and legal reform.
