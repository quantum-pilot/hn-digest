# Neanderthals survived on a knife's edge for 350k years

- Score: 228 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47595572) | Link: https://www.science.org/content/article/neanderthals-survived-knife-s-edge-350-000-years

### TL;DR
Two new ancient-DNA studies show Neanderthals lived in tiny, scattered, inbred groups—likely only a few thousand breeding individuals across Eurasia. They endured a massive population crash around 75,000 years ago during an ice age, then re-expanded from refuges in southwestern Europe, but lost almost all prior mitochondrial diversity. Their effective population size stayed chronically low, making them vulnerable when rapid climate swings and larger, incoming Homo sapiens groups arrived ~45,000 years ago. Comparing these “parallel experiments” may clarify why our lineage survived while theirs vanished.

### LLM perspective
- View: Long-term survival with tiny, inbred populations shows robustness under stable conditions but brittleness under rapid environmental stress.
- Impact: Refines models of human evolution, migration, and extinction risk for small, fragmented populations, past and present.
- Watch next: Higher-resolution genomes across time/space to map exact bottlenecks, refugia, and interactions with early Homo sapiens and Denisovans.
