# The OpenAI graveyard: All the deals and products that haven't happened

- Score: 215 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47602565) | Link: https://www.forbes.com/sites/phoebeliu/2026/03/31/openai-graveyard-deals-and-products-havent-happened-openai/

- TL;DR  
  Forbes catalogs a “graveyard” of OpenAI products and deals—most prominently the shutdown of Sora and its canceled $1B Disney partnership—arguing the company’s whiplash pivots reveal weak focus and financial discipline as it chases an IPO and fends off Anthropic. The piece notes abandoned or delayed hardware, social, and video projects as OpenAI refocuses on enterprise and coding tools. HN commenters are split between seeing healthy startup iteration and viewing it as reckless, trust-eroding experimentation from a company still searching for a truly durable business model.

- Comment pulse  
  - Pivoting is normal startup behavior → OpenAI stress-tests ideas, keeps winners like ChatGPT and kills losers fast — counterpoint: Sora’s billion‑dollar burn shows weak discipline.  
  - Critics: at OpenAI’s scale, constant product whiplash erodes trust, echoing Google’s graveyard; hype about “solving” work reduces sympathy when experiments implode.  
  - Some blame brain drain and lack of a cash‑cow business; others note Sora couldn’t just be repriced, making investors confront true economics.
