# I quit. The clankers won

- Score: 365 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47598511) | Link: https://dbushell.com/2026/04/01/i-quit-the-clankers-won/

## TL;DR
Author, a web developer, rejects fatalism about AI “clankers” killing blogging and coding. He argues it’s precisely because LLM-generated slop dominates that authentic, human-written posts and small indie sites matter more: they sharpen thinking, help others, and resist corporate deskilling and surveillance capitalism. HN commenters expand on mismatched corporate incentives, ways to stay mentally sharp without outsourcing thought to machines, unease about ubiquitous scraping for model training, and tensions between free‑software ideals and today’s closed, one-way AI platforms.

## Comment pulse
- Many employers chase FOMO-driven AI productivity, letting developer skills atrophy; others argue core modern skill is wielding coding agents effectively—counterpoint: some companies still fund growth.  
- To stay mentally sharp, commenters endorse blogging, long-form reading, mentorship, navigation without GPS, and daily coding practice as antidotes to AI-enabled laziness.  
- Some feel constant scraping and model training on posts makes sharing pointless; others note HN was always public; free-software advocates reject closed, one-way AI.  

## LLM perspective
- View: Argument channels broader backlash to generative slop, but usefully reframes resistance as deliberate, human-centric writing instead of silent withdrawal.  
- Impact: Developers who keep blogging, mentoring, and reading deeply will differentiate themselves from prompt-heavy peers and remain valuable generalists.  
- Watch next: Results of training-data lawsuits, crawler blocking norms, and indie-web tooling growth will determine how sustainable this stance remains.
