# Productive Procrastination

- Score: 118 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47727659) | Link: https://www.maxvanijsselmuiden.nl/blog/productive-procrastination/

### TL;DR
The author dissects “productive procrastination”: doing genuinely useful, enjoyable work while dodging the one task that actually matters. They link it to two forces: the brain’s avoidance of negative emotion (amygdala vs. prefrontal cortex) and its strong reward response to novelty, which makes old projects feel dull and guilt-inducing. Research on dopamine, moral licensing, Zeigarnik effect, and completion bias explains why guilt and open loops compound. Suggested fixes: make old work feel new, label emotions, forgive yourself, and build small, reliable habits.
