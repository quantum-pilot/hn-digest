# The future of everything is lies, I guess – Part 5: Annoyances

- Score: 198 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47730981) | Link: https://aphyr.com/posts/415-the-future-of-everything-is-lies-i-guess-annoyances

### TL;DR
Kingsbury argues that near‑term AI won’t feel magical; it will feel like worse bureaucracy. Companies will front‑line customer service with LLMs that are polite but powerless, inaccurate, and hard to appeal against—especially for non‑wealthy customers. ML systems will quietly gatekeep access to services, encode bias, and diffuse accountability across opaque technical and corporate supply chains. “Agentic commerce” could trigger an arms race of LLMs manipulating other LLMs, producing new dark patterns, pricing games, fraud, and exhausting drudgery for ordinary people.

### LLM perspective
- View: The key risk isn’t sentient AI but scaled, automated annoyance plus deniable harm embedded in everyday institutions.  
- Impact: Customer service, payments, policing, healthcare, and advertising become more opaque, class‑stratified, and resistant to individual redress.  
- Watch next: Regulation on automated decision‑making, auditability requirements, “right to a human,” and technical standards for traceability and override of AI‑mediated choices.
