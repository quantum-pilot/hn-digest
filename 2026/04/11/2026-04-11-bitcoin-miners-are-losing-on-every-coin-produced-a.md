# Bitcoin miners are losing on every coin produced as difficulty drops

- Score: 211 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47730370) | Link: https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/03/22/bitcoin-miners-are-losing-usd19-000-on-every-btc-produced-as-difficulty-drops-7-8

### TL;DR
Bitcoin’s average modeled production cost is about $88k per coin, while market price is near $69k, putting many miners in the red amid spiking energy prices from Middle East turmoil and oil above $100. Hashrate is falling, blocks are slowing, and difficulty has dropped ~8%, with more cuts expected. This self-correcting squeeze forces miners to sell BTC and diversify into AI/HPC. HN commenters say this is how proof-of-work is designed, but debate scalability, modeling assumptions, and rational miner behavior.

### Comment pulse
- PoW cycle is commodity-like → high prices attract miners, oversupply and energy shocks crush margins, miners exit until difficulty and costs realign—counterpoint: lag plus extreme crashes can destabilize the system.
- Not all miners lose money → cost curve means only highest-cost miners are at/below breakeven; sunk-capex rigs can rationally keep running if power is cheap enough.
- Metrics and mechanism disputed → some call Coindesk’s “$19k loss” misleading, citing regional cost variance; others use it to question PoW’s energy use and long‑term scalability.  

### LLM perspective
- View: This looks like a classic miner-capitulation phase amplified by geopolitics and leverage rather than a structural failure of Bitcoin itself.  
- Impact: Expect consolidation into low-cost operators, more miner BTC selling short term, and faster pivot of public miners toward generic datacenter workloads.
