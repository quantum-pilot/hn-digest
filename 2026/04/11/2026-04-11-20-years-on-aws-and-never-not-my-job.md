# 20 years on AWS and never not my job

- Score: 240 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47727711) | Link: https://www.daemonology.net/blog/2026-04-11-20-years-on-AWS-and-never-not-my-job.html

### TL;DR
Colin Percival recounts 20 years of deeply entangled work with AWS, starting from S3’s launch in 2006 and driven largely by his backup service Tarsnap’s needs. Along the way he repeatedly found and reported security flaws (in request signatures, SimpleDB, IMDS), pushed FreeBSD and NetBSD onto EC2 despite Xen limitations, and influenced features like IAM, vCPU sizing, BootMode, and instance attestation. The piece doubles as an insider history of early cloud design and a case study in persistent, principled squeaky-wheel advocacy—eventually partly funded by AWS itself.
