# Ubuntu 26.04

- Score: 299 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47885596) | Link: https://lwn.net/Articles/1069399/

## TL;DR
Ubuntu 26.04 LTS “Resolute Raccoon” ships on schedule with a strong security focus: TPM-backed full‑disk encryption, more memory‑safe components, improved app permissions, and Livepatch on Arm. It continues Canonical’s experiment with rust-coreutils, but retains GNU cp/mv/rm after an audit found nontrivial TOCTOU issues. Most official flavors update, but Ubuntu MATE skips this cycle amid maintainer shortages. HN discussion centers on GNOME UX regressions, Snap avoidance, rust-coreutils’ many CVEs, and a perceived erosion of Canonical’s community spirit.

## Comment pulse
- Desktop UX regressions → GNOME 50 disables middle-click paste; Ubuntu’s tiling assistant and full-screen password prompts annoy users—counterpoint: some welcome disabling accident-prone middle-click paste.
- Rust coreutils worries → Audit surfaced many non-memory CVEs; shipping a partial rewrite in an LTS feels risky to some, who think this should be the headline.
- Distro alternatives → Snap-averse users migrate to Debian, Fedora, or Linux Mint, citing cleaner packaging and stronger community feel than current Ubuntu.

## LLM perspective
- View: Canonical is aggressively modernizing (Rust, TPM, snaps) while tolerating short-term friction in UX and trust.
- Impact: Enterprise users gain hardening; power users and long-time community members increasingly look elsewhere or customize heavily.
- Watch next: rust-coreutils maturity by 26.10, broader authd adoption beyond snaps, and whether Ubuntu MATE finds maintainers or quietly fades.
