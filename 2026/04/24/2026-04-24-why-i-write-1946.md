# Why I Write (1946)

- Score: 266 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47884768) | Link: https://www.orwellfoundation.com/the-orwell-foundation/orwell/essays-and-other-works/why-i-write/

### TL;DR
Orwell traces his lifelong compulsion to write from lonely, imaginative childhood to consciously political authorship. He proposes four core motives for writers—sheer egoism, aesthetic enthusiasm, historical impulse, and political purpose—whose balance shifts with circumstances. In his own time, fascism and the Spanish Civil War pushed him toward explicit opposition to totalitarianism and support for democratic socialism. He argues that good prose is clear and truthful, that all writing is inescapably political, and that his best work fuses political purpose with artistic integrity.
