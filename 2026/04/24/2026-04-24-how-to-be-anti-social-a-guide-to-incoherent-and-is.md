# How to be anti-social – a guide to incoherent and isolating social experiences

- Score: 290 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47888372) | Link: https://nate.leaflet.pub/3mk4xkaxobc2p

## TL;DR
A short satirical checklist describes how to guarantee incoherent, isolating interactions: always assume bad intent, never question your assumptions, shun evidence, weaponize your in‑group, and refuse grace or understanding. The author later explains it was a quick rant about uncharitable behavior seen in family conflict and in people blaming Bluesky outages on “vibe coding.” Hacker News readers broaden it to discuss avoidant and anxious behavior, online flame‑war dynamics, and the importance of assuming good faith and offering grace.

## Comment pulse
- Many map the list onto avoidant or anxious behavior → self‑blame, fear of asking questions, and withdrawal define their social struggles — counterpoint: that’s asocial/avoidant, not antisocial.
- Others say it better fits online flame‑wars → bad‑faith assumptions, in‑group narrative building, and social-media habits that atrophy real‑world social skills.
- Some focus on solutions → practice assuming non‑malicious intent, zoom out to find shared ground, and value group grace over “winning” disagreements.
