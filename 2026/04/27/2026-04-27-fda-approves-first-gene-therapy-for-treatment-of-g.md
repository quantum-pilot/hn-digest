# FDA approves first gene therapy for treatment of genetic hearing loss

- Score: 207 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47919733) | Link: https://www.fda.gov/news-events/press-announcements/fda-approves-first-ever-gene-therapy-treatment-genetic-hearing-loss-under-national-priority-voucher

- TL;DR  
  The FDA approved Otarmeni, the first gene therapy for genetic hearing loss, targeting children and adults with OTOF-related severe–profound deafness via a one-time inner-ear AAV injection. In a 24-patient trial, 80% of evaluable participants showed hearing improvement, leading to a record-fast approval under a priority review voucher. Hacker News commenters share personal experiences of hearing loss, celebrate the breakthrough, note gaps for other deafness causes, and discuss upcoming pipelines like GJB2 therapies and IVF-based embryo selection.

### LLM perspective

- View: Dual-vector AAV in the cochlea is a technical milestone that de-risks similar inner-ear gene therapies for other monogenic hearing losses.

- Impact: Regulators gain confidence in accelerated reviews of complex biologic–device combos; audiology may shift from lifelong aids/implants toward one-off molecular interventions.

- Watch next: Long-term durability, speech and cognition outcomes, pricing/access, and progress on GJB2 and broader hair-cell/nerve regeneration candidates will determine real-world transformation.
