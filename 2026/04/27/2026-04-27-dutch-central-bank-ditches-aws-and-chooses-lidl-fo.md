# Dutch central bank ditches AWS and chooses Lidl for European Cloud

- Score: 301 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47922712) | Link: https://www.techzine.eu/news/infrastructure/140634/dutch-central-bank-chooses-lidl-for-european-cloud/

### TL;DR
- The Dutch central bank (DNB) plans to move from US hyperscalers like AWS to Stackit, the “sovereign” cloud run by Schwarz Digits, IT arm of Lidl’s owner.  
- Goal: reduce geopolitical and legal exposure to US providers (e.g., Cloud Act, prior Microsoft account incident) and model EU cloud sovereignty for the financial sector.  
- Stackit evolved from Lidl/Kaufland’s internal platform; it’s less mature than AWS/Azure but backed by multi‑billion‑euro investment and early big-name customers.

---

### Comment pulse
- Avoid proprietary cloud services → pure VMs + open source simplify exit, data locality, and sovereignty—counterpoint: you lose managed services and talent skews “cloud-first” now.  
- Managed cloud services are attractive → they replace multiple ops hires and shift blame/SLAs to vendors, but deepen dependency and make later migration politically painful.  
- Lidl/Stackit reaction → surprise at a supermarket group running a cloud; seen as OpenStack-ish, pricey vs Hetzner, with amateurish marketing but technically “solid foundation.”
