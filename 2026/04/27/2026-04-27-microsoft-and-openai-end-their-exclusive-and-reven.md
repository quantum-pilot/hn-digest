# Microsoft and OpenAI end their exclusive and revenue-sharing deal

- Score: 719 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47921248) | Link: https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-27/microsoft-to-stop-sharing-revenue-with-main-ai-partner-openai

### TL;DR
Microsoft and OpenAI have renegotiated their partnership: Azure is no longer exclusive, and Microsoft stops sharing revenue with OpenAI, but remains primary cloud provider and major shareholder. OpenAI can now spread training and inference across AWS, Google Cloud/TPUs, and specialized setups, easing severe compute constraints and improving leverage. Microsoft trades direct revenue share for license-free access to OpenAI models inside Azure, betting on cloud consumption. HN discussion centered on cloud winners, OpenAI’s independence, and increasingly thin model-level moats.
