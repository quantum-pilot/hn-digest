# US Supreme Court reviews police use of cell location data

- Score: 215 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47922940) | Link: https://www.nytimes.com/2026/04/27/us/politics/supreme-court-cell-data-geofence.html

## TL;DR
Supreme Court justices are examining whether police “geofence warrants” that compel tech firms to search all phones near a crime scene violate the Fourth Amendment. The case stems from a 2019 Virginia bank robbery where Google searched hundreds of millions of accounts to identify suspects. Arguments split ideological lines, focusing on whether detailed location histories are more like bank records (less protected) or a private “digital diary.” HN commenters highlight Google’s shift to on-device storage, dragnet risks, and the crumbling third‑party doctrine.
