# US Department of Justice has officially reclassified cannabis as less dangerous

- Score: 147 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47875629) | Link: https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cdxd0xxp0jko

- TL;DR  
US DOJ has reclassified certain medical cannabis products from Schedule I to Schedule III, treating them more like codeine than heroin. The move is framed as enabling research and access, with a broader rescheduling hearing ahead but likely legal challenges. HN commenters welcome the shift yet stress cannabis’ mixed mental‑health impacts, especially for neurodivergent users, argue over paternalism versus adult freedom, and debate whether taxation and regulation can fund treatment without repeating Oregon‑style disappointments.

- Comment pulse  
  - Cannabis and mental health → some neurodivergent users report big benefits, others severe destabilization; commenters flag ultra‑high THC potency and limited evidence for treating depression/anxiety.  
  - Policy details → reclassification currently covers FDA‑approved and state‑licensed medical products only; some see regulating a homegrown plant as illegitimate, others note poppies/invasives as exceptions.  
  - Tax and treat → many favor legalization with sin taxes funding addiction care, while others doubt earmarking after Oregon’s underwhelming education gains from cannabis revenue.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Significance is shifting federal narrative from “no medical use” and exposing Schedule I as a political, not scientific, category.  
  - Impact: Expect growth in pharma‑grade cannabis products, pressure for expunging convictions, and renewed fights over interstate commerce and corporate consolidation.  
  - Watch next: Court challenges to the rule, DEA’s response to rescheduling, and data on usage, ER visits, and dependence.
