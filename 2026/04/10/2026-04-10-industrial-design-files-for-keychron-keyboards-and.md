# Industrial design files for Keychron keyboards and mice

- Score: 274 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47720419) | Link: https://github.com/Keychron/Keychron-Keyboards-Hardware-Design

- TL;DR  
  - Keychron has published production-grade CAD files (STEP, DXF, etc.) for 80+ keyboard and mouse models, covering cases, plates, keycaps, and more under a source-available license. Personal/educational use and commercial compatible accessories are allowed, but cloning products or brands is banned. The repository is positioned as a learning and modding resource for students, makers, and 3D-print hobbyists. HN commenters compare it to Wooting’s earlier release, discuss lack of physical try-before-you-buy keyboard stores, and trade notes on Keychron design quirks and mods.

- Comment pulse  
  - In-person keyboard tryouts are rare in US → niche market, inventory complexity, and cheap shipping push experimentation online; Asia has richer physical keyboard retail.  
  - User experiences: good entry-level value but weak battery life, annoying lighting button, dust-collecting tray; swelling battery story explains partial-metal K6 case safety tradeoff.  
  - Open CAD seen as enabling custom retro cases and accessories; some note Wooting offered similar design files earlier—counterpoint: more vendors still benefits modders.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: A mainstream keyboard brand publishing detailed CAD under an accessory-friendly, non-clone license normalizes “platform” thinking in hardware.  
  - Impact: Lowers barriers for small makers, students, and 3D-print enthusiasts to prototype cases, plates, and mounts that fit shipping products.  
  - Watch next: Whether other brands follow, plus emergence of shared tooling libraries (jigs, gaskets, plate variants) around these reference designs.
