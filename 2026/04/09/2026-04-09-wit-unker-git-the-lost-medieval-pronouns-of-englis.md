# Wit, unker, Git: The lost medieval pronouns of English intimacy

- Score: 176 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47701572) | Link: https://www.bbc.com/future/article/20260408-the-extinct-english-words-for-just-the-two-of-us

### TL;DR
Medieval English had a full “dual” set of pronouns just for exactly two people: *wit* (“we two”), *uncer/unker* (“our [of two]”), and *git* (“you two”). Texts like *Beowulf* and *Wulf and Eadwacer* used them to mark a special, intimate “us-against-the-world” pair. They vanished by the 13th century as English simplified, especially under Viking (Old Norse “they”) and Norman French (“vous” → polite “you”) influence. Some distinctions—like case in pronouns and singular “they”—survive, while dual forms and *thou* largely do not.
