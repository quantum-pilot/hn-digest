# Netflix Prices Went Up Again – I Bought a DVD Player Instead

- Score: 175 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47709251) | Link: https://aywren.com/2026/04/09/netflix-prices-went-up-again-i-bought-a-dvd-player-instead/

### TL;DR
The blogger cancels Netflix after another price hike, refusing to pay nearly $20/month for ad-free access to a catalog that can lose shows at any time. Instead, they buy a compact DVD/Blu-ray player and build a cheap physical collection (especially anime and favorite series) that they truly own and can watch anytime, without ads or shifting licenses. Hacker News discussion branches into ripping discs to NAS, piracy-based setups, nostalgia for Netflix’s DVD era, and heavy use of libraries for media.

---

### Comment pulse
- Build your own library → Buy cheap DVDs/Blu-rays, rip to NAS, use Plex/Infuse; you control availability and quality. — counterpoint: DVD resolution and compressed box sets can look poor on 4K TVs.  
- Go fully DIY digital → Seedbox + Plex + radarr/sonarr recreates “all streaming” for ~€20/month, but setup is complex and some genres (e.g., reality TV) are poorly available.  
- Physical and public options persist → Libraries, DVD-by-mail outfits, and specialty stores still offer broad catalogs, often free or cheap, and avoid streaming’s content churn.
