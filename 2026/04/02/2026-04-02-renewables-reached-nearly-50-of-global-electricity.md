# Renewables reached nearly 50% of global electricity capacity last year

- Score: 238 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47615756) | Link: https://www.theregister.com/2026/04/01/renewables_generated_nearly_half_global_power/

## TL;DR
Renewables supplied 49.4% of global installed electricity capacity in 2025, driven by a record 692 GW of new capacity, three-quarters of it solar. Yet fossil projects rebounded—non‑renewable additions nearly doubled, with China adding 100 GW of mostly coal—and AI/datacenter demand is helping drive new gas and coal plants, especially in the US. IRENA warns that despite strong growth (5.15 TW installed), the world is not on track to hit the COP28 goal of 11+ TW by 2030.  
HN commenters highlight data showing solar and wind generation in 2025 already outpaced global demand growth, debate China’s coal strategy as backup for an increasingly renewable grid, and discuss how better forecasting software is enabling higher solar penetration.
