# The Super Nintendo Cartridges (2024)

- Score: 161 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47906161) | Link: https://fabiensanglard.net/snes_carts/

### TL;DR
SNES cartridges were more like custom expansion boards than simple ROMs. Beyond game data, they often contained copy‑protection CIC chips, battery‑backed SRAM for saves, and a zoo of “enhancement processors” (SA‑1, Super FX, CX4, DSP series, decompression and RTC chips). These offloaded 3D math, sprite manipulation, decompression, and AI, effectively upgrading the console per game. The article also shows how modern hackers repurpose SA‑1 and others to remove slowdown and even enable HD-style rendering in emulators.
