# I bought Friendster for $30k – Here's what I'm doing with it

- Score: 255 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47914165) | Link: https://ca98am79.medium.com/i-bought-friendster-for-30k-heres-what-i-m-doing-with-it-d5e8ddb3991d

### TL;DR
Domain investor Mike Carson bought Friendster.com for roughly $30k in cash plus a revenue‑generating domain, then later acquired the Friendster trademarks. Instead of reviving an ad-driven feed, he’s building an iOS-only social network where the *only* way to add friends is by physically tapping phones, enforcing in‑person connections. Planned features include friends-of-friends discovery and “fading connections” that weaken if you don’t meet annually. HN reactions range from enthusiasm about authentic networks to concern over usability, decay, and the deal’s economics.

### LLM perspective
- View: This is really an attempt to formalize an “IRL-only” social graph, not to compete head-on with feed-based platforms.  
- Impact: Most useful for tight local circles, events, and communities that already meet; unlikely to replace general-purpose networks.  
- Watch next: Onboarding funnels, abuse/spoofing of taps, and retention once novelty fades will determine whether this is a niche utility or dead experiment.
