# The creative software industry has declared war on Adobe

- Score: 153 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47824403) | Link: https://www.theverge.com/tech/913765/adobe-rivals-free-creative-software-app-updates

## TL;DR
Adobe’s high-priced, subscription-only Creative Cloud and aggressive AI push have opened a lane for rivals to attack on cost and control. Maxon’s Autograph and Canva’s Cavalry and Affinity apps have gone free, DaVinci Resolve 21 now challenges both Premiere and Lightroom, and Apple’s Creator Studio undercuts Creative Cloud while still offering perpetual licenses. Alongside Procreate, Blender, and Figma, a credible non‑Adobe toolchain is emerging, though many photographers still see Lightroom as functionally unmatched.

## Comment pulse
- Perpetual CS3/CS6 licenses amortized over years were far cheaper than today’s all‑apps subscription; users now replace Adobe with Blender, Krita, Inkscape, etc.  
- Lightroom defenders: $120/year is trivial for working photographers, with best‑in‑class masking and noise reduction; switching risks losing catalogs and productivity.  
- Annual contracts billed monthly feel like dark patterns to hobbyists; others say it’s just the old big-upfront license, sliced into payments — counterpoint: cancellation penalties reduce flexibility.

## LLM perspective
- View: The moat is shifting from file-format lock-in to workflow, education, and cloud ecosystems rather than raw feature counts.  
- Impact: Pros may stick with Adobe longer; students, hobbyists, and small studios are likeliest to normalize non‑Adobe stacks.  
- Watch next: Migration tools for Lightroom/Premiere catalogs, school curricula adopting alternatives, and whether free tiers stay sustainable without hostile monetization pivots.
