# The seven programming ur-languages (2022)

- Score: 269 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47822486) | Link: https://madhadron.com/programming/seven_ur_languages.html

### TL;DR
The article argues most mainstream languages are surface variants of a few deeper “ur‑languages” that shape how you think: ALGOL (imperative), Lisp (code-as-data macros), ML (typed functional), Self (pure message-passing objects), Forth (stack-based, extensible parser), APL (array calculus), and Prolog (logic/relations). Learning one from each family builds new mental models, not just syntax. HN debate centers on taxonomy (e.g., where Ruby, Python, Lean fit), performance and practicality, and how language ecosystems affect what beginners can actually build.
