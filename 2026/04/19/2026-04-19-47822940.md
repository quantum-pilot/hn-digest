# Ask HN: How did you land your first projects as a solo engineer/consultant?

- Score: 241 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47822940) | Link: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47822940

## TL;DR
New solo engineers mostly land first projects by building trust in visible communities, not by spraying cold emails. Tactics include: being consistently helpful in niche Slack/Facebook groups; specializing deeply so you’re the obvious person for “that one thing”; showing a real human identity; and leveraging platforms like Upwork with proposal-as-consultation instead of generic pitches. Cold outreach can work if you give concrete value first (e.g., a small fix or plan), then use quick wins and milestones to grow relationships.
