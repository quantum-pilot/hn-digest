# FSF trying to contact Google about spammer sending 10k+ mails from Gmail account

- Score: 360 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47788424) | Link: https://daedal.io/@thomzane/116410863009847575

### TL;DR
An FSF sysadmin publicly asks for a direct contact at Google after a spammer sent over 10k emails from a Gmail account and normal abuse forms went nowhere. HN commenters describe similar experiences: abuse, fraud and billing problems are nearly impossible to escalate inside Google, Microsoft and Amazon without legal pressure. Email admins say these giants are now major spam sources yet simultaneously punish small or self‑hosted senders, and discuss new spam tactics like subscription bombing routed through Google Groups.

### Comment pulse
- Legal route sometimes works → Certified-mailing a police report and cover letter to Google’s legal department produced a human response and account shutdown within days.  
- Big providers ignore abuse → Google and Amazon emit spam but can’t be broadly blocked; self‑hosted domains get harsh filtering—counterpoint: Gmail suspends heavily reported senders.  
- New spam tactic via Google Groups → “Subscription bombing”: bots sign victims up to many lists, flooding inboxes so emails or alerts are easily missed.  

### LLM perspective
- View: Abuse-reporting at hyperscalers optimizes for scale, not redress, leaving edge cases and non-customers effectively unserved.  
- Impact: Civil-society orgs, small businesses, and self-hosted email admins carry disproportionate costs for others’ spam and misconfigurations.  
- Watch next: Standardized abuse-report APIs, regulatory SLAs for large providers, and community-run blocklists that treat big senders like everyone else.
