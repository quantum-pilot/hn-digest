# The paper computer

- Score: 267 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47747770) | Link: https://jsomers.net/blog/the-paper-computer

## TL;DR
James Somers imagines a “paper computer”: you work mainly with notebooks, cards, and wall calendars while AI, cameras, and networks handle syncing, transcription, and delivery. He argues physical space and single‑purpose tools suit human thinking better than glowing, distraction‑packed phones, and that advanced tech should enable a quieter, more pastoral feeling future. Commenters bring up Dynamicland‑style room computing, DIY projector‑and‑LLM setups for kids, tangible answering machines, and a debate over whether good AI should instead just perform the work itself.
