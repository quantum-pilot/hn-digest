# Claude Opus 4.7

- Score: 1386 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47793411) | Link: https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-opus-4-7

## TL;DR
Claude Opus 4.7 is a drop‑in upgrade to 4.6 focused on hard, long‑running engineering and “agentic” workflows: better instruction‑following, self‑checking code, higher‑resolution vision, improved document reasoning, and more consistent use of file-based memory. Pricing is unchanged but an updated tokenizer and extra effort levels alter real token usage. Cyber capabilities are deliberately dialed down and guarded by new filters and a Cyber Verification Program. HN discussion centers on adaptive-thinking opacity, overzealous cyber filters, regressions in 4.6, and shifting loyalty to competitors.

## Comment pulse
- Adaptive thinking and reasoning output changed: new modes are confusing, summaries are hidden by default, and some feel Anthropic replaced “you pick the budget” with “trust us”.

- Tokenization worries spur “caveman”-style prompt compression, but engineers note tiny savings; others report 4.7 is actually cheaper per solved task via fewer reasoning tokens.

- Cyber filters aggressively block legitimate security research and even benign code, pushing researchers to Codex; some defend this as a short-term defense buffer—counterpoint: many see it as self-defeating censorship.

## LLM perspective
- View: The model itself looks strong; the real risk is growing complexity and opacity of harness features that developers must reverse‑engineer.

- Impact: Heavy users will benchmark end‑to‑end cost/quality, tune effort/adaptive-thinking, and may diversify across vendors to avoid sudden policy or quality shifts.

- Watch next: Transparent knobs for reasoning visibility, more precise cyber filters with lower false positives, and third‑party evaluations of agentic reliability on real software projects.
