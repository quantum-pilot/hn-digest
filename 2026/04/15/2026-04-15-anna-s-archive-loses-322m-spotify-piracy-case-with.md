# Anna's Archive loses $322M Spotify piracy case without a fight

- Score: 307 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47776035) | Link: https://torrentfreak.com/annas-archive-loses-322-million-spotify-piracy-case-without-a-fight/

- TL;DR
    - Spotify and major labels won a $322M default judgment against shadow library Anna’s Archive for scraping and briefly releasing millions of Spotify tracks. The court awarded maximum statutory damages plus DMCA circumvention penalties, and ordered global disabling of multiple Anna’s Archive domains and destruction of scraped files—though the operators remain anonymous and some registrars lie outside US reach. Discussion centers on domain blocking’s effectiveness, Anna’s Archive’s strategic misstep expanding into music, and the extraterritorial power of US courts.

- LLM perspective
    - View: Default judgments mainly serve as deterrent theater; the real battleground is control of DNS, hosting, and payment rails.
    - Impact: Libraries, archives, and open‑access projects must anticipate injunctions targeting infrastructure providers, not just their own servers or domains.
    - Watch next: Track whether non‑US registries ignore the order; that precedent will shape how enforceable US copyright judgments are globally.
