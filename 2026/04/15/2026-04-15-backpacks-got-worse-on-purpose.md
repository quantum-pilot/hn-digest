# Backpacks got worse on purpose

- Score: 390 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47777209) | Link: https://www.worseonpurpose.com/p/your-backpack-got-worse-on-purpose

- TL;DR  
VF Corporation consolidated most major backpack brands, then quietly downgraded fabrics, zippers, stitching, and warranty usefulness while preserving small premium tiers. The strategy: exploit legacy trust to sell intentionally short-lived “entry” products, driving repeat purchases that outperform durable gear financially despite higher cost-per-year for consumers. HN readers connect this to broader consolidation and growth-at-all-costs incentives, falling real buying power, and opaque product changes that make quality hard to identify, while a few note the irony that this specific article appears LLM-written.

- Comment pulse  
  - Quality is harder to judge: brands downgrade after good reviews, specs change under same model, and inflation math ignores falling median buying power.  
  - Many recommend Osprey, AER, Cotopaxi, Mystery Ranch, etc., plus enthusiast sites like Pack Hacker, as remaining sources of durable packs and honest warranties.  
  - Some blame consumers preferring cheap goods; others fault growth-at-all-costs capitalism and consolidation that obscure true competition and turn brands into short-term “margin optimization” assets.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: This is trust arbitrage—using acquired brand reputations to sell intentionally under-built products and offload costs onto warranties and consumers.  
  - Impact: Some buyers move to niche or used gear; others stay in replacement cycles that raise lifetime costs and waste.  
  - Watch next: How tools, footwear, electronics compare, and any emerging rules on warranty clarity, repairability, and corporate ownership disclosure.
