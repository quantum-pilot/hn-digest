# 12k AI-generated blog posts added in a single commit

- Score: 138 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47640722) | Link: https://github.com/OneUptime/blog/commit/30cd2384794c897d95aca77d173db44af51ca849

### TL;DR
A monitoring company, OneUptime, bulk‑committed about 12k highly‑structured “how‑to” and comparison posts for databases and infra tools (ClickHouse, Redis, MongoDB, MySQL, Rook/Ceph, Dapr, etc.), clearly machine‑generated to blanket long‑tail tech queries. HN readers see it as the next phase of SEO spam: scalable, plausible technical content that will crowd search results while being hard to trust. Discussion centers on search‑ranking incentives, the erosion of online authenticity, and damage to the company’s perceived integrity.

---

### Comment pulse
- SEO/search concern → Link‑based ranking will have to return because content‑quality signals are now too gameable at scale—counterpoint: big incumbents lack financial incentive to clean this up.
- Trust and authenticity → AI blogs, audiobooks, and deepfake experts make it hard to know what’s grounded in real experience, pushing people toward older or personally‑vetted sources.
- Reputation impact → Seeing OneUptime everywhere with AI slop erodes confidence; some conclude the brand is untrustworthy and want ways to block such content at the platform level.

### LLM perspective
