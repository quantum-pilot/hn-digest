# Gold overtakes U.S. Treasuries as the largest foreign reserve asset

- Score: 255 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47635056) | Link: https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/international/us/gold-overtakes-u-s-treasuries-as-the-worlds-largest-foreign-reserve-asset-in-2026-can-gold-challenge-the-u-s-dollars-dominance-and-hold-its-ground/articleshow/126420128.cms?from=mdr

- TL;DR  
  - Central banks now hold about $4T of gold—worth more than foreign‑owned U.S. Treasuries—after a 70% price surge and heavy 2025 buying, especially by emerging markets hedging sanctions, inflation and U.S. fiscal risk. Gold’s share of official reserves has climbed to roughly a quarter, yet the dollar still dominates FX reserves. Hacker News frames this as the long‑running erosion of dollar hegemony and U.S. “monetary empire,” with debate over blame, significance, and gold‑industry spin.

- Comment pulse  
  - Long erosion of dollar hegemony, not sudden shock → Gold’s rise reflects decades of falling dollar reserves since 1971; 2025 geopolitics mainly accelerant, not cause.  
  - US self‑inflicted decline → Commenters see America trading monetary empire for short‑term enrichment of elites via debt, war, deregulation—counterpoint: others stress broader global rebalancing instead.  
  - Skepticism on significance and data → Much shift is price-driven; WGC (gold miners) may be hyping; constraint is physical metal and US’s huge refinancing needs.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: This marks diversification of reserve assets, not replacement of the dollar; liquidity still anchors Treasuries’ role.  
  - Impact: Higher US borrowing costs, more volatile FX flows, and stronger bargaining power for large gold‑holding emerging economies.  
  - Watch next: Central bank disclosures, gold‑backed settlement schemes, and how US fiscal policy responds to thinner foreign demand for Treasuries.
