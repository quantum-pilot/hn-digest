# San Diego rents declined following surge in supply

- Score: 216 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47857477) | Link: https://www.kpbs.org/news/economy/2026/03/27/san-diego-rents-declined-more-than-19-of-nations-top-20-markets-following-surge-in-supply

- TL;DR  
  - Zumper data show San Diego’s median rents dropped 5.6% for one-bedrooms and 7.5% for two-bedrooms year-over-year, among the steepest falls in major U.S. markets. A roughly 15% jump in active listings and near-record housing permits—helped by pro-housing policies and community plan updates—boosted competition and forced landlords to offer lower prices or concessions. Hacker News discussion connects this to basic supply-and-demand, but also stresses landlord price-fixing, ADUs, and job losses as important context.

- Comment pulse  
  - YIMBYs: data prove more supply cuts rents; NIMBY claims that “supply and demand don’t apply to housing” seen as self-serving nonsense.  
  - Critics: corporate landlords blunt supply effects via rent-fixing, warehousing, concessions, subsidies; SD’s ADUs and public-housing-style models work better — counterpoint: still supply growth at core.  
  - Others note housing dominates FIRE math; falling rents accelerate retirement timelines. Some attribute San Diego’s drop to biotech job losses, contrasting with anti-building San Francisco.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Evidence from San Diego and Austin strengthens case for permissive zoning plus ADUs, not just luxury megaprojects.  
  - Impact: Lower rents reshape labor markets, FIRE plans, and migration; policy choices on supply now have clear, quantifiable household-level stakes.  
  - Watch next: Track multi-year rent trends vs. permit data, ADU counts, and job growth to distinguish cyclical slumps from durable supply-driven affordability.
