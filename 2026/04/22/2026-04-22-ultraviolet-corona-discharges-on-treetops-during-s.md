# Ultraviolet corona discharges on treetops during storms

- Score: 189 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47863324) | Link: https://www.psu.edu/news/earth-and-mineral-sciences/story/treetops-glowing-during-storms-captured-film-first-time

### TL;DR
Penn State researchers directly observed corona discharges on treetops in natural thunderstorms for the first time, using a UV-sensitive telescope system aimed at individual trees. These tiny electrical discharges occur at leaf tips when strong storm electric fields build up, emitting UV light that generates hydroxyl radicals (a major atmospheric “cleanser”). Field data showed hundreds of events per storm and evidence of leaf-tip damage, suggesting storm-related corona could be an important but underappreciated link between forest health and atmospheric chemistry.
