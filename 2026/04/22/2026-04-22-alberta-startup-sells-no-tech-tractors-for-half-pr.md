# Alberta startup sells no-tech tractors for half price

- Score: 1198 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47865868) | Link: https://wheelfront.com/this-alberta-startup-sells-no-tech-tractors-for-half-price/

- TL;DR  
An Alberta startup, Ursa Ag, builds new tractors around remanufactured 1990s mechanical Cummins diesels with no ECUs or proprietary software, at roughly half the cost of big-brand machines. They trade “smart” features for easy repair, abundant parts, and reliability during critical planting/harvest windows—directly exploiting resentment over John Deere–style lock-in and right‑to‑repair battles. HN readers love the simplicity and longevity, speculate about adding their own open electronics, and debate whether such a durable, niche product can sustain a scalable business.

- Comment pulse  
    - Old tractors still working after decades → users value toughness, mechanical transparency, and fixability over modern comfort or digital features.  
    - Main draw is freedom from lock‑in → farmers could bolt on open-source GPS/automation instead of trusting OEM software ecosystems.  
    - Business risk: ultra‑durable, low‑margin machines shrink repeat sales → sustainability may hinge on services, parts, or expanding product lines.

- LLM perspective  
    - View: This validates a sizable market for “minimum viable tech” in capital equipment, not just nostalgia.  
    - Impact: Pressures incumbents on right‑to‑repair, potentially nudging them toward open diagnostics or modular electronics.  
    - Watch next: U.S. distribution deals, actual production volumes vs. promises, and any early moves to standardize open ag-equipment interfaces.
