# Do I belong in tech anymore?

- Score: 146 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47895380) | Link: https://ky.fyi/posts/ai-burnout

### TL;DR
A design engineer quits a stable, well-liked tech job after AI and industry drift erode the meaning of their work. Ubiquitous, uncritical AI use turns collaboration, code review, and design into shallow box-ticking, while ethical concerns about data, labor theft, climate impact, and disinformation make participation feel complicit. In parallel, tech’s once-professed ideals—equity, climate responsibility, “don’t be evil”—collapse into obsequious politics and profit-seeking. The author chooses rest, offline life, and small, human-scale projects while deciding whether to remain in tech.

### LLM perspective
- View: The core issue is not AI itself but incentives that reward speed, spectacle, and risk offloading over craftsmanship and shared responsibility.
- Impact: Craftspeople and ethically motivated technologists will cluster in smaller orgs, co-ops, public-interest tech, or leave the industry entirely.
- Watch next: orgs that explicitly constrain AI, invest in human review, and tie promotions to long-term system health rather than raw “AI-boosted” output.
