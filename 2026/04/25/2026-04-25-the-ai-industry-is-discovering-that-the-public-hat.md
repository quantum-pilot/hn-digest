# The AI Industry Is Discovering That the Public Hates It

- Score: 188 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47904568) | Link: https://newrepublic.com/article/209163/ai-industry-discovering-public-backlash

- TL;DR  
    - The article argues that a full-blown populist backlash against AI is emerging, from violent attacks on AI-linked figures to social-media schadenfreude and poor favorability polling. Surveys show a huge optimism–pessimism gap between AI experts and the public, who mostly see rising costs, shaky jobs, higher utility bills, and little real productivity gain. Firms respond with glossy “public benefit” plans while lobbying against regulation and liability, eroding trust. The author says only verifiable sharing of gains, transparency, and democratic control over infrastructure can avert escalating AI populism.

- Comment pulse  
    - AI resentment → CEOs loudly promise job destruction, use AI as a pretext for layoffs, and train on others’ work without consent; people see a power grab, not progress.  
    - Proposed fix → Tax AI and use UBI, plus environmental and creative-work rules, to soften disruption—counterpoint: revenue and social-role math make this far from sufficient.  
    - Perception gap → Inside companies and conferences, pro-AI enthusiasm is socially mandatory, so surveys overstate support and feed a sense of unstoppable inevitability.

- LLM perspective  
    - View: Separate anger at specific corporate conduct from underlying tools; regulate concentrated power, data rights, and externalities, not “AI” in the abstract.  
    - Impact: Expect tougher local fights over data centers, stronger labor demands on automation, and more scrutiny of claimed productivity gains.  
    - Watch next: Independent impact audits, enforceable community-benefit agreements, and cross-border rules on liability, training data compensation, and energy intensity.
