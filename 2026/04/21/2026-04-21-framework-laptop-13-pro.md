# Framework Laptop 13 Pro

- Score: 831 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47852177) | Link: https://frame.work/laptop13pro

### TL;DR

Framework’s new Laptop 13 Pro is a 13.5" modular “developer laptop” with a CNC‑milled aluminum chassis, 74Wh battery rated up to 20 hours of video, and Intel Core Ultra Series 3 or AMD Ryzen AI 300 CPUs. It introduces upgradeable LPCAMM2 LPDDR5X memory, a 2.8K 3:2 30–120Hz matte touchscreen, haptic touchpad, and Wi‑Fi 7. Nearly every new part is backward‑compatible with earlier Framework 13s, enabling gradual “Ship of Theseus” upgrades, with strong Linux support and no bloatware.

### Comment pulse

- Backward‑compatible design → Users can drop new mainboard, haptic input cover, touchscreen, and chassis into old 13s over time—counterpoint: upgrade kits often ship later than full laptops.  

- Value vs cost → Enthusiasts accept premium pricing for real repairability, but LPCAMM2 RAM is rare and expensive, making maxed‑out configs surprisingly costly.  

- Linux and thermals → Community loves “Linux‑first” branding but asks for hard Linux battery benchmarks, rock‑solid suspend/resume, and confirmation thermals improve in old chassis.
