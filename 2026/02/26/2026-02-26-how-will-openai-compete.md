# How will OpenAI compete?

- Score: 457 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47158975) | Link: https://www.ben-evans.com/benedictevans/2026/2/19/how-will-openai-compete-nkg2x

## TL;DR
Evans argues OpenAI currently has no durable moat: frontier models are near-commodities, ChatGPT usage is broad but shallow, and there’s no network effect or must-have product on top. OpenAI’s response—ads, app stores, agents, huge capex, full-stack “platform” ambitions—may only buy it a seat in an eventual infrastructure oligopoly, not Windows‑style control. He’s skeptical standards/APIs or “ChatGPT identity” will create lock‑in; long-term advantage may hinge on relentless execution, not structural power. HN debates user stickiness, local models, privacy, and Google’s latent edge.

## Comment pulse
- OpenAI has real consumer stickiness → habits, chat history, and brand reduce switching—counterpoint: casual users are fickle; ads or outages push them to the next free bot.  
- Local models will obsolete cloud LLMs → VAX-to-PC analogy—counterpoint: state-of-the-art needs >100B params; VRAM and datacenter economics favor centralized inference for most users.  
- Strategy is “too big to fail” → massive infra bets entangle suppliers and markets—counterpoint: public skepticism and plentiful alternatives make a government-style bailout unlikely.

## LLM perspective
- View: Foundation models trend toward interchangeable utilities; differentiation shifts to distribution, UX, vertical solutions, and trust (safety, governance, privacy).  
- Impact: Hyperscalers can bundle AI into existing products; OpenAI must either own key workflows or accept a premium-API, R&D-heavy role.  
- Watch next: Real DAU/retention vs competitors, device-level assistants from Apple/Google, and open-weight benchmarks narrowing the capability gap with closed models.
