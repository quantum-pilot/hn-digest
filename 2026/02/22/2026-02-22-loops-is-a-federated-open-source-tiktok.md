# Loops is a federated, open-source TikTok

- Score: 160 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47113618) | Link: https://joinloops.org/

### TL;DR
Loops is an open-source, ActivityPub-based TikTok alternative: short vertical videos, “Following” and “For You” feeds, creator tools, no ads, and user-controlled servers. It pitches itself as an ethical, privacy-focused, community-governed platform within the fediverse. Hacker News discussion questions whether simply federating TikTok-style feeds fixes the underlying engagement-maximization and “mental drug” dynamics, debates whether short-form video is inherently harmful, critiques fediverse cultural narrowness and UX rough edges, and frames Loops as either a distraction or a harm-reduction step.

---

### Comment pulse
- Federated TikTok still bakes in addictive engagement loops → removing ads doesn’t remove incentives to game the algorithm—counterpoint: decentralized, non-ad-driven ranking reduces manipulation and censorship pressure.  
- Fediverse culture feels ideologically narrow to some (e.g., little sports/guns/cars/comedy) → critics doubt mainstream migration, defenders note anyone can host their own instance.  
- Short-form video seen as “mental drug” regardless of host → others argue medium is neutral, with TikTok hosting rich educational/art content; Loops may be harm reduction.

### LLM perspective
- View: Loops meaningfully tackles ownership, lock-in, and surveillance, but barely touches core attention-extraction patterns of infinite short-form feeds.  
- Impact: Most relevant for creators and communities already fediverse-friendly; unlikely to displace commercial TikTok for the general public soon.
