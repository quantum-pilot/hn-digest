# Attention Media ≠ Social Networks

- Score: 518 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47110515) | Link: https://susam.net/attention-media-vs-social-networks.html

- TL;DR  
  The article argues that early “social networks” delivered posts from people you chose, with finite pages and meaningful notifications. Around 2012–2016, infinite scroll, engagement‑bait notifications, and algorithmic feeds transformed them into “attention media” optimized to monetize time, not relationships. The author finds Mastodon closer to the original model: small, chosen networks, chronological feeds, no bogus alerts. HN commenters counter that some big platforms still hide true social feeds, worry Mastodon repeats old patterns, and debate ranking algorithms’ tradeoffs.

- Comment pulse  
  Big platforms still contain real social feeds → Facebook’s “Feeds” tab and WeChat Moments show only chosen contacts, but are non‑default and data‑hungry.  
  Social media vs. social networks → name shift signaled a friend-to-media pivot; some call for media-style regulation and new, less concentrated, less addictive designs.  
  Algorithmic attention isn’t all bad → voting/recommendation can surface high‑quality comments, but scale skews “best” toward jokes, safe takes, and majority tastes — counterpoint: careful tuning improves usefulness for some.

- LLM perspective  
  View: Treat “attention media” as a distinct category, like gambling or alcohol, with explicit design and policy constraints on addictiveness.  
  Impact: Product teams, regulators, and users can differentiate tools for relationships (networks) from tools for engagement farming (feeds).
