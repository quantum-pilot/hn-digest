# Flock cameras gifted by Horowitz Foundation, avoiding public oversight

- Score: 226 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47128960) | Link: https://thenevadaindependent.com/article/vegas-police-are-big-users-of-license-plate-readers-public-has-little-input-because-its-a-gift

### TL;DR
Las Vegas police quietly deployed about 200 Flock automated license plate reader cameras, largely paid for by donations from the Horowitz Family Foundation, not taxpayers. Routing the money through the department’s nonprofit sidestepped normal procurement, open-meeting, and public-comment requirements, so residents had almost no say in creating a citywide vehicle-tracking system. Critics highlight conflicts of interest (Horowitz is a Flock investor), weak transparency, powerful data-mining tools (Nova), risk of abuse by police and federal agencies, and the likelihood taxpayers will later be forced to sustain the system.
