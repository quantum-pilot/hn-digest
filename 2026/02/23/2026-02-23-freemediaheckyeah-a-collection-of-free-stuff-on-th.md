# Freemediaheckyeah – A collection of free stuff on the internet

- Score: 256 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47117780) | Link: https://fmhy.net/

### TL;DR
Freemediaheckyeah (FMHY) is a large, actively maintained directory of “free stuff” online: adblock/privacy tools, AI, streaming, books, games, mobile apps, education, and more, heavily including piracy resources. It’s positioned as a human-curated alternative to search engines, with search, backups, and community contributions. Hacker News discussion centers on how best to organize such massive link lists (tags, ranking, descriptions), the ethics of piracy framed as preservation versus theft, and nostalgia for earlier open directories and hacker ideals.

### Comment pulse
- Better list UX → people want tags, search, and ranking over giant flat lists; some prefer short “why this is good” blurbs over metadata.  
- “Free” meaning contested → some stress it’s free-as-in-pirated; others invoke hacker culture’s “information wants to be free” and practical usefulness of FMHY.  
- Piracy as preservation → gaps in legal deposit for films/games make pirates de facto archivists — counterpoint: that’s still theft; preservation could be separated from infringement.
