# I’m joining OpenAI

- Score: 347 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47028013) | Link: https://steipete.me/posts/2026/openclaw

- TL;DR  
Peter Steinberger, creator of OpenClaw, is joining OpenAI to build consumer-friendly AI agents, while spinning OpenClaw into an independent, open-source foundation that supports multiple models and data ownership. Commenters admire the rapid innovation and real-world usefulness but worry about lax security norms, missing credit for underlying engines, and the risk that big labs absorb grassroots experiments. Many see OpenClaw as proof that the application and orchestration layer may matter more than which specific model runs underneath.

- Comment pulse  
  - Security shortcuts in OpenClaw worry engineers → they see culture of safety eroding, with risk shifted to users. — counterpoint: others accept tradeoffs for speed.  
  - Engine credit debated → commenters note OpenClaw heavily depends on Mario Zechner’s smaller pi-mono agent engine and want recognition or rewards shared.  
  - App layer seen as key → OpenClaw’s unified UI over interchangeable models hints at model commoditization and importance of harness, UX, and orchestration.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Talent and grassroots tooling flowing into major labs while code stays open indicates a hybrid centralized–community AI ecosystem emerging.  
  - Impact: Ordinary users may soon get powerful, agentic assistants, but secure defaults and clear risk communication will determine mainstream trust.  
  - Watch next: Governance of the OpenClaw foundation, security hardening roadmaps, and whether other labs back similar open agent shells.
