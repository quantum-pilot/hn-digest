# Aging muscle stem cells shift from rapid repair to long-term survival

- Score: 68 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46847746) | Link: https://phys.org/news/2026-01-sprint-marathon-aging-muscle-stem.html

- TL;DR  
    In aging mouse muscle, stem cells accumulate high levels of NDRG1, a tumor-suppressor protein that inhibits mTOR, slowing their activation and tissue repair but extending their survival in stressful aged tissue. Blocking NDRG1 reverts old cells to youthful, fast-repair behavior, yet depletes the stem-cell pool after repeated injuries. Researchers frame this as “cellular survivorship bias”: aging favors slower, resilient cells over sprinters, implying therapies must balance short-term regeneration against long-term stem-cell maintenance.
