# Google workers seek 'red lines' on military A.I., echoing Anthropic

- Score: 277 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47175931) | Link: https://www.nytimes.com/2026/02/26/technology/google-deepmind-letter-pentagon.html

## TL;DR

Google AI staff are joining Anthropic in pushing back on unrestricted Pentagon access to large language models. Over 100 Google employees wrote to chief scientist Jeff Dean demanding “red lines” that bar Gemini from domestic mass surveillance and fully autonomous weapons. In parallel, dozens of OpenAI staff and more Google workers publicly criticized the Pentagon’s hardball negotiations with Anthropic over similar limits. Hacker News debates whether employee resistance can meaningfully slow military AI amid a global arms race and entrenched defense interests.

## Comment pulse

- Arms-race worry → If U.S. engineers refuse military AI, rivals like China gain an edge; others argue for treaties and that internal resistance still slows capabilities.

- “Genie is out” vs restraint → Some think limits are futile; others note past nuclear near-misses as proof that norms and red lines can prevent worst outcomes.
