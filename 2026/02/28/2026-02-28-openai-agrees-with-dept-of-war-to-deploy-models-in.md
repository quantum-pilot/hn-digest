# OpenAI agrees with Dept. of War to deploy models in their classified network

- Score: 1327 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47189650) | Link: https://twitter.com/sama/status/2027578652477821175

### TL;DR
OpenAI has agreed to deploy its models inside the U.S. Department of War’s classified networks under an “all lawful uses” framework, with redlines implemented via references to existing law (e.g., bans on domestic mass surveillance and autonomous weapons). This contrasts with Anthropic, which insisted on its own TOS-defined redlines and reportedly faced aggressive government pressure for refusing. Hacker News discussion centers on whether “lawful use” is an adequate safeguard, employee and user complicity, and the state’s leverage over AI labs.
