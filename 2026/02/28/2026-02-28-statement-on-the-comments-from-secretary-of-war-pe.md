# Statement on the comments from Secretary of War Pete Hegseth

- Score: 1118 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47188697) | Link: https://www.anthropic.com/news/statement-comments-secretary-war

### TL;DR
Anthropic says US Secretary of War Pete Hegseth plans to label it a “supply-chain risk” after it refused two uses of Claude: mass domestic surveillance of Americans and fully autonomous weapons. The company argues current models are unsafe for lethal autonomy and that bulk US surveillance violates fundamental rights, calls the move unprecedented, and says it can only restrict Claude in defense contracts, not other customers. Hacker News largely praises the stand while debating its US‑centric ethics and durability under competition.

### Comment pulse
- Anthropic is genuinely walking away from lucrative defense work → many see this as rare, value-driven restraint—counterpoint: incentives may later replace principled leaders.  
- Policy line (no US mass surveillance, no fully autonomous weapons) → praised as ethical bar—counterpoint: critics note it permits foreign surveillance and human-in-the-loop killing.  
- Government pressure framed as loyalty test → some compare it to authoritarian regimes and say it highlights competitors’ willingness to support surveillance and autonomous weapons.
