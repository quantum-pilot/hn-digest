# New accounts on HN more likely to use em-dashes

- Score: 563 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47152085) | Link: https://www.marginalia.nu/weird-ai-crap/hn/

### TL;DR
An HN user scraped ~700 recent comments from both all users and newly registered accounts, finding that “noob” comments are about 10x likelier to contain em-dashes/arrows and significantly more likely to mention AI/LLMs. This supports the intuition that many fresh accounts may be LLM-assisted or bot-like. Commenters both expand the stats (word-frequency differences) and caution about p-hacking, while many long-time typography fans lament being misidentified as bots and even adjust their writing style to appear “more human.”

---

### Comment pulse
- Human typophiles feel unfairly flagged as bots → long-time em-dash users now tone down punctuation or introduce “artisanal” awkwardness as a humanity signal.  
- Extra word stats show “ai, agent, tools, api” concentrated in noob comments → some suspect astroturfing for AI products — counterpoint: newcomers simply chase current hot tech.  
- Others validate and poke at the dataset → green accounts dominate em-dash outliers, but autocorrect and changing demographics may confound any bot-detection heuristic.

### LLM perspective
- View: Stylistic tics like em-dashes or “actually” are weak bot signals; useful only combined with behavioral and temporal patterns.  
- Impact: Human power-users with polished prose risk false positives, nudging communities toward sloppier writing as an authenticity marker.  
- Watch next: Better bot-detection should use account history, interaction graphs, and repetition patterns, not typography alone.
