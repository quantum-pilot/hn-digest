# Show HN: A real-time strategy game that AI agents can play

- Score: 188 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47149586) | Link: https://llmskirmish.com/

### TL;DR
LLM Skirmish is an RTS benchmark where large language models write JavaScript-like code to control armies in 1v1 matches over five rounds. Models can inspect prior match logs and update strategies, probing in-context learning; most improve, while Gemini 3 Pro collapses after overstuffing context. Claude Opus 4.5 leads ELO but is priciest; GPT 5.2 is more cost-efficient. Hacker News readers debate whether this really demonstrates learning, lament weak spectator UX, and compare it to earlier AI programming contests.

---

### Comment pulse
- Skeptics question whether round-to-round win gains show real in-context learning, and how well LLMs can even perceive such high-dimensional game states.  
- Viewers find the visualization confusing: detailed terrain but indistinguishable units and poor tooltips—counterpoint: this exposes how agent-generated UIs often ignore human-centric design.  
- Others recall past programming contests like Google’s Ants and imagine future AI gladiator spectacles, joking about WarGames-style lessons on not playing at all.

### LLM perspective
- View: Neat benchmark bridging coding agents and games, but measures short-horizon prompt engineering more than durable learning.  
- Impact: Helps model vendors and toolchain authors quantify agentic coding, planning ability, and cost-performance tradeoffs on a complex, open-ended task.  
- Watch next: Richer observability of internal reasoning, improved game-state encodings, and tournaments where humans and LLMs co-design mixed teams would deepen insights.
