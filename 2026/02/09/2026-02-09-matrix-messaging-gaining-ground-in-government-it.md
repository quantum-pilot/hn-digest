# Matrix messaging gaining ground in government IT

- Score: 210 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46944245) | Link: https://www.theregister.com/2026/02/09/matrix_element_secure_chat/

## TL;DR

Matrix, an open, federated messaging protocol, is increasingly used as the secure chat layer for governments seeking digital sovereignty. The UN, International Criminal Court, German and French public sectors, Swiss Post, Austrian healthcare, and others are adopting Element-based stacks or embedding Matrix in national workspaces. Matrix 2.0 and the Element X client improve performance and group calls. HN commenters like the sovereignty and openness but report rough UX, reliability issues, hosting complexity, and unresolved privacy/jurisdiction questions slowing broader take‑up.

## Comment pulse

- Consumer users want stickers/gifs and smooth sync; Matrix clients feel buggy and joyless vs Telegram/Discord — counterpoint: some value distraction-free, work-focused messaging.  

- Self‑hosting Matrix 2.0 (Synapse, MAS, Call) is complex; small groups often depend on a resident admin, while Mattermost-style single binaries win on simplicity.  

- Regulated sectors worry about group forward secrecy granularity, unencrypted metadata, GDPR exposure from federation, and UK‑based governance, even though many European agencies still deploy it.
