### TL;DR
A blog post showcases AI-generated children’s books whose illustrations veer into accidental body horror (broken jaws, nightmare owls), raising concerns about quality, safety, and taste in kids’ media. HN readers split: some see this as emblematic of “AI slop” flooding culture and shifting fact-checking onto parents; others use LLMs to craft highly customized, beloved bedtime stories with careful prompting. Many respond by recommending human-made children’s books as examples of real craft, intent, and editorial care.
