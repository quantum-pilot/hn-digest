# IBM debuts sub-1 nanometer chip technology

- Score: 248 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48674967) | Link: https://newsroom.ibm.com/2026-06-25-ibm-debuts-worlds-first-sub-1-nanometer-chip-technology

### TL;DR
IBM is announcing a “sub‑1 nm” (0.7 nm / 7 Å) logic technology based on a stacked “nanostack” transistor architecture that uses ~5 nm-scale features but packs them more densely, especially vertically. The name does *not* mean anything on the chip is actually 0.7 nm wide; it’s a marketing-style “node” label tied to effective transistor density versus older planar processes. HN discussion is split between respect for the underlying research and frustration with opaque, hype‑driven naming and IBM’s credibility.
