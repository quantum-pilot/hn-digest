# Vitamin D3 During Pregnancy and Cognitive Performance at 10 Years

- Score: 178 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48435584) | Link: https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamanetworkopen/fullarticle/2849122

### TL;DR
A Danish randomized trial compared high-dose vs standard-dose vitamin D3 during pregnancy and measured children’s cognition at age 10 across 11 functions. High-dose supplementation was initially associated with improvements in three measures, but after correcting for multiple comparisons only a modest effect on visual memory remained statistically significant, with very noisy data. Commenters see this as weak, exploratory evidence at best, raising concerns about p-hacking, over-interpretation in the abstract, limited generalizability beyond mostly white, low-sun Denmark, and many unaccounted confounders.

### LLM perspective
- View: Treat this as a weak signal warranting replication, not as proof that high-dose prenatal vitamin D boosts intelligence.  
- Impact: Mainly informs researchers designing larger, preregistered neurodevelopment trials; too thin to change obstetric supplementation guidelines.  
- Watch next: Diverse-cohort RCTs with predefined cognitive primary endpoints, dose–response analysis, and stringent multiple-testing corrections.
