# How Liminalism Became the Defining Aesthetic of Our Time

- Score: 165 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48431085) | Link: https://hyperallergic.com/how-liminalism-became-the-defining-aesthetic-of-our-time/

### TL;DR
- Liminalism is an internet-born aesthetic centered on photos of empty, familiar spaces—malls, corridors, offices—that feel uncanny, nostalgic, and placeless. The article roots it in Backrooms creepypasta, COVID-emptied cities, and a lineage from Surrealism and Edward Hopper, arguing it visualizes late-capitalist alienation and digital homogenization. Hacker News commenters push back on calling it “defining,” emphasize its niche, point to missing influences like MyHouse.WAD and House of Leaves, and intriguingly liken liminal spaces to AI latent spaces under late capitalism.
