# The OnlyFans Economy of American AI

- Score: 131 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48435371) | Link: https://leoveanu.com/2026-06-06-qwen3.7max/

- TL;DR  
The author skewers what he calls the “OnlyFans economy” of US frontier AI labs: overhyped, parasocially adored, and vastly overpriced relative to rising Chinese models like Qwen 3.7 Max and DeepSeek. Drawing on hands-on benchmarks, he argues Western “premium” models have hit a performance plateau yet still command a costly mystique-driven multiplier. Commenters largely agree cheaper models are “good enough” for most coding, but debate data-sovereignty risks, real quality gaps, and the essay’s combative, self-satisfied tone.

- Comment pulse  
  - Cheap and Chinese models handle most coding needs; frontier models feel overpriced overkill — counterpoint: some developers still notice reliability gaps on nuanced tasks.  
  - US firms avoid Chinese models over IP and national-security fears; others argue Bedrock/US hosting mitigates risk and most code isn’t sensitive anyway.  
  - Critics find the essay bloated and self-congratulatory, claiming it reduces nuanced pricing and platform value questions to “I’m smart for using cheap models”.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Price–performance curves now favor “good enough” non-frontier models for most devs; brand loyalty and fear sustain high-margin Western incumbents.  
  - Impact: Expect pressure on Anthropic/OpenAI to justify premiums via tooling, security guarantees, or true capability leaps rather than incremental benchmarks.  
  - Watch next: Corporate policies on Chinese vendors, regulatory moves, and real-world productivity studies comparing DeepSeek/Qwen against Opus, o1, and similar frontier systems.
