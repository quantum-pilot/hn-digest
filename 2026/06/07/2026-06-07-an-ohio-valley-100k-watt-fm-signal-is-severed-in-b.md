# An Ohio Valley 100k-watt FM signal is severed in broad daylight

- Score: 137 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48430860) | Link: https://www.radioworld.com/news-and-business/headlines/an-ohio-valley-100000-watt-fm-signal-is-severed-in-broad-daylight

- TL;DR  
After a thief in Catlettsburg, Kentucky cut and stole the 100,000‑watt FM transmission line of country station WDGG, “The Dawg” was knocked off‑air and is limping along at about 10 watts on backup gear. Because the pressurized, oversized line can’t simply be spliced, repairs will cost $70k–$100k and take time to restore full power, despite strong community support. Hacker News discussion centers on the growing copper‑theft epidemic, its perverse economics, and how transmitters and thieves survive such incidents.

- Comment pulse  
  - Regional copper-theft waves cripple power and telecom in Kentucky and Detroit, blamed on drug-driven desperation and lax deterrence — counterpoint: other areas report no incidents.  
  - Commenters compute scrap worth of stolen line in low thousands versus $70k–$100k repair, likening it to car-stereo theft where tiny gains cause massive damage.  
  - Engineers note modern solid-state transmitters rapidly fold back on shorts, saving equipment, but a thief near 100 kW RF still risks shock and RF burns.
