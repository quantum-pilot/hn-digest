# LLMs are eroding my software engineering career and I don't know what to do

- Score: 768 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48434312) | Link: https://human-in-the-loop.bearblog.dev/llms-are-eroding-my-software-engineering-career-and-i-dont-know-what-to-do/

### TL;DR
A senior backend/fintech engineer describes how LLMs have rapidly eaten into his three hard-won moats: domain knowledge, debugging/distributed-systems skills, and code-quality/architecture “taste.” At his AI-embracing employer, agents now draft design docs, implement features, and one-shot most production bugs, making him feel like an interchangeable “LLM pilot” while hiring de-emphasizes domain specialization. HN replies split between “your expertise still matters, especially in high‑risk domains” and “models are improving so fast that most mid‑tier engineers will be squeezed out.”

---

### Comment pulse
- Domain expertise still matters → Current agents hallucinate regulations and misread complex requirements; compliant fintech and similar systems remain too risky to outsource.  
- Pace of change is scary → LLMs already ship MVPs; if hallucinations/compliance improve, hand-coding becomes niche—like mental arithmetic after calculators. — counterpoint: breakthroughs often hit S‑curves.  
- Where moats remain → Truly proprietary or novel domains, deep architecture, and long-term ownership still resist automation, but hiring is shifting toward “few top pilots + tools” models.
