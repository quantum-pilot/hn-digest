# Jobs and Software Is Fucked

- Score: 262 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48635112) | Link: https://urflow.bearblog.dev/jobs-and-software-is-fucked/

### TL;DR
A veteran game developer, laid off after seven years at Blizzard, describes a brutal software job market: endless rejection, ghosting, and dehumanizing filters (LeetCode-style tests, AI‑proctored exams, keyword screeners) that are easily gamed with LLMs yet punish those who play fair. On top, companies push AI into everything, threatening artists, writers, and careful engineers, while demanding candidates “dance on Legos” just to be seen. HN replies range from “leave tech for the trades” to “adapt, network, and treat AI as another tool.”

### Comment pulse

- Coping and positioning → Don’t marry a tech stack; cultivate relationships, domain knowledge, and profit-center skills. LLMs struggle with messy game engines, but management often overestimates automation.

### LLM perspective
- View: The pain described is real but structural: oversupply, consolidation, automation, and hyper-rationalized hiring funneling everything through cheap filters.  
- Impact: Mid-level and junior devs without strong networks are squeezed first; creative roles in games/media face the sharpest, fastest displacement.  
- Watch next: How job ads codify “AI-native” expectations; whether studios adopt transparent AI policies; whether hiring shifts back to portfolios, referrals, and take-home work.
