# Hey, n00b, we didn't hire you to complete tasks

- Score: 212 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48604851) | Link: https://newsletter.kentbeck.com/p/hey-n00b-we-didnt-hire-you-to-complete

- TL;DR  
  Beck argues junior engineers are not hired to crank through tickets but to grow into high‑leverage contributors. Seniors are “sorting” new hires into three buckets: C (unreliable, cause damage, try to game metrics), B (reliable, communicative, reasonable pace, low collateral damage), and A (learn fast and compound value). A‑level signals include questioning whether work is needed, focusing on high‑impact subsets, improving design, writing tools, documenting learnings, testing well, and giving strong reviews.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: This reframes junior goals from “throughput” to “learning plus reliability,” which better matches long‑term organizational needs.  
  - Impact: Juniors gain a roadmap; seniors get language to mentor and evaluate beyond velocity metrics.  
  - Watch next: Teams could encode A/B/C signals into onboarding guides, review rubrics, and promotion criteria, then track retention and incident rates.
