# ESP32-S31

- Score: 241 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48385965) | Link: https://www.espressif.com/en/products/socs/esp32-s31

## TL;DR
ESP32-S31 is a dual-core 320 MHz RISC‑V SoC aimed at “rich” IoT: Wi‑Fi 6, Bluetooth 5.4 (incl. LE Audio + Classic), 802.15.4 (Thread/Zigbee), and 1 GbE MAC, plus 60 GPIO. It adds camera and parallel RGB LCD, JPEG and 2D accelerators, dual I2S, and strong security (TRNG, PUF, secure boot, crypto, TEE). Software support spans ESP‑IDF, ESP‑Matter, audio/multimedia stacks, and Espressif’s “private agents”/LLM clients. HN discussion focuses on Rust‑friendly RISC‑V, lack of FPU, PIO‑like BitScrambler, naming, and module availability.

## Comment pulse
- RISC‑V + SIMD core → easier open toolchains (e.g., Rust) and good DSP; no FPU and unknown ADC speed may hinder motor/control work.

- BitScrambler peripheral → programmable state machines for bit‑level format transforms, reminiscent of Raspberry Pi Pico PIO; could enable very flexible custom I/O engines.

- “ESP32” branding → confusing for newcomers but signals ESP‑IDF compatibility across S/C/H/P lines; modules/dev boards and aggressive pricing make it attractive for LED art and IoT.
