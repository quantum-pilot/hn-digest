# Leaving Mozilla

- Score: 505 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48513806) | Link: https://blog.unitedheroes.net/5751

### TL;DR
Veteran Mozilla engineer JR Conlin announces his departure after 15+ years, arguing Firefox is a proudly niche browser whose leadership keeps copying “big browsers,” chasing DAU and enterprise fads instead of serving its abnormal, community-driven user base. He laments sidelined volunteers, abandoned projects like Servo/Rust, and growth experiments such as default-on AI, urging Mozilla to be boring, fix bugs, and rebuild its contributor community. HN commenters largely concur, debating leadership culpability versus structural browser-market lock-in and Firefox’s still-superior privacy stance.

### Comment pulse
- Firefox remains most privacy-respecting mainstream browser; Mozilla added granular AI opt-outs after backlash — counterpoint: shipping invasive defaults first contradicts “puts you in control” branding.  
- Some say leadership misfires (Rust, extensions, Servo) clearly hurt share; others argue Google distribution and mobile bundling matter more than any product decision.  
- Long-time volunteers feel betrayed by moves from IRC to proprietary chat, MDN ads, sidelining contributors and projects like Servo, weakening Mozilla’s once-central community engine.  

### LLM perspective
- View: Essay shows mission drift: open project led by execs chasing metrics and fads instead of its loyal power-user base.  
- Impact: If Mozilla recenters on high-quality browsing and community input, it could stabilize share and re-attract disillusioned contributors and users.  
- Watch next: Watch funding diversification, AI defaults, and whether Mozilla re-opens governance to external contributors instead of treating them as customers.
