# US Supreme Court rules geofence warrants require constitutional protections

- Score: 397 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48720924) | Link: https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/jun/29/supreme-court-geofence-warrants-case-decision

- TL;DR  
  Supreme Court held that police “geofence” demands for Google location histories are Fourth Amendment searches, requiring probable cause and particularity, not broad dragnets. The specific warrant used to identify bank-robbery suspect Okello Chatrie was unconstitutional because it swept in many bystanders and expanded in stages without individualized suspicion. However, the Court still allowed the evidence under the “good‑faith” exception, since officers relied on unsettled law. HN discussion centers on this remedy gap, broader surveillance implications, and the ideological split on the Court.
