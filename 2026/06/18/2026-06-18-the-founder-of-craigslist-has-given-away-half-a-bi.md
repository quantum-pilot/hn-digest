# The founder of Craigslist has given away half a billion dollars

- Score: 296 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48588216) | Link: https://www.independent.co.uk/us/money/craigslist-multimillionaire-craig-newmark-b2980681.html

### TL;DR
Craigslist founder Craig Newmark has already donated about $500M and pledged at least half his fortune, arguing that tech wealth is mostly luck and “too much money for anyone to have.” Shaped by Holocaust-survivor teachers and a lifelong bias toward modest living, he kept Craigslist simple, rejected VC “monetize everything” pressure, and funds journalism, cybersecurity, veterans, and even pigeon rescues. He’s alarmed by a new billionaire backlash against philanthropy, while Hacker News debates Craigslist’s social harms, scams, and dated product stewardship.

---

### Comment pulse
- Modest founder archetype → Many praise Newmark’s low-key lifestyle, simple SF office history, and “Buffett-like” refusal of status goods as unusually grounded tech wealth.  
- Platform harms and neglect → Critics say Craigslist enabled rental scams, trafficking, and gutted local classifieds, while dragging its feet on governance and product improvements.  
- Effort vs. responsibility → Some defend active moderation and note his anti-scam philanthropy; others argue fixing Craigslist itself would have outperformed donations in societal impact.

### LLM perspective
- View: Newmark exemplifies “earn a lot, live small, give big,” contrasting starkly with the status-obsessed tech-billionaire narrative.  
- Impact: His stance normalizes large-scale giving among non-billionaires and reframes anti-philanthropy rhetoric as a cultural rather than purely ideological shift.  
- Watch next: Scrutiny of philanthropy’s structure (foundations, control, transparency) and whether major platforms are judged on giving vs. how they run their core products.
