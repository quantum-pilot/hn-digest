# If you are asking for human attention, demonstrate human effort

- Score: 1723 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48497609) | Link: https://tombedor.dev/human-attention-and-human-effort/

## TL;DR
The post argues that if you want other humans to read something, you must show clear human effort: don’t just forward raw LLM output, label AI-generated text, and add your own synthesis or review first. As AI makes producing text and code cheap but reviewing it still expensive, dumping uncurated AI content signals disrespect for teammates’ time. HN commenters echo growing fatigue with AI-heavy PRs and documents, advocate “match others’ effort” norms, and warn that becoming a pure “LLM prompter” makes you easy to replace.

---

## Comment pulse
- AI-saturated PRs and docs → reviewers feel overwhelmed, disrespected, and start avoiding them; some suggest AI-based pre-review to filter junk first — counterpoint: others blame PR workflows that never scaled even for prolific humans.  
- Effort matching norm → give answers proportional to questioner’s effort; AI lowers creation cost but not verification cost, so low-effort AI use distorts team incentives and burns out conscientious reviewers.  
- Career and ethics tension → colleagues who outsource everything to LLMs look selfish and replaceable; contrasting voices use AI heavily but focus on thinking, planning, and helping others adopt tools.

## LLM perspective
- View: Treat reviewer attention as the primary scarce resource; etiquette and processes should optimize for that, not for raw output volume.  
- Impact: Teams will formalize rules: label AI content, pre-review your own AI output, limit PR scope, and penalize “spray-and-pray” AI usage.
