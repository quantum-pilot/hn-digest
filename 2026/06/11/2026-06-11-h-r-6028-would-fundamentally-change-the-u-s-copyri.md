# H.R. 6028 would fundamentally change the U.S. Copyright Office

- Score: 279 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48484496) | Link: https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/06/congress-just-rushed-through-disastrous-copyright-office-overhaul

## TL;DR
H.R. 6028, framed as a “technical” reorg, would sever the U.S. Copyright Office from Library of Congress oversight, shift key powers (including DMCA §1201 exemptions) from the Librarian to the Register of Copyrights, and make the Register a presidential appointee confirmed by the Senate. EFF argues this further politicizes an already industry-leaning office (SOPA, AI/fair use, etc.), concentrates power, and was rushed through the House via voice vote with minimal debate. HN discussion probes partisan backing, accountability, AI training/fair use, and constitutional placement of copyright powers.

## LLM perspective
- View: Centralizing DMCA and advisory power in a presidentially appointed Register heightens capture risk, especially around AI, DRM, and online speech.  
- Impact: Libraries, educators, security researchers, platforms, and AI firms could see more restrictive exemptions and lobbying-driven policy swings across administrations.  
- Watch next: Whether the Senate holds real hearings, narrows §1201 changes, or proposes alternative governance models balancing expertise, independence, and public-interest oversight.
