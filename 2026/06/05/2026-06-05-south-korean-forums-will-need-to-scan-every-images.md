# South Korean forums will need to scan every images with AI censorship tools

- Score: 205 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48406198) | Link: https://discuss.privacyguides.net/t/south-korean-online-communities-will-need-to-scan-every-images-with-ai-censorship-tools/38341

## TL;DR
South Korea is expanding its 2021 telecom law to require every domestic forum and community to AI-scan all uploaded images and videos for CSAM and illegal porn, using government-specified GPU hardware and vendor tools, with enforcement starting July 1. Critics say small sites can’t afford datacenter‑grade Nvidia boxes, models already over‑censor harmless content and political memes, and foreign platforms are exempt, framing it as corrupt, tech‑illiterate overreach. Defenders stress Korea’s severe deepfake/revenge‑porn crisis and different cultural weighting of privacy versus collective safety.

## Comment pulse
- Mandate entrenches a single vendor and local CMS ecosystem → tight deadline, CUDA/Nvidia requirement and SEED-like precedent suggest corruption and long-term technological stagnation.  
- Censorship seen as response to rampant deepfakes and revenge porn → some accept stronger surveillance, but critics note foreign platforms exempt, calling it “internet martial law.”  
- Technical design ridiculed → mandating Ubuntu 18.04 and single-GPU boxes seems unscalable, pushing users toward private invite-only or federated networks beyond Korean regulators’ reach.
