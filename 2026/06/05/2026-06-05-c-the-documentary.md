# C++: The Documentary

- Score: 364 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48408016) | Link: https://herbsutter.com/2026/06/04/c-the-documentary-released-today/

### TL;DR
C++: The Documentary is a feature-length oral history of the language, from Bell Labs “C with Classes” experiments through standardization, STL, commercial adoption, the early‑2000s “winter,” and the “modern C++” renaissance. Herb Sutter highlights C++ as a 40‑year success that’s again rapidly growing among top languages. Hacker News readers enjoy the storytelling and luminaries like Andrei Alexandrescu, while rehashing long‑running arguments over C++’s mounting complexity, its relationship to C, and whether safer successors like Rust should supplant it.

### Comment pulse
- Historical take: C++ rode C’s popularity; Zortech C++ on MS‑DOS gave critical mass; some say it also “saved” C from feature bloat.  
- Complexity fatigue: post‑C++98 features and dense guidebooks (e.g., Alexandrescu) empowered experts but left many preferring simpler stacks like Go or Java.  
- Security angst: critics argue C++’s opt‑in safety is untenable in an LLM‑aided exploit era—counterpoint: ecosystem lacks a universally viable, safer systems successor.
