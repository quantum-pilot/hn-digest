### TL;DR
People’s “oh shit” GenAI moments cluster around three themes: power, proximity, and risk. Power: local LLMs on consumer PCs answering questions, AIs weaponizing proof‑of‑concept exploits, and reverse‑engineering obscure synth protocols or bricked piano firmware in hours. Proximity: image/video‑based diagnosis that walks novices through furnace, HVAC, and appliance repairs; end‑to‑end planning for towing cars or evaluating real‑estate. Risk: dual‑use security implications, trivialized reverse engineering, and the persistent problem of highly confident but possibly wrong factual outputs.
