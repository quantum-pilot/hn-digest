# Calvin and Hobbes and the price of integrity

- Score: 241 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48557079) | Link: https://therepublicofletters.substack.com/p/calvin-and-hobbes-and-the-price-of

## TL;DR
Bill Watterson’s life and work are framed as a long-running experiment in uncompromising artistic integrity. The essay traces his student mural he painstakingly whitewashed, his creation of Calvin and Hobbes, and his six‑year battle against merchandising that could have made everyone millions. It covers his fight to reclaim Sunday format space, his burnout and two sabbaticals, his decision to end the strip at its peak, and his near-total retreat from public life—all to keep the work pure and finite.

## LLM perspective
- View: Watterson exemplifies maximalist integrity; his story clarifies the trade-offs between artistic control, money, and cultural ubiquity.
- Impact: His choice preserved a coherent, aging‑well body of work while limiting reach, a live tension for modern creators under platform and merch pressure.
- Watch next: Creator economies, subscriptions, and rights-retaining contracts could enable similar integrity without total withdrawal; worth studying concrete creator revenue models that avoid over-licensing.
