# Larry Ellison: "Citizens will be on their best behavior because we’re recording"

- Score: 283 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48373391) | Link: https://www.techradar.com/pro/quote-of-the-day-by-oracle-co-founder-larry-ellison-citizens-will-be-on-their-best-behavior-because-were-constantly-recording-and-reporting-everything-that-is-going-on-a-dire-warning-on-the-erosion-of-privacy

TL;DR
- Larry Ellison envisions AI continuously analyzing footage from pervasive cameras and auto‑reporting incidents, arguing this will keep “citizens on their best behavior.” TechRadar frames it as an Orwellian warning about AI-driven mass surveillance in streets and workplaces. Hacker News dives into how AI removes the labor bottleneck that once constrained surveillance, fundamentally shifting power dynamics and raising the question of whether such systems can coexist with democratic accountability, even if they sometimes bring concrete, localized benefits.

Comment pulse
- AI makes surveillance cheap and constant → behavior is scored; many urge “privacy for people, transparency for power” via auditable algorithms, body‑cams, and open procurement.
- AI traffic cameras in Bangladesh → commenters report improvement in road-law compliance and reduced corruption — counterpoint: others fear mission creep, errors, and targeting dissidents.
- Some note the quote came from a police body‑cam discussion → argue TechRadar’s headline omits context and Ellison implied mutual accountability, not endorsement of surveillance.

LLM perspective
- View: AI-enabled surveillance is structurally tempting to governments and firms; resisting it requires explicit legal constraints, not just norms.
- Impact: Without symmetry, expect chilling effects on speech, labor organizing, and dissent, even if nominal crime or accident rates improve.
- Watch next: rules on explainability, warrant requirements for bulk video search, and bans on workplace or public-space monitoring without oversight.
