# Gmail thinks I'm stupid, so I left

- Score: 547 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48375016) | Link: https://moddedbear.com/gmail-thinks-im-stupid-so-i-left

- TL;DR  
The author quit Gmail after 16 years because generative-AI features became intrusive and partly non‑disableable: auto-summaries, prewritten replies, constant “help me write” prompts, and “Tab to improve” hints that feel condescending and usage‑metrics driven. Rather than fight the UI, they moved to Fastmail with their own domain and are debating whether to import old mail or start fresh. HN commenters echo frustration with AI nagging, praise Fastmail’s speed and simplicity, and debate whether AI-written emails help or just waste attention.

- Comment pulse  
Gmail’s AI push is disrespectful → unsolicited summaries, giant suggested replies, and constant prompts feel like Google assumes users can’t communicate—counterpoint: helpful for non-native speakers or very weak writers.

Broader OS/app hostility → Chrome, Windows, and others now bombard users with AI and upsell popups; some suggest Linux desktops to escape forced “dark patterns.”
