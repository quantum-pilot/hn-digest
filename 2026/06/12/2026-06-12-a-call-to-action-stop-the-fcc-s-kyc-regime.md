# A Call to Action: Stop the FCC's KYC Regime

- Score: 330 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48504697) | Link: https://blog.lopp.net/call-to-action-stop-the-fcc-kyc-regime/

### TL;DR
The FCC is considering “Know Your Customer” rules that would require identity verification (e.g., government ID, address) for nearly all phone service, including prepaid “burner” SIMs. Jameson Lopp argues this creates a nationwide telecom identity registry that won’t meaningfully stop robocallers—who can easily use stolen identities—but will endanger privacy, chill anonymous speech, worsen SIM‑swap risk, and invite mission creep via law‑enforcement watchlists and long data retention. He urges targeted enforcement against high‑volume abusers instead, and calls for public comments opposing the proposal.

### Comment pulse
- Fix the root cause: end caller ID spoofing and properly deploy SHAKEN/STIR, rather than KYC every subscriber → reduces spam while preserving anonymity for legitimate blocked callers.  
- Phones already act as location trackers; tying them strongly to legal identity magnifies surveillance and future speech-control potential → some imagine keeping a “sacrificial” KYC phone that only forwards.  
- Telcos have poor breach histories; centralizing more PII is dangerous → some users want full traceability of callers for accountability — counterpoint: that excludes vulnerable people needing anonymous communication.

### LLM perspective
- View: This is a classic security-theater vs civil-liberties tradeoff; identity mandates rarely stop serious abusers but reliably centralize power and data.  
- Impact: Most on vulnerable users (DV survivors, activists, journalists) and on overall SIM-swap risk; also entrenches telecoms as de facto identity brokers.  
- Watch next: Final FCC rule text, law-enforcement lobbying, SHAKEN/STIR effectiveness data, and whether major carriers or tech platforms publicly oppose broad KYC requirements.
