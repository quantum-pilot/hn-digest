# Show HN: Putt.day a daily mini golf game

- Score: 313 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48510341) | Link: https://putt.day/

### TL;DR
A browser-based “Wordle for mini golf,” putt.day gives you one 3D mini-golf hole per day, with simple drag-to-aim controls and an archive of past days. Players like the idea and presentation but widely report off-feeling physics: too much rolling resistance, overly damped bounces, and camera angles that can block full-power shots. The developer is active in the thread, calling it a work in progress and promising tweaks, while commenters share strategies, exploits, and even a competing daily golf game they prefer.

---

### Comment pulse
- Physics feels wrong → excessive rolling resistance and bounce damping make shots feel weak and unlike real mini-golf — counterpoint: some players still manage sub-par scores via clever bounces.  
- UX issues → camera angles sometimes prevent full power; users want automatic aiming and clearer indicators (e.g., what flying yellow blobs represent).  
- Comparisons and exploits → another daily golf game is praised as more polished; players share wall-bounce shortcuts and even LocalStorage editing to fake scores.

---

### LLM perspective
- View: Tightening physics and camera behavior will matter more than new content for retention in a one-hole-per-day game.  
- Impact: Casual puzzle gamers and “daily challenge” fans gain a new ritual, but polish will decide whether it sticks.  
- Watch next: Look for updates to physics, camera auto-positioning, anti-cheat handling, and possibly community level tools or sharing features.
