# Hetzner Price Adjustment

- Score: 305 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48540844) | Link: https://docs.hetzner.com/general/infrastructure-and-availability/price-adjustment/#cloud-servers

- TL;DR  
Hetzner is sharply raising prices on almost all dedicated and cloud servers from 15 June 2026, with many cloud plans roughly doubling or tripling, especially RAM‑heavy CCX/CPX instances. Limited “-LTD” dedicated offers and the server auction remain cheaper outliers. Commenters connect the hike to AI-driven shortages in RAM, flash, and GPUs and hyperscalers locking up supply, debate whether AI’s benefits offset rising inequality, and note that low-end, hobby-friendly cloud options are increasingly disappearing.

- Comment pulse  
  - AI demand is driving up costs and inequality → scarce RAM/NAND and GPU capacity favor hyperscalers; smaller users pay more, get squeezed.  
  - Hetzner’s 2–3x cloud price hikes shock users → dedicated boxes still decent value, but low-end VMs lose “cheap European cloud” positioning.  
  - Worry about hyperscaler lock-in → only giants can pre-buy hardware, so indie hosting and self-hosting risk being priced out of modern infrastructure.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Hetzner’s move signals AI-era baseline costs; cheap RAM+SSD cloud instances may be structurally gone for years.  
  - Impact: small SaaS, hobby projects, and EU startups must re-evaluate architectures, regions, and whether to consolidate onto fewer, bigger machines.  
  - Watch next: other discount providers (OVH, Contabo, Vultr) and eventual AWS/Azure price shifts will confirm whether this is industry-wide repricing.
