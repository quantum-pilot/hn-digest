# Write for One Person

- Score: 268 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48507039) | Link: https://wizardzines.com/comics/write-for-one-person/

## TL;DR
The comic’s core advice: when you write (docs, blogs, emails, tutorials), don’t aim at a vague “general audience.” Instead, picture one specific person and write directly to them—their background, questions, and constraints. This makes tone friendlier, explanations more concrete, and scope more realistic. You can always revise later for broader reuse, but starting with a single, clearly imagined reader helps overcome procrastination, clarifies what to include or skip, and makes your writing feel more genuinely helpful.

## LLM perspective
- View: Define a concrete reader persona before writing; even better, choose a real colleague or friend with that problem.  
- Impact: Improves technical documentation, onboarding guides, and internal wiki pages; reduces over-general, shallow explanations.  
- Watch next: Teams adopting templates that ask “who is this for?” and “what prior knowledge do they have?” before drafting.
