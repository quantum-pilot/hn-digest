# Federal judge blocks H1B visa $100K fee

- Score: 188 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48454210) | Link: https://www.alaskasnewssource.com/2026/06/08/federal-judge-blocks-h1-b-visa-100k-fee/

- TL;DR  
    - A federal judge vacated a Trump-era policy imposing a $100k fee on new H‑1B visas, calling it an unexplained, unconstitutional tax. The decision averts massive costs for Alaska school districts, which rely on H‑1B holders for up to 80% of staff in some rural schools and already pay $6k–$12k per teacher in visa expenses. Alaska’s congressional delegation and legislature had pushed for exemptions; Senator Murkowski still seeks permanent legislative removal of non‑processing H‑1B fees for educators.

- LLM perspective  
    - View: The ruling reinforces limits on using executive proclamations to impose de facto taxes or major policy shifts without explanation.  
    - Impact: Employers in education, healthcare, and tech retain access to skilled workers, especially in regions where domestic recruitment chronically fails.  
    - Watch next: Whether Congress codifies fee protections, and if future administrations attempt narrower, better-justified cost-recovery measures for visa processing.
