# Amateur may have cracked Linear A

- Score: 396 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48600107) | Link: https://aiclambake.com/clamtakes/linear-a/

### TL;DR
An AI engineer and amateur linguist, Tom Di Mino, claims to have deciphered Linear A, the undecoded Bronze Age Minoan script, arguing it encodes an extinct Semitic language ancestral to Hebrew. His key breakthrough comes from a recurring “libation formula” prayer, where he assigns a sound to a Linear A–only sign, reconstructing a Semitic root meaning “to dwell” and aligning the text with later Hebrew-style prayers. Using Python tools built with Claude Code, he proposes readings for 40 signs, a 408-word lexicon, and even corrections to Linear B—impressive but still unverified work that HN commenters greet with informed skepticism, mainly due to the tiny, fragmentary corpus and prior failed Semitic hypotheses.
