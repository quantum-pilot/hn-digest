# How Japan's railways stayed one while splitting apart

- Score: 180 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48570730) | Link: https://arun.is/blog/jr-logo/

### TL;DR
Japan’s national railway was broken up in 1987 into seven private JR companies, yet it still feels like one unified system. That’s largely due to a deliberately shared identity: a single JR logo, color system, and even redesigned railway kanji, all created in just 124 days by Nippon Design Center. The logo’s robustness, instant nationwide rollout, and grounding in deeper “mind/behavior/visual” corporate identity theory explain why it’s never been redesigned—unlike other privatized giants such as Japan Airlines. HN comments focus on JR Pass economics, delightful UX details, and cultural trade-offs.
