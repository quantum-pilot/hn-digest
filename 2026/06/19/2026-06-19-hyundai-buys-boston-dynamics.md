# Hyundai buys Boston Dynamics

- Score: 601 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48600312) | Link: https://startupfortune.com/hyundai-takes-full-control-of-boston-dynamics-as-softbank-exits-for-325-million/

### TL;DR
Hyundai is buying SoftBank’s remaining 9.65% stake in Boston Dynamics for $325m, taking full control after its 2021 majority purchase. The move aligns Boston Dynamics’ Atlas humanoid program tightly with Hyundai’s EV factories, where Atlas is slated to start real work in Georgia by 2028, focusing first on parts sequencing. Commenters debate why the valuation looks modest versus Tesla’s robotics hype, and whether humanoid robots are justified versus bespoke automation for the “long tail” of still‑manual factory tasks.

### Comment pulse
- Hyundai already owned 80%; this final 9.65% is SoftBank exercising a put, seen as cheap versus frothy robotics valuations like Tesla or SpaceX-adjacent companies.  
- Critics question humanoid robots for manufacturing, preferring task-specific machines—counterpoint: legacy plants, human-shaped spaces, and dexterous “long tail” tasks favor general-purpose, human-scale embodiments.  
- Some see Hyundai’s bet as broader than cars: a hedge against South Korea’s shrinking workforce and a platform for general-purpose service and industrial robots.
