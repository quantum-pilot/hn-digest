# I'm Eric Ries, author of "The Lean Startup" and new book "Incorruptible" – AMA

- Score: 798 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48477135) | Link: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48477135

### TL;DR
Eric Ries’s new book “Incorruptible” argues that corporate “corruption” and enshittification are driven less by bad people than by structural incentives—especially public-market pressure for short‑term gains. He contrasts typical firms (which fail at succession and drift from their mission) with rare cases like Costco, Patagonia, and Novo Nordisk that deliberately engineer governance, ownership, and business models to resist this gravity. HN discussion probes whether structure really beats leadership, how broadly corruption patterns generalize, and whether financial markets themselves can be reformed.
