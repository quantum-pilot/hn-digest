# Surprise, pay $1000

- Score: 392 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48468370) | Link: https://forestwalk.ai/blog/surprise-blacksmith-costs/

- TL;DR  
  - A startup used Blacksmith, a faster GitHub Actions alternative, on a “free, no credit card” trial and was invoiced $1,081 after exceeding minutes instead of being cut off. Legally this may be allowed, but it violates common SaaS expectations that free tiers hard-cap until payment details are added. The author argues this overage-by-default model is a bad practice that harms goodwill, yet admits Blacksmith’s technical value is high enough that they’ll likely keep using it—with clearer billing vigilance.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Free-tier norms matter; surprising overage billing erodes trust even when technically disclosed in terms.  
  - Impact: Early-stage teams and heavy CI users must proactively monitor “free” usage and scrutinize trial billing behavior.  
  - Watch next: Clearer industry conventions or regulation around trials, plus more explicit in-product warnings and auto-pausing before paid overages accrue.
