# The case against geometric algebra (2024)

- Score: 116 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48617782) | Link: https://alexkritchevsky.com/2024/02/28/geometric-algebra.html

- TL;DR  
  Geometric Algebra (GA) is framed as a social movement built on Clifford and exterior algebras. It usefully promotes multivectors and wedge products, but the author argues its core dogma—the geometric product and mixed-grade multivectors—lacks clear geometric meaning, conflates objects with operators, and adds complexity without insight compared to exterior algebra and operator-based formulations. He also criticizes GA culture as overzealous and crackpot-attracting, which some HN commenters see as unfairly ad hominem, while others—especially practitioners and a few GA researchers—agree that foundations, conventions, and pedagogy around GA remain messy and underspecified.

- Comment pulse  
  - Critics say the piece is too ad hominem, straw-mans GA; want concrete mathematical failure, not sociology — counterpoint: defenders cite GP/mixed-grade arguments as real flaws.  
  - Math/physics commenters: GA’s unified Maxwell-equation, symmetry stories interesting, but differential forms/antisymmetric tensors already give clearer gauge structure and are standard.  
  - Practitioners: PGA/GA can simplify real robotics/graphics problems and tutorials are valuable, yet foundations (duals, metrics, units) feel inconsistent and users hit conceptual walls.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Treat GA as one coordinate system, not a revolution; emphasize exterior algebra and explicit operators over geometric-product centrality.  
  - Impact: Physicists, graphics engineers, and students benefit if textbooks separate multivectors-as-objects from multivectors-as-transformations and clarify units, duals, and metrics.  
  - Watch next: Compare GA-based libraries against quaternion/matrix methods on robustness, performance, pedagogy; standardize conventions for duals, degeneracies, and dimensional analysis.
