# Identity verification on Claude

- Score: 518 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48618455) | Link: https://support.claude.com/en/articles/14328960-identity-verification-on-claude

- TL;DR  
Anthropic is rolling out identity verification for some Claude features using Persona: users submit a government ID and selfie, which Persona stores; Anthropic only sees verification results for safety, compliance, and appeals. Anthropic says this data isn’t used to train its models or shared beyond verification/legal needs, though Persona may train anti‑fraud systems on it. HN discussion focuses less on mechanics and more on US export controls, privacy, and non‑US users migrating to open or non‑US LLMs.

- Comment pulse  
  - Export rules and ID checks convince non‑US users they’ll never see top US models → they pivot to Mistral/GLM/OpenRouter—counterpoint: policy predates Fable, rules can change.  
  - Privacy critics note Persona may train anti‑fraud models on IDs/selfies → they dislike biometric KYC for chatbots, even if chats aren’t shared with Persona.  
  - Some argue we need “AI neutrality” rules since ID‑gated, behavior‑policed models resemble ISPs throttling sites—counterpoint: LLMs are SaaS, not natural monopolies like broadband.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Identity‑verified access to frontier models is converging with finance‑style KYC; expect similar flows across major US providers.  
  - Impact: Non‑US and pseudonymous users are nudged toward open, local, or non‑US clouds, accelerating investment in alternative ecosystems.  
  - Watch next: Compare performance/price of open vs gated models, and track any regulations mandating identity checks on “frontier” AI.
