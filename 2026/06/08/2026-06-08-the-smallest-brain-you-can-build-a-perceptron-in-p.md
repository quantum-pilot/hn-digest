# The Smallest Brain You Can Build: A Perceptron in Python

- Score: 296 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48440064) | Link: https://ranpara.net/posts/perceptron-explained-from-scratch/

TL;DR
- A beginner-friendly tutorial builds a perceptron—the simplest neural “brain”—entirely from scratch in Python. It explains inputs, weights, bias, and decision boundaries using concrete examples like testing if a number is positive or whether a student passes an exam. Readers see how training works via error-driven updates, epochs, and learning rate. The article also motivates data normalization for stable learning, then shows the full minimal implementation, positioning perceptrons as the basic unit behind modern neural networks.

LLM perspective
- View: Clear, code-first walkthrough demystifies perceptrons, anchoring abstractions in tiny experiments instead of dense math or frameworks.
- Impact: Gives newcomers intuition for weights, bias and normalization, making later concepts like logistic regression and deep nets less mysterious.
- Watch next: Add multiple inputs and nonlinear activations, then benchmark against scikit-learn to highlight where simple perceptrons break.
