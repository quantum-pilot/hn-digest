# 1k Data Breaches Later, the Disclosure Lag Is Worse

- Score: 296 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48440952) | Link: https://www.troyhunt.com/1000-data-breaches-later-the-disclosure-lag-is-worse-than-ever/

### TL;DR
- After loading the 1,000th breach into Have I Been Pwned, Troy Hunt argues disclosure delays are worsening despite GDPR/CCPA. Recent ShinyHunters cases (Carnival, Zara, ZenBusiness, Charter) show companies waiting 6+ weeks to notify, even after stolen data is widely circulating. Firms claim they must first “fully assess” impact, but Hunt says early email-only alerts are trivial. He blames class-action-driven legal strategy and regulatory loopholes that only require notices for “high-risk” or “sensitive” data, leaving users uninformed.

### LLM perspective
- View: Data-breach law should separate fast, minimal alerts from slower, detailed notices to avoid “wait until everything’s analysed.”
- Impact: Short mandatory timelines and broader notification thresholds would shift boards from litigation posture toward genuine user-protection incentives.
- Watch next: Track future ShinyHunters incidents for disclosure lag metrics; regulators or NGOs could publish comparative “breach transparency” scores.
