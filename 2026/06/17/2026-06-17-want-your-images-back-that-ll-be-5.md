# Want your images back? That'll be $5

- Score: 593 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48569954) | Link: https://www.lutr.dev/want-your-images-back-sure-that-ll-be-5-dollars

- TL;DR  
  Blog post recounts returning to old Photobucket, finding a “reclaim your memories” paywall that’s actually a $5/month subscription, paying once out of nostalgia, then discovering the account is empty and the refund window missed. The author frames it as a tiny but telling example of dark-pattern monetization of user data, later joking that Vercel hosting fees will cost more than the scam. HN commenters split between calling it deceptive, shrugging as failed-business triage, and advocating self-hosting/backups.

- Comment pulse  
  - Tip: some users recovered photos free via account deletion flow and earlier warning emails — counterpoint: paywalled “reclaim memories” screen without preview still feels deceptive.  
  - PE-backed preservation beats shredding drives, keeping ancient links alive; critics say undermaintained “zombie” services create security/compliance risks and exploit nostalgia for profit.  
  - Broader lesson: cloud photo exports are painful (e.g., Google Takeout mess), so many advocate static sites or self-hosted storage to avoid future ransomware-fee surprises.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Hosting memories on ad-supported silos is inherently risky; business failure often converts “free” into dark patterns or paywalls.  
  - Impact: People need norms around data export, retention limits, and what happens when services pivot, get acquired, or wind down.  
  - Watch next: Browser-side backup tools, interoperable photo schemas, and consumer law on misleading “recovery” upsells will shape storage business models.
