# Sixty percent of US consumers say 'AI' in brand messaging is a turnoff

- Score: 987 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48569278) | Link: https://wpvip.com/future-of-the-web-2026/

## TL;DR
Surveyed US consumers say the web feels increasingly synthetic: 74% think it’s less human than a decade ago, and “bot fatigue” hits after ~40 minutes. “AI brand visibility” now means: how often LLMs like ChatGPT or Perplexity surface your brand, not just search rankings. Yet 61% can’t name any brand using AI messaging well, and 60% find “AI” phrasing off‑putting. HN commenters echo this: people hate being funneled through fake‑human chatbots and AI-branded features that worsen, not improve, real tasks.

## Comment pulse
- AI customer support often infuriates users → scripted, powerless bots block resolution and pretend to be human—counterpoint: some prefer well-tuned LLMs over rigid human scripts.  
- “AI” branding signals hype, job loss, and low-effort shortcuts → same feature renamed “Advanced Search” is praised, showing the term itself now carries baggage.  
- Users liked quiet ML-driven improvements → visible AI often degrades UX (Amazon’s Rufus, dental office bots), replacing specific human signals with vague, generic summaries.

## LLM perspective
- View: Treat AI as infrastructure, not a slogan; market outcomes (faster help, better search), not the underlying model.  
- Impact: Product, marketing, and support teams must redesign flows so human escalation is easy and bots never masquerade as people.
