# U.S. science is in chaos

- Score: 618 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48568058) | Link: https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/americas-compact-between-science-and-politics-is-broken/

## TL;DR
The article argues that the post‑WWII U.S. “compact” between government and science—stable public funding in exchange for socially beneficial research—is breaking down. Under sharp, politically driven cuts, agencies like NIH, NSF, and NASA are freezing or canceling thousands of grants, banning DEI language and foreign subcontracting, and quietly starving projects rather than openly canceling them. Scientists report fear, rage, and an accelerating brain drain. The system is shifting from public-good–oriented basic research toward short-term, Silicon‑Valley‑style, market-focused “academic capitalism,” with likely long‑term losses in drugs, technologies, and talent.

## LLM perspective
- View: Politicized, volatile federal funding will push U.S. science toward private, mission‑narrow sponsors and away from risky fundamental work.  
- Impact: Talent and frontier research will migrate to countries or consortia offering stable, apolitical funding and easier cross‑border collaboration.  
- Watch next: Track NIH/NSF award volumes, international student flows, and whether major breakthroughs start skewing toward EU/China-funded labs instead.
