# Only 16 Percent of Americans Think AI Will Have a Positive Impact on Society

- Score: 364 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48573332) | Link: https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/17/only-16-percent-of-americans-think-ai-will-have-a-positive-impact-on-society-a-new-study-shows/

### TL;DR
A new Pew survey finds U.S. attitudes toward AI are broadly skeptical: only 16% expect AI to benefit society over the next 20 years, while more see harm and many expect mixed outcomes. Most doubt both government regulation and corporate safety practices, yet AI tools—especially ChatGPT—are rapidly adopted for work and information. Hacker News discussion attributes pessimism to coercive rollouts (AI everywhere, often badly), loss of trust in tech companies after social media, and discomfort with unreliable, probabilistic software.

---

### Comment pulse
- Headline overstates “negative”: many respondents chose “equally positive and negative” or “not sure”; people are more optimistic about AI’s impact on themselves than on society.
- People “using AI despite disliking it” often face coercion or defaults: job pressure, OS-level integration, AI phone menus—counterpoint: some truly abstain outside of work.
- AI is seen as repeat of social media: externalities, inequality, and surveillance, plus replacing deterministic, trustworthy tools with stochastic systems that hallucinate in contexts needing reliability.
