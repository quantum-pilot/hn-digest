## TL;DR
- Many commenters resonate with the essay’s claim that older programmers may be the last to deeply understand the whole stack, as computing jumps from deterministic, inspectable layers to opaque AI-driven abstractions. They describe losing “pushback” from systems, fragile AI-generated code, and younger users lacking conceptual models beyond polished apps. Others argue this is a familiar generational shift: specialization always pruned low-level knowledge, and tools like LLMs can also teach, not just replace, real understanding—if used intentionally.
