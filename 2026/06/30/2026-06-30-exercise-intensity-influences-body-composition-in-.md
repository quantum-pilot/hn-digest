# Exercise intensity influences body composition in healthy older adults (2025)

- Score: 192 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48730694) | Link: https://www.maturitas.org/article/S0378-5122(25)00571-7/fulltext

### TL;DR
A six‑month study of healthy adults around 72 compared low- and moderate-intensity treadmill walking to high‑intensity intervals. All groups lost similar fat, but HIIT participants maintained more muscle, suggesting intensity shapes body composition even without weightlifting. Hacker News commenters stress that HIIT can mimic light strength work for beginners, yet isn’t a substitute for resistance training, may not generalize to younger or heavier people, and carries higher overuse and cardiac risks without warm-up, recovery, and periodization.
