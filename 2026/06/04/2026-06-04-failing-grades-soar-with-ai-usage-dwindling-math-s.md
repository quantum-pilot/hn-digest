# Failing grades soar with AI usage, dwindling math skills in Berkeley CS classes

- Score: 721 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48392004) | Link: https://www.dailycal.org/news/campus/academics/failing-grades-soar-as-professors-see-greater-ai-usage-dwindling-math-skills-in-uc-berkeley/article_16fad0bf-02cb-4b8c-8d88-888ffd9f8608.html

### TL;DR
Spring 2026 fail rates in key UC Berkeley CS courses spiked far above department norms (CS 10: 35% F, CS 61A: 11%; average GPA ~2.3 vs 2.8–3.3 guideline). Professors attribute this to heavy, often undisclosed reliance on LLMs for homework, weakened math preparation (exacerbated by test‑optional admissions and AI‑permissive prerequisite courses), fewer TAs, and collapsing engagement/office‑hour attendance. They’re moving toward clear grade thresholds, more remediation, and explicit expectations that students struggle productively instead of outsourcing thinking to AI.
