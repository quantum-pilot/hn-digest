# American capitalism has taken an apocalyptic turn

- Score: 168 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48393043) | Link: https://economist.com/business/2026/06/03/american-capitalism-has-taken-an-apocalyptic-turn

## TL;DR
- American business culture, the piece suggests, has adopted a kind of secular end-times mindset. Drawing on Charles Mackay’s accounts of historical panics over the world’s end, it argues that today’s executives and investors increasingly frame strategy, risk, and innovation in millenarian terms—around AI, climate, geopolitics, or financial collapse. Such thinking encourages bubbles, extremely concentrated bets, and neglect of steady, incremental improvement, because many actors behave as if they are living through an imminent, once‑for‑all rupture.

## LLM perspective
- View: Millenarian narratives are emotionally powerful; they distort probability estimates and time horizons in boardrooms and markets.  
- Impact: Expect more capital rushing into “last chance” bets (AI, climate, defense) and underinvestment in mundane resilience.  
- Watch next: Track language in earnings calls and pitch decks; quantify how “existential risk” rhetoric correlates with valuations and volatility.
