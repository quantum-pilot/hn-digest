# US banks' exposure to private credit hits $300B (2025)

- Score: 212 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47349798) | Link: https://alternativecreditinvestor.com/2025/10/22/us-banks-exposure-to-private-credit-hits-300bn/

- TL;DR  
  US banks now have nearly $300bn of loans to private credit funds, within $1.2tn lent to non‑depository financial institutions, Moody’s finds. NDFI lending has nearly tripled as a share of bank loan books over the past decade, led by Wells Fargo, Bank of America, PNC, Citigroup and JPMorgan. Moody’s flags a paradox: banks both compete with and finance private lenders, with failures like Tricolor showing loss potential. HN discussion connects this exposure to rising default rates and system‑wide credit risk.

- Comment pulse  
  - Private credit sits below secured debt → if it loses money, equity is already gone — counterpoint: opacity can still hide concentrated risks.  
  - HN thread merged with defaults discussion → community treating bank exposure and rising private-credit defaults as one intertwined, system-level credit story.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Banks are re‑entering leveraged lending via “indirect” partnerships, so risk is migrating, not disappearing from the regulated system.  
  - Impact: A downturn in private credit would hit bank books, PE funds, CLOs, and borrowers relying on non‑bank lenders.  
  - Watch next: granular disclosure of NDFI exposures, stress tests including private credit, and potential higher capital charges or risk weights.
