# US private credit defaults hit record 9.2% in 2025, Fitch says

- Score: 214 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47349806) | Link: https://www.marketscreener.com/news/us-private-credit-defaults-hit-record-9-2-in-2025-fitch-says-ce7e5fd8df8fff2d

### TL;DR
Fitch reports record 9.2% default rates in 2025 among US mid‑market corporate borrowers in private credit, up from 8.1% in 2024. These mostly small, highly leveraged firms use floating‑rate loans tied to still‑elevated Fed rates, with little interest‑rate hedging, leaving cashflows exposed. Defaults include bankruptcies and distressed exchanges; software borrowers, though central to private credit, show no recorded defaults due to sector classification quirks. HN commenters focus on leveraged buyouts, bank exposure, “extend‑and‑pretend” loss‑masking, and whether emerging problems could become systemic.

### Comment pulse
- Private equity LBO model loads companies with acquisition debt, driving price hikes and cuts; if deals sour, banks rush to offload exposure, not run firms.  
- Bank exposure is sizable, prompting fears of moral hazard and QE‑backed bailouts — counterpoint: others expect contained losses, more like a sector shakeout.  
- Lenders allegedly play extend‑and‑pretend to avoid write‑downs while products push private credit into 401(k)s; limited ways to short these risks frustrate skeptics.  

### LLM perspective
- View: This looks like an overdue repricing of risky, floating‑rate mid‑market credit rather than an immediate, system‑wide credit crisis.  
- Impact: Borrowers face tighter terms and more equity; weaker funds may close or merge as defaults expose poor underwriting.  
- Watch next: Fed rate path, bank disclosures on private‑credit exposures, and whether retirement products accumulate the riskiest slices of loans.
