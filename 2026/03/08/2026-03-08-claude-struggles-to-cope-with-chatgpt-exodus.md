# Claude struggles to cope with ChatGPT exodus

- Score: 138 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47298997) | Link: https://www.forbes.com/sites/barrycollins/2026/03/06/claude-struggles-to-cope-with-chatgpt-exodus/

- TL;DR  
Anthropic’s Claude chatbot has seen a huge influx of users after OpenAI signed a broad AI contract with the renamed U.S. Department of War, sparking a boycott and “ChatGPT exodus.” The surge pushed Claude to the top of the U.S. App Store but also caused several multi‑hour outages across its app, API, and coding tools as Anthropic scrambles to scale infrastructure. Meanwhile, the Pentagon labeled Anthropic a “supply‑chain risk,” and Hacker News debates AI commoditization, surveillance ethics, and model loyalty.

- Comment pulse  
  - AI vendors are interchangeable → switching from OpenAI to Claude took minutes; moats look like compute, uptime, and workflow tooling—counterpoint: only 3–4 serious players may survive.  
  - OpenAI’s “no domestic surveillance of U.S. persons” line alarms non‑Americans → reads as tacit permission to surveil everyone else amid existing intelligence‑sharing pacts.  
  - Users hop between ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude with little attachment → choices driven by features (multimodal vs coding), UX (Cowork, GUIs), rate limits, and perceived “personality.”

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Reliability and rate‑limits will increasingly matter more than tiny quality differences between frontier models.  
  - Impact: Startups and enterprises will design for provider fungibility, using abstractions to keep bargaining power.  
  - Watch next: Multi‑provider orchestration tools, clearer defense/ethics policies, and which smaller labs can fund enough compute to stay competitive.
