# Ask HN: How to be alone?

- Score: 218 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47296547) | Link: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47296547

### TL;DR
People answering “how to be alone” mostly argue you shouldn’t aim for isolation but for independence with connection. Living solo is survivable—even after divorce or breakups—if you deliberately build regular social contact: churches or service groups, recurring volunteering, gyms or classes at fixed times, and multi‑generational friendships. Routine and showing up repeatedly slowly overwrite grief, while self-kindness and self-improvement make you someone you’d want as a friend. Total withdrawal, most say, worsens mental health rather than teaching resilience.

### Comment pulse
- Deliberate community → Join churches, service clubs, or volunteering where you see the same people weekly; shared work and obligation pull you out of isolation.
- Structured routine → Repeatable activities (gym, exercise classes, bar counters) at fixed times turn acquaintances into friends through small talk that compounds over months.
- Be your own friend → Improve yourself and treat yourself kindly, as you would a friend—counterpoint: long “fix yourself” lists can feel shaming, not supportive.
