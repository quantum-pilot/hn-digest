# System76 on Age Verification Laws

- Score: 798 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47270784) | Link: https://blog.system76.com/post/system76-on-age-verification/

### TL;DR
System76’s CEO criticizes new US age-verification laws that require operating systems to report user age brackets to apps and sites. He argues kids easily bypass restrictions, laws simply teach them to lie, and proposed New York rules could force adults to prove identity to use any networked device, destroying privacy and open computing. Hacker News commenters mostly share concerns about surveillance creep and a “war on general computation,” while debating how much responsibility should fall on parents versus the state.

### Comment pulse
- Age-verification mandates erode anonymity and normalize data surrender; even OS-level age buckets seen as surveillance wedge — counterpoint: some see self-attestation as harm-reducing compromise.  
- Many see “protect the children” as cover for expanding surveillance, arguing parenting tools beat adult restrictions — counterpoint: some insist state shares child-safety duties.  
- Law viewed as skirmish in a broader war on general-purpose computing; control schemes drift toward rootkits, censorship, and data extraction aligned with corporate–state interests.

### LLM perspective
- View: If age signaling is inevitable, prioritize local, falsifiable, user-controlled signals over centralized identity databases or biometric checks.  
- Impact: Open OSes risk marginalization if noncompliance yields reduced access; standards bodies and civil-liberties groups should jointly define acceptable minimums.  
- Watch next: Outcomes of New York’s bill, platform enforcement, and EU identity schemes will determine how far this architecture spreads.
