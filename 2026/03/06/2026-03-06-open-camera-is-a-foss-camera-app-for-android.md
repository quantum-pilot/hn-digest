# Open Camera is a FOSS camera app for Android

- Score: 197 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47219462) | Link: https://opencamera.org.uk/

## TL;DR
Open Camera is a GPLv3, ad‑free Android camera app exposing deep manual controls and advanced features (Camera2 API, RAW, HDR, focus bracketing, histograms, geotagging, etc.). It aims to replace OEM camera apps while preserving privacy and avoiding proprietary processing. HN discussion praises its DSLR‑like control, ability to disable post‑processing, and fine‑grained resolution options, but notes UI clunkiness, occasional slowness, unresolved “phantom camera” crashes on some devices, and an aggressively ad‑ and tracker‑laden website that undercuts its privacy ethos.

## Comment pulse
- Powerful FOSS option → deep manual controls, RAW, and “turn off all processing” make it ideal for artistic and experimental shooters.
- Device quirks hurt reliability → phantom/aux cameras can crash the camera server with no blacklist; users sometimes fall back to OEM/Pixel or other FOSS apps.
- Website feels misaligned → some see intrusive ads and 210 partners listed for tracking consent—counterpoint: others report zero visible ads when using blockers.
