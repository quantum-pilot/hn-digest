# Iran War Cost Tracker

- Score: 266 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47237080) | Link: https://iran-cost-ticker.com

### TL;DR
A live “Iran War Cost Tracker” estimates U.S. taxpayer spending for Operation Epic Fury using a bottom‑up daily burn model: roughly $380M/day for initial strikes, then $220M/day and $155M/day in later, less intense phases. It itemizes personnel, ships, aircraft, fuel, munitions, C4ISR, and a large “overhead” bucket to reflect hidden and classified costs, while explicitly excluding long‑term veteran care, broader economic disruption, and environmental damage. Comparisons to post‑9/11 wars and social programs highlight the vast opportunity cost. HN debates marginal vs sunk costs, missing data, geopolitical fallout, and whether “how will we pay for it?” is really about politics, not affordability.

### Comment pulse
- Incremental vs baseline cost → Some argue many forces are paid for anyway; others say you must count full capability cost over decades of preparation.  
- Missing or fuzzy numbers → Interceptor use, true ordnance volumes, and alleged $1B radar hits are uncertain; commenters doubt the tracker’s precision and some media claims.  
- Opportunity cost framing → War costs contrasted with school lunches; economists note states aren’t households and deficits are political choices, not strict financial limits.
