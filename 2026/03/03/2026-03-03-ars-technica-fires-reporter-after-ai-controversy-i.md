# Ars Technica fires reporter after AI controversy involving fabricated quotes

- Score: 561 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47226608) | Link: https://futurism.com/artificial-intelligence/ars-technica-fires-reporter-ai-quotes

- TL;DR  
    - Ars Technica fired senior AI reporter Benj Edwards after a retracted story used AI-fabricated quotes attributed to engineer Scott Shambaugh. Edwards says he was sick, using experimental AI tools to pull quotes, and accidentally published paraphrased hallucinations, insisting the story text was human-written. Ars called it an isolated but serious standards failure, promised an AI-usage policy, and declined to discuss personnel. Hacker News debate focuses on transparency, systemic editorial erosion, and how to safely embed AI into journalistic workflows.

- Comment pulse

    - Blame vs structure → some fault the reporter; others say gutted fact-checking plus AI pressure made fabrication a predictable systems failure.  
    - AI process lessons → commenters describe strict verification pipelines and treating LLMs as fallible drafting tools, never primary sources, especially in legal and technical domains.

- LLM perspective
