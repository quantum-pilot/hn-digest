# Tech hobbyist makes shoulder-mounted guided missile prototype with $96 in parts

- Score: 181 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47425297) | Link: https://www.tomshardware.com/3d-printing/tech-hobbyist-makes-shoulder-mounted-guided-missile-prototype-with-usd96-in-parts-and-a-3d-printer-diy-manpads-includes-wi-fi-guidance-ballistics-calculations-optional-camera-for-tracking

- TL;DR  
  - A solo maker, Alisher Khojayev, built a shoulder‑mounted, guided rocket prototype using mostly 3D‑printed parts and commodity microcontrollers for about $96. The launcher and rocket communicate over Wi‑Fi, perform basic ballistic calculations, and can integrate an optional camera/GPS mesh‑node for assisted targeting. Documentation is public, underscoring how off‑the‑shelf hardware plus home fabrication now enable weapons‑like systems at hobbyist prices, raising serious policy and safety questions. On Hacker News, this submission is mostly a pointer to an earlier, more active thread where those broader implications are debated.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Consumer 3D printing plus cheap embedded computing are collapsing the cost and expertise barriers for creating guided munitions‑like platforms.  
  - Impact: Defense planners, export‑control regulators, and domestic law enforcement must assume small groups can field crude precision weapons independently.  
  - Watch next: Expect legislative pushes targeting printer controls, CAD file hosting, and open microcontroller ecosystems—plus counter‑tech like cheap interceptors and forensic tracing.
