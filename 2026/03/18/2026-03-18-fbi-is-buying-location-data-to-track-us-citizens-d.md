# FBI is buying location data to track US citizens, director confirms

- Score: 342 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47430797) | Link: https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/18/fbi-is-buying-location-data-to-track-us-citizens-kash-patel-wyden/

- TL;DR  
    - FBI director Kash Patel told Congress the bureau again buys “commercially available” location and other data from brokers, sidestepping traditional warrant requirements. Agencies tap ad-tech datasets built from mobile apps and real-time bidding rather than requesting records from carriers, a theory of legality untested in court. Senator Ron Wyden calls it a Fourth Amendment workaround and is pushing the Government Surveillance Reform Act to require warrants. Hacker News focuses on the ad-tech supply chain, stricter consent rules, and personal defenses like pruning apps.

- Comment pulse  
    - Ad-tech supply chain enables mass, opaque location resale; intermediaries diffuse accountability; commenters blame app devs, data brokers, and Apple/Google; advise uninstalling apps, tightening permissions.  
    - Many want location governed like wiretaps: explicit per-recipient consent and warrant requirements for government buyers—counterpoint: others stress user responsibility for oversharing location to apps.  
    - Carpenter ruling requiring warrants for cell-site records seen as limited; loophole remains for “commercial” app data and national-security claims, effectively sidestepping Fourth Amendment.

- LLM perspective  
    - View: Treat ad-tech bidstream access as sensitive surveillance infrastructure; regulate intermediaries like telecoms, with auditing, retention limits, and licensing.  
    - Impact: If Congress closes the data-broker loophole, entire broker business models and free-to-play app monetization strategies will need redesign.  
    - Watch next: Track enforcement actions against individual brokers and OS-level crackdowns on invasive SDKs; both will reveal how serious policymakers are.
