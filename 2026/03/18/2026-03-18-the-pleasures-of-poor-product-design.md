# The pleasures of poor product design

- Score: 246 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47420432) | Link: https://www.inconspicuous.info/p/the-pleasures-of-poor-product-design

### TL;DR
Designer Katerina Kamprani’s long-running project “The Uncomfortable” reimagines everyday objects—a fork with a chain handle, a waterfall teapot—as deliberately unusable, exposing how much good design we take for granted. About half her ~50–60 concepts are physical prototypes, half hyperrealistic renders. She treats the work as humorous, personal art rather than a business, refusing mass production or AI tools because effort and friction are core to her process. Unexpectedly, disabled users see their own experience of bad design reflected in her pieces.
