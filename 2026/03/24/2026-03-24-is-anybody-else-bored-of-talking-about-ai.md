# Is anybody else bored of talking about AI?

- Score: 434 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47508745) | Link: https://blog.jakesaunders.dev/is-anybody-else-bored-of-talking-about-ai/

## TL;DR

The author happily uses AI daily and finds it transformative, but is tired of endless posts about “my Claude workflow” instead of what people are actually building. They argue that tech culture has shifted from obsessing over code to obsessing over tools, with managers now mandating “use more AI” and even tracking tokens like lines of code. HN commenters agree the discourse is shallow but say AI forces deeper systems thinking, exposes institutional confusion (especially in education), and raises real questions about long‑term social impact.

---

## Comment pulse

- AI as a power tool → Senior engineers who understand full systems get huge leverage; novices can still build whole apps, but many feel drained by constant “vibecoding” and hype.

- Institutions flailing → Colleges and companies push “AI is the future” without pedagogy or strategy, leaving staff and students whipsawed between “AI is cheating” and “AI is mandatory”.

- Automation anxiety vs optimism → Some see AI as resource waste and job-eroding; others argue history shows automation frees humans for higher‑value work — counterpoint: only if power structures cooperate.
