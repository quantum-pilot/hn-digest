# Epic Games to cut more than 1k jobs as Fortnite usage falls

- Score: 233 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47503810) | Link: https://www.reuters.com/legal/litigation/epic-games-said-tuesday-that-it-will-lay-off-more-than-1000-employees-2026-03-24/

### TL;DR
Epic Games is laying off over 1,000 people and cutting $500M in marketing, contracting, and hiring as Fortnite engagement and average playtime fall, despite the title still topping console MAUs. CEO Tim Sweeney says Epic has been “spending significantly more than we’re making,” especially in today’s tougher market for live-service games, and stresses the cuts aren’t AI-driven. HN commenters largely blame overexpansion—Epic Games Store subsidies, big platform bets, and ballooning headcount—rather than Fortnite itself, plus a failure to bank its boom-time profits.

### Comment pulse
- Fortnite likely remains profitable → cash burned on Epic Games Store exclusivity deals, giveaways, and ambitious platform projects; staff now absorb the cost of failed bets.  
- Flat/declining Fortnite revenue vs. ~4× headcount → estimated ~$320k/employee puts salaries + 25% platform royalties close to or above game income.  
- Some see strategic stagnation and poor risk-taking discipline; others cite Nanite, Lumen and a bold but expensive attempt to challenge Steam/Roblox — counterpoint: “innovation” didn’t translate into new hits.
