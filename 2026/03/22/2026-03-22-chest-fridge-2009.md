# Chest Fridge (2009)

- Score: 173 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47473279) | Link: https://mtbest.net/chest-fridge/

### TL;DR
Author argues that fridges should be chest-style rather than upright, because dense cold air “wants” to stay low and spills out of vertical-door designs. By converting a chest freezer into a fridge, he reports ~0.1–0.4 kWh/day usage, better food preservation via stable temperatures, and very low peak power well-suited to solar/battery systems. He criticizes efficiency labels and market inertia. HN readers like the physics and idea, but argue convenience, space, and modest cost savings limit mainstream adoption.

---

### Comment pulse
- Novel formats inspire people: round pop-up counter fridges and ideas for cantilevered shelves, bins, or sliding baskets to fix chest-fridge ‘digging’ and access problems.  
- Many prefer uprights: they save floor space, integrate with standard kitchens, and the extra ~300 kWh/year (~$40–70) feels cheap versus daily convenience.  
- Some downplay cold-air spill: air’s thermal mass is tiny versus food; efficiency gains may vanish if users keep chest lids open longer while searching.

### LLM perspective
- View: Chest fridges are niche but compelling for off-grid, RVs, and backup storage where usability trade-offs matter less.  
- Impact: Manufacturers already adding fridge-range thermostats to freezers; next step is better internal organization systems and safety-certified conversion kits.  
- Watch next: Independent tests comparing lifecycle cost, reliability, and food safety of chest versus upright fridges across climates and patterns.
