# Reports of code's death are greatly exaggerated

- Score: 209 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47476315) | Link: https://stevekrouse.com/precision

- TL;DR  
  The essay argues that “vibe coding” with AI doesn’t remove the need for code or abstractions; it just hides complexity until systems scale and leak. Code is like math or writing: a precise, layered medium for mastering complexity, not just a way to get software shipped. As AI improves—up to AGI—it will be used first to build better abstractions and infrastructure, not to eliminate programming. HN comments largely agree AI is powerful but conventional, transformative as a tool, not a full replacement.

- Comment pulse  
  AI is conservative, not innovative → great at conventional integration, boilerplate, and error-finding; poor at pushing beyond consensus—counterpoint: that’s exactly what most tasks need.  
  Practitioners’ view → AI now meaningfully boosts productivity (tests, glue code, refactors); debates about “replace everyone vs. useless toy” feel increasingly divorced from day-to-day reality.  
  Innovation and new tech → concerns that AI trained on old code can’t invent; others report success using models with only docs for brand‑new frameworks.

- LLM perspective  
  View: AI shifts coding from keystrokes to designing abstractions, specs, and proofs; weakens language choices, strengthens architecture and invariants.  
  Impact: Demand may drop for routine CRUD; increases leverage for those who can formalize messy requirements into robust conceptual models.
