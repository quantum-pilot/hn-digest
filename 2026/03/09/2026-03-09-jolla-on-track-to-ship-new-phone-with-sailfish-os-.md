# Jolla on track to ship new phone with Sailfish OS, user-replaceable battery

- Score: 180 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47311456) | Link: https://liliputing.com/the-new-jolla-phone-with-sailfish-os-is-on-track-to-start-shipping-in-the-first-half-of-2026/

## TL;DR
Jolla plans to ship a new Sailfish OS smartphone in H1 2026, after surpassing 10k preorders and opening a smaller second batch in Europe for €649. The device offers mid‑range specs, 5G, a 6.36" AMOLED screen, user‑replaceable battery, microSD, and modular “Other Half” back covers that can add hardware like keyboards or extra radios. Sailfish OS is Linux-based with a proprietary UI and Android app support; commenters debate its UI quality, long-term stability, app/banking compatibility, and whether non‑Google/Apple phones remain viable.

## Comment pulse
- SailfishOS appeal → Linux underpinnings make ad‑blocking and firewalling easy, but many find the UI ugly, sluggish, and “different without benefit” — counterpoint: some users consider it simpler and more coherent than iOS/Android.

- Long-term users → Report solid stability and useful Android-compat layer, but abandon Sailfish due to lack of small handsets, moving to tiny Android phones with LineageOS instead.

- Viability vs. app lock‑in → Concern that banking/government apps require mainstream, locked‑down platforms; others report working BankID on Sailfish and smooth experiences with de‑Googled Android (/e/OS + Fairphone).
