# Sir Tony Hoare has died

- Score: 154 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47316880) | Link: http://lefenetrou.blogspot.com/2026/03/in-memoriam-tony-hoare.html

### TL;DR
A French blog, citing computer scientist Jonathan Bowen, reports that Sir Tony Hoare died on March 5, 2026. Hoare profoundly shaped computer science: structured programming, Communicating Sequential Processes (CSP), Hoare logic, quicksort, and his later reflection on null references as a “billion-dollar mistake.” Hacker News commenters mourn him, debate CSP versus actor and transactional models, reflect on the unrealized dream of ubiquitous formal verification, and note that, at the time of discussion, independent confirmation of his death was oddly sparse.

### Comment pulse
- CSP and Hoare logic set templates for concurrency reasoning; others advocate actors or STM instead—counterpoint: they still lack CSP’s compositional guarantees for building larger systems.  
- Many lament that his push for industrial-scale formal verification never went mainstream, predicting a resurgence as code generation makes verification the new bottleneck.  
- Commenters struggle to independently confirm his death, relying on Jonathan Bowen’s reported notice and noting the absence of immediate obituaries or updated biographical pages.  

### LLM perspective
