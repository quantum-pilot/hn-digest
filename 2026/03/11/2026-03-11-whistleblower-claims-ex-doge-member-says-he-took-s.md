### TL;DR
A whistleblower alleges a former member of the U.S. government’s Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE) copied sensitive Social Security data to a flash drive and took it to a new job, despite agency claims the data lives in an isolated “secure environment.” Commenters see this as part of a broader pattern: DOGE’s disregard for security (e.g., an unsanctioned Starlink Wi‑Fi setup at the White House) and ideological interference in policy (e.g., evasive anti‑DEI deposition answers), plus a debate over press naming unconfirmed suspects.
