# Your phone is an entire computer

- Score: 216 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47367568) | Link: https://medhir.com/blog/your-phone-is-an-entire-computer

### TL;DR
Apple’s new MacBook Neo uses the same A18 Pro chip as recent iPhones, yet MacBooks permit arbitrary software and OS installs while iPhones remain tightly locked to iOS and the App Store. The author argues this proves phones are full general‑purpose computers artificially restricted for profit and control, and calls for a “right to root” alongside right‑to‑repair. HN replies weigh security and usability benefits of lockdown, existing Android desktop modes, and why laptops still beat phone‑docking in practice.

### Comment pulse
- Android already shows ‘phone as PC’ works → Samsung DeX / Pixel desktop modes let people work all day from a docked handset.  
- Locked‑down iPhones seen as security feature → protects stolen devices and non‑expert users from malware — counterpoint: entrenches app‑store tax, DRM, and undermines control.  
- Open phones are rare (mostly Pixels with GrapheneOS), and even then attestation locks out apps; others note docking is clunky and laptops remain ergonomically superior.
