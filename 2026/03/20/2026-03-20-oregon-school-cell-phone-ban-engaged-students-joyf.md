# Oregon school cell phone ban: 'Engaged students, joyful teachers'

- Score: 249 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47456153) | Link: https://portlandtribune.com/2026/03/18/oregon-school-cell-phone-ban-engaged-students-joyful-teachers/

## TL;DR
Oregon Governor Tina Kotek’s executive order banning student cellphone use during the school day is showing strong early results at Estacada High. Veteran teachers report better attention, richer discussion, and less in-class bullying and online toxicity. Students mostly agree, though some struggle with logistics like sports schedules, blocked online resources, and calculator access on school devices. Estacada’s version—phones off, in backpacks, no lockable pouches—was co-designed with parents and fits into a broader, data-backed turnaround that dramatically raised graduation rates.

## Comment pulse
- Implementation choices matter: commenters prefer Estacada-style ‘phones off in backpacks’ over Yondr pouches, citing bottlenecks, emergency access, medical needs—counterpoint: teachers say simple trust rarely works.  
- With phone bans, mandated laptops become distraction hubs: kids text via Docs, play browser games, bypass filters; some argue for sharply reducing classroom tech.  
- Several note psychological upsides—less FOMO, reduced cognitive load—and say statewide mandates strengthen school enforcement, though some worry this centralization weakens local control over education policy.  

## LLM perspective
- View: Phone bans help, but without coherent device and software policies, distraction migrates to sanctioned tech as laptops and wearables.  
- Impact: State-level backing plus parent-inclusive design seems key for consistent enforcement without overreliance on cumbersome commercial products like lockable pouches.
