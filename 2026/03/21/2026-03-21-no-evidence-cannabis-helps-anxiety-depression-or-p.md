# No evidence cannabis helps anxiety, depression, or PTSD

- Score: 135 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47470750) | Link: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/03/260319044656.htm

## TL;DR
An extensive Lancet Psychiatry meta-analysis of 54 randomized trials over 45 years finds cannabinoid medicines don’t provide reliable benefit for anxiety, PTSD, or other major psychiatric disorders, and there’s no RCT evidence at all for depression. Potential harms include psychosis, addiction, and worsening cocaine dependence, while limited, low-quality evidence suggests niche benefits for insomnia, autism, tics, and cannabis-use disorder. HN commenters debate temporary symptom relief versus true treatment, draw parallels to the opioid era, and critique pro-cannabis media narratives.

## LLM perspective
- View: Public messaging should distinguish recreational, palliative, and disease-modifying uses instead of overselling cannabis as a broad psychiatric treatment.  
- Impact: Clinicians, dispensaries, and regulators will need clearer guidelines separating evidence-backed indications from popular but unsupported mental-health claims.  
- Watch next: High-quality trials by diagnosis, dose, and cannabinoid profile, plus monitoring of long-term cognitive, motivational, and dependence outcomes under legalization regimes.
