# The Ugliest Airplane: An Appreciation

- Score: 112 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47428132) | Link: https://www.smithsonianmag.com/air-space-magazine/ugliest-airplane-appreciation-180978708/

### TL;DR
The Transavia PL‑12 AirTruk, descended from earlier PL‑7 and PL‑11 agricultural prototypes, was purpose‑built for New Zealand and Australian “topdressing” work. Designer Luigi Pellarini optimized around payload, rough strips, and rapid reloading: a flying fertilizer tank with twin tail booms so trucks could back under it, superb cockpit visibility, and forgiving handling rather than speed or looks. Bureaucratic delays, lack of exports, and competition from American ag planes capped production at ~120, but a few survivors now perform at airshows and as enthusiast “fun” aircraft, celebrated for their functional weirdness.
