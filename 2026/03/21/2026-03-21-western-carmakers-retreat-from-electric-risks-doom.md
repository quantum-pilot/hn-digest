# Western carmakers' retreat from electric risks dooming them to irrelevance

- Score: 160 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47467034) | Link: https://www.theguardian.com/business/2026/mar/21/west-carmakers-retreat-electric-vehicle-risks-irrelevance-iran-war-evs-china

### TL;DR
Guardian argues Western legacy automakers are slashing EV plans, writing down tens of billions and lobbying to dilute phase-out dates, just as Chinese firms that bet early on batteries, software and vertical integration flood global markets with cheaper, longer-range models. Oil-price spikes from Middle East conflict highlight the strategic risk of clinging to combustion. Hacker News commenters counter that European OEMs still ship strong EV lineups, but note patchy charging infrastructure, harsh winter range limits and fierce Chinese price competition.

### Comment pulse
- Real-world EV experience varies by region: some German drivers see broken chargers and severe winter range loss; others report dense, reliable DC fast-charging networks.  
- Are Western firms retreating? Commenters list strong EU EV lineups, but point to huge write-downs, job cuts and Chinese models 25–50% cheaper eroding margins.  
- Pace of transition: some say going 100% EV now ignores infrastructure, grid and home-upgrade limits; others argue oil shocks prove delaying electrification is risk.
