# Bird brains (2023)

- Score: 284 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47573887) | Link: https://www.dhanishsemar.com/writing/bird-brains

- TL;DR  
  New Zealand road crews discovered kea parrots deliberately moving traffic cones to stop cars and extract food from humans, prompting “kea gyms” as enrichment. The essay surveys experiments showing bird cognition—mirror self-recognition, Aesop-style tool use, delayed gratification, sophisticated vocal communication, and extraordinary spatial memory. A key study finds parrots and corvids pack primate-level neuron counts into tiny brains, overturning “bird brain” as an insult and informally ranking corvids and parrots alongside great apes in intelligence.
