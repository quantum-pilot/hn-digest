# FTC action against Match and OkCupid for deceiving users, sharing personal data

- Score: 232 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47575616) | Link: https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2026/03/ftc-takes-action-against-match-okcupid-deceiving-users-sharing-personal-data-third-party

### TL;DR
The FTC settled with OkCupid and Match Group Americas over allegations that OkCupid secretly gave a facial-recognition startup access to data on millions of users—photos, locations, and demographics—despite privacy policies promising limited, disclosed sharing and opt-outs. The company allegedly did this mainly because OkCupid’s founders had invested in the third party, then spent years denying and concealing it. The resulting order bars Match/OkCupid from misrepresenting any aspect of their data collection, sharing, or privacy controls, but appears to impose no monetary penalties, which frustrated many HN readers.
