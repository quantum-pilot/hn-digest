# Effort to prevent government officials from engaging in prediction markets

- Score: 170 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47291406) | Link: https://www.merkley.senate.gov/merkley-klobuchar-launch-new-effort-to-ban-federal-elected-officials-profiting-from-prediction-markets/

## TL;DR
Senators Jeff Merkley and Amy Klobuchar introduced the End Prediction Market Corruption Act, banning the President, VP, members of Congress, and senior federal officials from trading prediction-market event contracts. Prompted by suspiciously well-timed Polymarket bets on Iran and Venezuela events, the bill aims to prevent use of nonpublic government information for profit and bolsters CFTC enforcement. Hacker News commenters broadly accept the conflict-of-interest problem but question the bill’s narrow scope, practical enforceability, and whether existing ethics/insider-trading rules already cover this behavior.

---

## Comment pulse
- Ban is too narrow → appointed officials, military, staff, and relatives can still trade; some propose mandatory real-identity, public bets to curb abuse.  
- Support for banning officials → insider trading is hard to police, and election bets may distort close races via shilling and defamation—counterpoint: campaigns could just hedge by betting on opponents.  
- Skeptics → private info will reach markets via proxies anyway; prediction markets are valuable information tools; unclear what this adds beyond current conflict-of-interest laws.
