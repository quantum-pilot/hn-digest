# OkCupid gave 3M dating-app photos to facial recognition firm, FTC says

- Score: 311 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47591104) | Link: https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2026/03/okcupid-match-pay-no-fine-for-sharing-user-photos-with-facial-recognition-firm/

## TL;DR
OkCupid (owned by Match Group) secretly gave Clarifai about 3 million dating-profile photos plus demographic and location data in 2014, helping build facial-recognition models for age, sex, and race—without user consent and in violation of its own privacy policy. The FTC says OkCupid then lied publicly and tried to obstruct its investigation. Under a Trump-era, all-Republican FTC, the companies settled with no fine and only a promise not to misrepresent data practices going forward, angering privacy-minded observers.
