# New iron nanomaterial wipes out cancer cells without harming healthy tissue

- Score: 215 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47207404) | Link: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/02/260228093456.htm

- TL;DR  
  - Oregon State researchers built an iron-based metal–organic framework “nanoagent” that exploits tumors’ acidity and hydrogen peroxide to catalyze two kinds of reactive oxygen species—hydroxyl radicals and singlet oxygen—inside cancer cells. In mice bearing human breast tumors, IV dosing led to complete tumor regression with no observed systemic toxicity. HN commenters welcome the promise but stress it’s still an “in mice” result, human trials are a decade-scale process, and recent cancer progress often feels incremental rather than miraculous.
