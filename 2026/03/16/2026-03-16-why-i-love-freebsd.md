# Why I love FreeBSD

- Score: 330 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47397574) | Link: https://it-notes.dragas.net/2026/03/16/why-i-love-freebsd/

### TL;DR
The author describes switching from Linux to FreeBSD in 2002 after discovering its uniquely thorough Handbook and has since used it for decades of stable, low-drama servers. FreeBSD’s appeal is its cohesive “whole OS” design, conservative evolution, and native tools like jails, ZFS boot environments, read‑only rootfs, and bhyve—backed by a passionate, non‑hype community and a strong Foundation. HN commenters largely confirm the long-term reliability and storage strengths, while acknowledging Linux’s broader hardware support and some isolated driver issues.

### Comment pulse
- FreeBSD lauded for multi-year uptimes, minimal admin work, strong docs, first-class ZFS and tape/SCSI support; Linux still wins on breadth of hardware and tooling.  
- Many value it as a cohesive Unix OS, not a kit of parts; Linux absorbs bleeding-edge features and bloat, leaving BSDs a smaller, focused culture.  
- Experiences vary: one saw Intel NIC mbuf failures and quit FreeBSD; others report flawless networking on similar hardware and blame firmware quirks, not the OS.

### LLM perspective
- View: FreeBSD shines as an opinionated server OS emphasizing predictability, human-scale complexity, and integrated features over maximal hardware support.  
- Impact: Sysadmins and homelabbers tired of container sprawl can replace Docker/Kubernetes stacks with simpler jails plus native ZFS boot environments.  
- Watch next: maturity of hardware support, improved jail orchestration tooling, and whether major vendors continue funding FreeBSD development and documentation.
