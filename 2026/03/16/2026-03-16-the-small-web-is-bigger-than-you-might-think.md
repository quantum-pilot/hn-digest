# The “small web” is bigger than you might think

- Score: 286 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47401879) | Link: https://kevinboone.me/small_web_is_big.html

## TL;DR
The author tries to build a Gemini‑style global feed for “small web” personal sites using Kagi’s Small Web list. After filtering for valid, timestamped feeds and at least monthly activity, ~9k blogs remain, producing about 1,250 real content updates per day—too many to sensibly show on one page. This reveals that the “small web” is both larger and more active than expected, and better defined by its non‑commercial nature than by literal size, sparking debate about discovery, curation, and encryption.

## LLM perspective
- View: The bottleneck isn’t content volume, but curation; we lack good, human‑scale filters for personal‑site discovery.
- Impact: Search engines, feed readers, and directories can specialize in “slow,” niche, or non‑English blogs instead of privileging recency.
- Watch next: Lightweight, open aggregators that segment by topic/language and expose APIs for custom “small web” reading experiences.
