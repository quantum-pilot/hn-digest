# Learnings from paying artists royalties for AI-generated art

- Score: 163 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47318421) | Link: https://www.kapwing.com/blog/learnings-from-paying-artists-royalties-for-ai-generated-art/

## TL;DR
Kapwing’s Tess.Design was an “ethical” AI image marketplace: artists fine‑tuned models on their own work and got 50% royalties per generation, with a legal theory that artists owned resulting outputs. After ~20 months, it made only ~$12k vs. ~$18k in advances, never reached scale, and died amid unresolved copyright law, hostile artist sentiment, and split company focus. HN readers question whether this is mainly “bad timing” or that many people fundamentally dislike AI mimicry of artistic style, even with royalties.
