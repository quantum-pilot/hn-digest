# Pentagon formally labels Anthropic supply-chain risk

- Score: 364 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47266084) | Link: https://www.wsj.com/politics/national-security/pentagon-formally-labels-anthropic-supply-chain-risk-escalating-conflict-ebdf0523

### TL;DR
The Pentagon has formally labeled Anthropic a “supply‑chain risk,” a designation usually reserved for companies tied to foreign adversaries. That move effectively severs Anthropic from any contractor doing Defense Department work and escalates an ongoing feud over AI safety, access, and contracts. Anthropic plans a court fight. Hacker News commenters see this as a major escalation in politicizing procurement, a dangerous precedent for arbitrary economic punishment, and possibly a boon for rivals like OpenAI amid lobbying and ethics concerns.

### Comment pulse
- Designation normally targets foreign threats → using it in a domestic contract dispute chills all tech firms considering U.S. government work.  
- Politicized punishment of vendors → blurs into authoritarian tactics; commenters fear both parties will weaponize it—counterpoint: some argue the public already ignores worse abuses.  
- OpenAI benefits from Anthropic’s exclusion → some suspect lobbying or bribery; many doubt “ethics pledges” will matter when lucrative Defense deals appear.
