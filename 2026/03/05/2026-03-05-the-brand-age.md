# The Brand Age

- Score: 189 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47264756) | Link: https://paulgraham.com/brandage.html

### TL;DR
Swiss watchmaking had a “golden age” (1945–1970) focused on thinness and accuracy, then was crushed by Japanese competition, currency shifts, and superior quartz movements. A few firms survived by pivoting from engineering to luxury branding: oversized, instantly recognizable designs, heavy advertising, and deliberate scarcity (e.g., Patek’s Nautilus waitlists) turned mechanical watches into Veblen goods and financial assets. Graham argues this “brand age” subordinates good design to distinctiveness, deforms markets and taste, and generalizes beyond watches; his prescription is to avoid brand games and simply follow interesting problems.
