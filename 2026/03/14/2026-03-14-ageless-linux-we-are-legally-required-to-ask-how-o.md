# Ageless Linux. We are legally required to ask how old you are. We won't

- Score: 127 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47381791) | Link: https://agelesslinux.org/

- TL;DR  
Ageless Linux is a Debian-based “distribution” implemented mostly as a bash script that renames `/etc/os-release` and installs a manifesto refusing to comply with California’s Digital Age Assurance Act (AB 1043). The law broadly defines “operating system providers” and “covered application stores,” effectively sweeping in Linux distros, repos, and hobby projects while imposing age-verification APIs that Big Tech already has. The project is deliberate civil disobedience: invite enforcement, expose overbroad drafting and selective prosecution, and argue for education-focused child safety instead of identity-surveillance infrastructure.
