- TL;DR  
German philosopher and sociologist Jürgen Habermas has died aged 96 in Starnberg. A central figure of the Frankfurt School’s second generation, he reshaped debates on democracy, the public sphere, and the role of communication in legitimizing power. Landmark works include Structural Transformation of the Public Sphere, Knowledge and Human Interests, Legitimation Crisis, and Theory of Communicative Action. Hacker News commenters share entry points into his dense oeuvre, debate whether his ideas survive the internet era, and connect his death to current threats to democracy.
