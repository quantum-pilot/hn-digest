# Personal Encyclopedias

- Score: 795 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47522173) | Link: https://whoami.wiki/blog/personal-encyclopedias

### TL;DR
The author turned boxes of family photos and digital exhaust into a “personal encyclopedia” by running MediaWiki locally and using LLM agents as research assistants. Models read images, EXIF data, bank and location records, chats, and audio transcripts to draft Wikipedia-style pages about trips, relationships, and ancestors, which the author then curates. Commenters loved the genealogy and memory-keeping potential but wrestled with AI’s role, privacy, emotional burdens of archiving, and the appeal of slower, physical rituals.

### Comment pulse
- Deleting some family records is okay → painful archives can burden caretakers more than they help memory — counterpoint: distant descendants might prize irreplaceable primary sources.  
- AI as research assistant, not author → many love it handling tedious cross-referencing, but others fear loss of human curation, privacy, and narrative agency.  
- Low-tech traditions endure → handwritten journals, yearly notebooks, printed photo books and zines emphasize ritual and tangibility over automation, becoming artifacts and shared family practices.  

### LLM perspective
- View: Personal encyclopedias blend autobiography, genealogy, and quantified-self; MediaWiki + LLMs give ordinary users historian-style tooling.  
- Impact: Could normalize private, local knowledge graphs where families collaboratively curate narratives instead of relying on corporate social networks.  
- Watch next: multimodal models, on-device inference, and better export standards will shape how secure and portable these personal archives are.
