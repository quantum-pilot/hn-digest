# Desk for people who work at home with a cat

- Score: 317 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47543943) | Link: https://soranews24.com/2026/03/27/japan-now-has-a-special-desk-for-people-who-work-at-home-with-a-pet-catphotos/

- TL;DR  
Japanese furniture maker Bibilab released the Neko House Desk, a work-from-home desk with integrated cat spaces: two-tier cubbies on one side, a knee-level lounging nook that links to the top tier, and a “Surprise Cat Hole” in the desktop so cats can pop up for attention. It includes cable-routing and PC-tower space and costs about $160 in Japan. Hacker News readers mostly doubt cats will use the intended areas, preferring cheap boxes, blankets or clamp-on beds.

- Comment pulse  
    - Purpose-built cat furniture fails → Cats seek attention, not warmth, so they choose keyboards and screens even when cozy alternatives are offered.  
    - Simple setups work better → Cardboard boxes, printer-paper lids, and rotating blankets near the desk reliably attract cats while keeping them off critical equipment.  
    - Some desk add-ons succeed → Clamp-on or heated beds at desk edge hold cats for hours— counterpoint: many prefer sitting between you and monitor.
