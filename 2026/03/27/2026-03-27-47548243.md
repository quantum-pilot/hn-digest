# If you don't opt out by Apr 24 GitHub will train on your private repos

- Score: 607 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47548243) | Link: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47548243

## TL;DR
GitHub is changing Copilot defaults so that, starting April 24, interaction data from private repos (prompts, accepted suggestions, surrounding code, filenames, navigation) may be used to train models for individual Free/Pro users unless they opt out. Business/enterprise Copilot plans remain excluded. Hacker News debate focuses on whether this is “training on private repos” in practice, the reliability of GitHub’s assurances, the broader trend that any unencrypted SaaS data will eventually feed AI, and the legality of opt‑out consent, especially in the EU.
