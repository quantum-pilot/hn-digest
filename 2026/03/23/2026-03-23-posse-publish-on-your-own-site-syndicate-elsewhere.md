# POSSE – Publish on your Own Site, Syndicate Elsewhere

- Score: 387 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47486726) | Link: https://indieweb.org/POSSE

## TL;DR
POSSE (Publish on your Own Site, Syndicate Elsewhere) is an IndieWeb pattern: you publish first on your own domain, then cross‑post or link copies to social platforms. Your site holds the canonical URL; silo copies link back, improving ownership, resilience to platform changes, search ranking, and enabling “backfeed” of replies/likes. The article details implementation patterns, tools, and alternatives (PESOS, COPE) plus CRUD ideas. HN discussion focuses on comments, automation friction, spammy-feeling cross‑posts, and using RSS plus external discussion hubs.

## Comment pulse
- Host on your own site, but outsource discussion → let HN/Reddit/Mastodon threads form, then link back from the article instead of running your own comments.  
- Automation vs friction → some find cross‑posting hard due to hostile APIs and link‑penalties; others rely on tools like micro.blog or custom scripts — counterpoint: over‑broadcasting can feel spammy.  
- Readers want control → prefer subscribing via RSS, then choosing where to discuss; IndieWeb events and “small web” projects motivate people to build personal sites again.
