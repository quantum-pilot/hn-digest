# US and TotalEnergies reach 'nearly $1B' deal to end offshore wind projects

- Score: 300 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47492599) | Link: https://www.lemonde.fr/en/international/article/2026/03/23/us-and-totalenergies-reach-nearly-1-billion-deal-to-end-offshore-wind-projects_6751739_4.html

### TL;DR
US Interior and TotalEnergies agreed to cancel about 4 GW of US offshore wind leases and redirect the company’s $928m lease payments into US oil and gas projects, chiefly LNG. The Trump administration will then reimburse TotalEnergies up to that amount, effectively swapping previous offshore-wind support for fossil-fuel investment. HN commenters see it as a taxpayer-funded subsidy to increase emissions and reward a foreign oil major, and debate what this says about US policy stability and long‑term prospects.

### Comment pulse
- Deal structure: Interior says TotalEnergies must first invest ~$928m in US oil/gas, then gets reimbursed; commenters call this a backdoor fossil subsidy and opaque penalty.  
- Many argue Trump’s hostility is ideological and personal (e.g., past fight over turbines near his golf resort), likening this to candlemakers petitioning against the sun.  
- Clarifications: affected wind farms were early-stage with no construction; separately, some commenters discuss hedging against US political risk via foreign property or dual residency.

### LLM perspective
- View: Retroactively canceling clean-energy leases in favor of fossil projects raises perceived regulatory risk for long-horizon infrastructure investors.  
- Impact: Could chill offshore wind bids, shift capital to friendlier jurisdictions or shorter-payback projects, and entrench LNG infrastructure for decades.  
- Watch next: response in upcoming offshore lease auctions, project financing terms, and whether Congress or courts scrutinize such reimbursements.
