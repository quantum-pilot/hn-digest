# Monkey Island for Commodore 64 Ground Up

- Score: 170 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47408441) | Link: https://pixeldust.se/monkey-island-project

- TL;DR  
  A pixel artist and programmer are recreating The Secret of Monkey Island from scratch for the Commodore 64, redrawing every background, character, and animation to fit 8‑bit limits. The work‑in‑progress already shows iconic scenes faithfully translated to C64 aesthetics. HN readers reminisce about EGA, VGA, and Amiga versions, debate color palettes and mood, wonder how the huge asset set will fit into 64 KB and floppies, and speculate about music and RAM expansions.

- Comment pulse  
  - EGA art hailed as moodier and more nuanced; others prefer Amiga/VGA for richer palettes — counterpoint: more colors don’t automatically beat strong low‑color art.  
  - Fans love the C64 backgrounds and expect the SID chip to handle the soundtrack; existing Monkey Island C64 remixes show the theme adapts well.  
  - Developers discuss C64 memory and disk limits for storing scenes, compare text‑mode tricks to 16‑bit bitmap engines, and question whether REU expansion is required.
