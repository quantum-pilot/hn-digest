# Illinois Introducing Operating System Account Age Bill

- Score: 230 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47416131) | Link: https://www.ilga.gov/Legislation/BillStatus?DocTypeID=HB&DocNum=5511

## TL;DR
Illinois HB5511, the Children’s Social Media Safety Act, would require operating systems by 2028 to collect a user’s birth date/age at account setup and expose an “age category” signal to social media platforms. Platforms must use that to detect minors and apply youth‑protective defaults, with violations treated as consumer fraud from 2027. Hacker News discussion centers on Meta’s lobbying, shifting liability away from platforms, OS‑level surveillance risks, erosion of anonymity, and the likelihood of a digital‑ID ecosystem emerging around such laws.

## Comment pulse
- Meta is backing age‑verification laws to offload COPPA liability to OS vendors and future ID brokers → platforms avoid huge fines—counterpoint: some say liability shielding without real verification is actually a feature.  
- OS‑level age signals are a privacy and anonymity hazard → they create a universal identifier for advertisers, doxxers, and governments, and will pressure toward strong ID and digital passports.  
- Scope and design are shaky → vague “OS provider” definition, misuse on shared devices, burden on open‑source OSes, and potential slippery slope from optional DOB field to mandatory verified identity.
