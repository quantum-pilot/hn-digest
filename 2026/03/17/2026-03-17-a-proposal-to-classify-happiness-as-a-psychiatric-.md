# A proposal to classify happiness as a psychiatric disorder (1992)

- Score: 169 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47412097) | Link: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC1376114/

- TL;DR  
  The paper jokingly proposes classifying happiness as “major affective disorder, pleasant type” because it fits common disease criteria: statistical rarity, distinctive symptoms, cognitive biases, and neural correlates. The punchline is that the only reason it isn’t a disorder is that we like it, exposing how value-laden psychiatric categories are. HN readers connect this to American culture’s fixation on “being happy,” unrealistic expectations of constant positivity, Buddhist ideas of neutral baseline, and concerns about psychiatry and pharma medicalizing normal moods.

  - Impact: Could encourage clinicians and patients to focus on functional impairment and suffering, not deviation from culturally idealized emotional targets.
