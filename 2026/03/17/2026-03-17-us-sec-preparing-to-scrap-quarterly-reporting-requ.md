# US SEC preparing to scrap quarterly reporting requirement

- Score: 723 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47406779) | Link: https://www.reuters.com/business/finance/us-sec-preparing-eliminate-quarterly-reporting-requirement-wsj-says-2026-03-16/

- TL;DR  
SEC is reportedly drafting a rule to make quarterly earnings reports optional, letting U.S. public companies report only twice a year. Supporters say this could reduce compliance costs and blunt short‑term pressure to hit every 90‑day target. Critics on HN fear regular investors will lose timely information while insiders and sophisticated traders gain an edge, especially as markets move toward 24/7 trading and faster derivatives. Many argue the real problem is earnings‑obsessed culture, not the frequency of disclosure.

- Comment pulse  
  - Less frequent reports shift power to insiders and accredited investors; public markets already shrinking, so reduced transparency hurts small savers — counterpoint: may slightly encourage IPOs.  
  - Quarterly deadlines drive gamesmanship: shifting shipments or orders across cutoff dates, burning staff time on optics instead of operations; fewer reports might reduce deadline‑driven distortions.  
  - HN worries about combining 0DTE options and near‑24/7 trading with slower disclosures, amplifying volatility and manipulation when important information is scarcer and unevenly distributed.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Expect compromise structures: mandatory quarterly key metrics, but full audited financials only semiannual for mid‑cap and larger firms.  
  - Impact: If adopted, event‑driven funds and quant shops will adapt quickly; retail investors may rely more on alternative, less reliable data.  
  - Watch next: Watch SEC comment file, exchange rule changes, and any parallel push to raise disclosure standards for large private unicorns.
