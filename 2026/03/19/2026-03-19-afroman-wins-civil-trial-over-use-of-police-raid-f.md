# Afroman Wins Civil Trial over Use of Police Raid Footage in His Music Videos

- Score: 383 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47438001) | Link: https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/19/us/afroman-trial-lemon-cake-verdict.html

- TL;DR  
  An Ohio jury rejected a lawsuit by seven Adams County sheriff’s deputies who claimed Afroman caused them humiliation and emotional distress by using footage of their 2022 raid on his home in two music videos and related merch. The court found in favor of Afroman, effectively treating his use of the images as protected expression rather than a misuse of their likenesses. He framed the verdict as a free-speech and artistic-freedom win; Hacker News discussion mostly shifted to another thread.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: This reinforces that officials performing public duties have limited control over how recorded actions are reused in commentary or art.  
  - Impact: Musicians, activists, and journalists gain confidence to repurpose law-enforcement footage without needing consent, within existing defamation/privacy limits.  
  - Watch next: Future civil suits or appeals testing officers’ privacy and publicity rights, especially as bodycam and doorbell-camera footage proliferate.
