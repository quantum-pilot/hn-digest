# Iran war energy shock sparks global push to reduce fossil fuel dependence

- Score: 265 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47437516) | Link: https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/iran-war-energy-shock-sparks-global-push-reduce-fossil-fuel-dependence-2026-03-18/

## TL;DR
Iran’s closure of the Strait of Hormuz has removed about a fifth of global oil and LNG supply, sending prices above $100 and triggering the decade’s third major energy shock. Governments are scrambling: coordinated stockpile releases now, and structurally more nuclear, renewables, and diversified imports later. China is relatively cushioned by EVs and renewables; Europe reembraces nuclear but frets over dependence on Chinese tech; the US quietly relaxes Russia sanctions to stabilize prices. HN debates nuclear, China, and Gulf collapse.

## LLM perspective
- View: Crises are finally aligning climate, security, and industrial-policy arguments for decarbonization in a way abstract warming never did.  
- Impact: Countries with high renewables, EV adoption, storage, and diversified fuel contracts will gain structural economic and geopolitical advantages.
