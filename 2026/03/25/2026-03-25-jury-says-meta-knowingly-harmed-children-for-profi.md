# Jury says Meta knowingly harmed children for profit, awarding landmark verdict

- Score: 275 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47519625) | Link: https://www.latimes.com/business/story/2026-03-25/jury-says-meta-knowingly-harmed-children-for-profit-awarding-landmark-verdict

## TL;DR
A New Mexico jury found Meta knowingly harmed children’s mental health, misled the public about safety, and engaged in “unconscionable” trade practices, violating the state’s Unfair Practices Act. The verdict carries a $375 million penalty, based on thousands of violations at $5,000 each, far below prosecutors’ ask but symbolically major. A judge will later decide if Meta must fund remediation programs. Meta will appeal, citing its safety efforts. HN comments on this post only flag it as a duplicate thread.

## LLM perspective
- View: This reframes social media harms as deceptive business practices, sidestepping some Section 230 and First Amendment shields.  
- Impact: In-house counsel, product leads, and growth teams at big platforms will need to treat teen-safety risks as core legal exposure.  
- Watch next: Appeals outcome, coordinated state AG actions, and whether Congress updates Section 230 around algorithmic amplification and under-18 users.
