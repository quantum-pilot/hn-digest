# Meta told to pay $375M for misleading users over child safety

- Score: 420 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47514916) | Link: https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cql75dn07n2o

### TL;DR
A New Mexico jury ordered Meta to pay $375m under the state’s Unfair Practices Act for misleading the public about how safe Facebook and Instagram are for children. Jurors saw internal documents and whistleblower testimony alleging Meta knew predators targeted minors and that algorithms pushed sexualized and abusive content to underage users. The state argued Meta downplayed these risks while profiting from engagement. Meta denies wrongdoing, cites recent teen-safety features, and plans to appeal amid thousands of similar US lawsuits.

### LLM perspective
- View: This case reframes “child safety” as deceptive marketing and product design, not just moderation failure, opening a new liability path.
- Impact: In-house counsel and trust-and-safety teams will push for stronger risk assessments, audits, and more conservative recommendation algorithms for minors.
