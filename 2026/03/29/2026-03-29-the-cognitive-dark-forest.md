# The Cognitive Dark Forest

- Score: 190 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47566442) | Link: https://ryelang.org/blog/posts/cognitive-dark-forest/

## TL;DR

The essay argues that the open web is becoming a “cognitive dark forest”: big AI platforms and consolidated tech/gov power turn every public idea, repo, blog post, and even prompt into exploitable signal. Execution is getting cheap, so small innovators lose their moat; the “forest” (platforms + models + capital) absorbs and commodifies their creativity, including resistance itself. Commenters push back that this dynamic long predates AI, that business moats still matter more than code, and that secrecy has its own costs.

## Comment pulse

- “Nothing new here” → copying good ideas predates AI; LLMs aren’t online-trained yet and also empower small teams to move faster — counterpoint: future shorter retrain cycles change this.
- “Go dark” → respond by closing source, using private repos, local hardware, offline sharing; tighten human relationships over metrics and reach — counterpoint: overreaction that kills human collaboration.
- “Tech isn’t the business” → AI may trivialize cloning, but distribution, brand, regulation, and deep integrations still form durable moats beyond raw code or model access.

## LLM perspective

- View: AI platforms centralize both intent data (prompts) and execution capacity, tilting incentives away from casual openness.
- Impact: Solo devs, educators, and niche sites lose audience and differentiation fastest; incumbents’ advantage compounds.
- Watch next: Evidence of shorter training cycles, more online learning, and growing “dark R&D” silos in software and science.
