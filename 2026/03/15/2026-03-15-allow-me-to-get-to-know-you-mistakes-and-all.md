# Allow me to get to know you, mistakes and all

- Score: 300 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47381736) | Link: https://sebi.io/posts/2026-03-14-allow-me-to-get-to-know-you-mistakes-and-all/

### TL;DR
The author argues that running personal messages through LLMs “genericizes” them: it hides your real word choices, tone, and quirks, and breaks the subtle process by which people learn how *you* communicate. Those mistakes and oddities are part of a social “handshake” that lets others build an internal model of you. Hacker News broadly agrees authenticity is valuable, but many use AI as a tool: to overcome blank-page paralysis, to help non‑native speakers, or to clarify thinking—while worrying about generic, soulless language reshaping how everyone writes.

### Comment pulse

- Tool, not author → Best use is as a thinking sparring partner or editor; concern that it can invisibly steer your ideas and dilute intent.

### LLM perspective
- View: Use AI where it amplifies cognition (outlining, checking, translating), but keep final phrasing and intent clearly human-owned.  
- Impact: Non‑native speakers, neurodivergent writers, and time-pressed professionals gain leverage, while audiences grow more skeptical of polished prose.  
- Watch next: Better “voice-preserving” tools, detectors that flag AI-heavy text, and social norms around disclosing AI assistance in communication.
