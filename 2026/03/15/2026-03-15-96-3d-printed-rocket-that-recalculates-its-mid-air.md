# $96 3D-printed rocket that recalculates its mid-air trajectory using a $5 sensor

- Score: 374 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47385935) | Link: https://github.com/novatic14/MANPADS-System-Launcher-and-Rocket

### TL;DR
A hobbyist has open-sourced a proof-of-concept, mostly 3D‑printed, shoulder-launched guided rocket system built for about $96 in hardware. It uses an ESP32 flight computer with a $5 IMU to adjust trajectory mid‑flight; CAD, firmware, and simulations are all provided. Hacker News discussion admires the engineering and low-cost guidance, compares it to historic “minimal-brain” missiles, and notes how consumer electronics now approach mil‑grade capabilities. Many commenters, however, question the rocket’s actual performance and raise ethical, political, and proliferation concerns.
