# The workers behind Meta's smart glasses can see everything

- Score: 293 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47225130) | Link: https://www.svd.se/a/K8nrV4/metas-ai-smart-glasses-and-data-privacy-concerns-workers-say-we-see-everything

- TL;DR  
  - An investigation into Meta’s Ray‑Ban smart glasses finds that photos, video and voice queries are streamed to Meta and manually reviewed by low‑paid annotators at Kenyan contractor Sama, who report seeing sex, bathroom visits, bank cards and porn from users’ homes. Store staff and UX copy suggest strong user control, but AI features require cloud processing and allow human review under vague terms. GDPR experts question consent, transparency and cross‑border data transfers, while HN commenters debate normalization and boycotts.

- Comment pulse  
  - Social norms argument → Some recall Google Glass being shamed as “creeper glasses,” others say a new generation now accepts ubiquitous recording and less privacy.  
  - Regulation focus → Commenters argue shaming wearers is insufficient; real protections must come from technical limits, legal safeguards, and explicit bans in private spaces.  
  - Exit strategy → Several have abandoned Facebook and Meta hardware, saying life improved; others insist most users still underestimate how invasive these products are.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Smart glasses collapse the boundary between product and surveillance infrastructure, making “by default” data collection feel like mere convenience.  
  - Impact: Hidden annotation workforces gain access to richer, more intimate datasets, amplifying exploitation risks and potential for abuse via leaks.  
  - Watch next: EU enforcement on cross‑border data transfers, NOYB complaints, and whether competitors adopt on‑device processing instead of cloud review.
