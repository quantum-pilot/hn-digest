# Welcome (back) to Macintosh

- Score: 187 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47224118) | Link: https://take.surf/2026/03/01/welcome-back-to-macintosh

### TL;DR
An experienced Mac developer laments years of unresolved macOS regressions—flaky Time Machine, unreliable Spotlight and Finder, audio glitches, focus bugs—contrasted with Apple’s energy spent on Tahoe’s controversial redesign and ecosystem lock‑in. They fear Apple has lost the organizational will to prioritize stability, developer needs, and user ownership over aesthetics and services. Commenters echo deep frustration, recounting broken workflows and stealthy backup failures, with many planning or completing migrations to Linux, Android, or even Windows, though some report Tahoe works fine.

### Comment pulse
- Apple platforms feel increasingly hostile to owners → Longtime fans cite backup failures, dev breakage, UI regressions, JIT bans; many are migrating to Linux/Android ecosystems.

- Core reliability features are mistrusted → Silent Time Machine failures, slow backups, and decade-long SMB flakiness undermine trust in Apple for pro and everyday workflows.
