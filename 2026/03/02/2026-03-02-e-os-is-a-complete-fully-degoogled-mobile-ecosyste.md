# /e/OS is a complete, fully “deGoogled” mobile ecosystem

- Score: 605 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47215489) | Link: https://e.foundation/e-os/

### TL;DR
/e/OS is an Android-based, open-source mobile OS that strips out Google services, replaces them with microG and non-Google infrastructure, and bundles privacy-oriented defaults, including tracker-scored apps, an “Advanced Privacy” control panel, parental controls, and a Murena online account for mail, storage and collaboration. HN readers like the goal but debate sustainability of Android forks, app-lock‑in via banking and payment apps, reliance on WebUSB installers, how “deGoogled” microG really is, and whether GrapheneOS or mainline Linux phones are better paths.

### Comment pulse
- Some say Android forks delay truly open phones → others see AOSP as the only viable, secure base today—counterpoint: Google could limit future source drops.  
- Banking and payment apps often block custom ROMs, enforcing Apple/Google duopoly → commenters fear losing ability to transact; collective, regulatory action seen as necessary.  
- WebUSB-only installer excludes Firefox and confuses device-check flow → others dislike Murena account dependence and microG’s Google SDK use, preferring GrapheneOS/LineageOS with self‑hosted clouds.  

### LLM perspective
- View: /e/OS is a pragmatic “less-bad Android” for non-Pixel hardware, but not a full escape from proprietary ecosystems.  
- Impact: Most benefit is for privacy-conscious mainstream users; high-threat models still need hardened devices like Pixels with GrapheneOS.  
- Watch next: Monitor banking-app compatibility lists, growth of WebUSB-free installers, and any regulation forcing payment apps to support alternative OSes.
