# “It turns out” (2010)

- Score: 226 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47248319) | Link: https://jsomers.net/blog/it-turns-out

### TL;DR
Somers dissects the phrase “it turns out,” inspired by Paul Graham’s essays. In everyday speech it honestly reports a discovery (“I checked; they don’t have roast beef”). But in argumentative writing it can smuggle in big, under-argued claims while sounding neutral, hard-won, and inevitable—readers infer that evidence exists, even when it’s missing. HN and earlier commenters counter that the phrase often just flags “result of work; details skipped,” and can be a useful, even polite, rhetorical tool.
