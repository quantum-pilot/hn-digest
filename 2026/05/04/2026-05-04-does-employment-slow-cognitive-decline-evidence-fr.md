# Does Employment Slow Cognitive Decline? Evidence from Labor Market Shocks

- Score: 188 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48009983) | Link: https://www.nber.org/papers/w35117

### TL;DR
Using US Health and Retirement Study data, the authors exploit local labor-demand shocks as an instrument for employment to estimate causal effects on cognition. Negative shocks that push people out of work lead to notably faster declines in cognitive test scores, concentrated among men aged 51–64. The results extend earlier work focused on formal retirement and suggest that continued employment can modestly delay age-related cognitive decline. Hacker News discussion centers on whether it’s “jobs” specifically or meaningful, structured, social activity that matters.
