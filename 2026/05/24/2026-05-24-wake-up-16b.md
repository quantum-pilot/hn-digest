# Wake up! 16b

- Score: 406 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48253060) | Link: https://hellmood.111mb.de/wake_up_16b_writeup.html

### TL;DR
A 16-byte DOS program in x86 real mode produces synchronized visuals and sound by evolving a Sierpinski triangle directly in video memory and piping the same data to the PC speaker. It relies on BIOS-initialized text buffer patterns, segment wrapping, XOR-based cellular automata (rule 60), and a carefully chosen memory stride to turn a 1-bit fractal into “Matrix rain” columns and gritty, self-similar bytebeat audio. HN readers mostly marvel at the apparent “witchcraft” and go down math/CA rabbit holes.
