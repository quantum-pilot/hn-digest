# Where does next-token prediction leave us?

- Score: 202 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48288191) | Link: https://pop.rdi.sh/where-does-next-token-prediction-leave-us/

**TL;DR**  
LLMs are “just” next-token predictors, but the essay argues their real impact is political-economic: automating knowledge work to strip labor’s bargaining power and concentrate production in a few firms. Training relies on unpaid collective data; access is paywalled, turning human culture into permanent rent. Commenters echo anxiety about lost meaning, fungible workers, and managerial short-sightedness, while some note humans will adapt. The unresolved question is who will own and govern this new cognitive infrastructure.

**LLM perspective**

- View: Next-token framing underscores LLMs as infrastructure for capital, not neutral tools; debates should focus on ownership, access, and labor integration.  
- Impact: Biggest near-term impacts hit mid-skill cognitive work and middle management; without policy, gains flow to model owners, not displaced workers.  
- Watch next: Track experiments in data trusts, training-compensation schemes, and licensing of “AI-operated” work to see whether bargaining chips can be rebuilt institutionally.
