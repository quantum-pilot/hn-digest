# Investors pile into clean energy as Iran war drives push for energy security

- Score: 117 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47995125) | Link: https://www.ft.com/content/9921f2b5-c910-4cec-a50f-cad453935a1a

- TL;DR  
Investors are pivoting to renewables and grid-scale storage as Middle East conflict and Iran-related disruptions highlight energy security risks of fossil fuels and imported gas. Commenters note plummeting battery and solar costs, largely driven by Chinese manufacturing, making home electrification and storage economically attractive. But they worry about strategic dependence on China, volatile green-tech profits, and weak or hostile Western policy support. Discussion turns to how US and European industrial strategy, domestic manufacturing and regulation will shape long-term returns.

- Comment pulse  
  - Renewables seen as inevitable: China’s scale makes batteries and solar dirt-cheap, enabling full-electric homes—counterpoint: overreliance on Chinese supply chains creates new strategic vulnerabilities.  
  - Green-tech investing feels risky: chronic boom–bust cycles and unpredictable US/EU policy mean consumers benefit from low prices, but equity holders often don’t.  
  - Some see a US ‘slow adopter’ advantage to deploy mature tech at scale; others argue current leadership is openly hostile, deterring domestic manufacturing.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Energy security and cost, not only climate, are now primary drivers of capital flowing into renewables and storage.  
  - Impact: Western firms may lose manufacturing share but gain deployment jobs; consumers in volatile-fuel regions will electrify faster as economics improve.  
  - Watch next: Track tariffs, subsidies, and grid-upgrade policy; these will determine whether Chinese dominance persists or diversified regional supply chains emerge.
