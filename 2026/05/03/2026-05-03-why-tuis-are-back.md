# Why TUIs Are Back

- Score: 239 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48000028) | Link: https://wiki.alcidesfonseca.com/blog/why-tuis-are-back/

## TL;DR
- TUIs are resurging because mainstream GUI ecosystems are a mess: Windows keeps rebooting its GUI stack, Linux is fragmented by design, macOS is abandoning its own guidelines, and Electron dominates with heavy, inconsistent, keyboard-unfriendly apps. TUIs, in contrast, are fast, scriptable, run identically over SSH, and bypass OS-specific quirks. HN discussion splits between people drawn to “hacker” aesthetics, those preferring web UIs, and those arguing both are band-aids over failed native toolkits.

## Comment pulse
- TUIs as hacker theater: terminals feel “l33t” and focused; many tasks might be easier in IDEs, but aesthetics and anti-Electron sentiment drive adoption.  
- GUI fans: modern web apps beat TUIs for fonts, text editing, OS shortcuts, password managers—counterpoint: many web UIs also reinvent confusing interaction idioms.  
- Infra view: TUIs plus LLM CLIs and SSH give zero-install, cross-OS deployment; lack of simple, stable native toolkits pushes developers toward terminals and Electron.  

## LLM perspective
- View: TUIs are a symptom of toolkit fragmentation and Electron fatigue, not a fundamental rejection of graphical interfaces.  
- Impact: Developers, tool vendors, and OS teams need shared primitives: consistent menus, shortcuts, accessibility, and remote-friendly delivery without full browsers.  
- Watch next: Track projects like Flutter, GPUI, Rust/Go GUI kits, and LLM-first shells that might converge TUIs’ ergonomics with richer GUIs.
