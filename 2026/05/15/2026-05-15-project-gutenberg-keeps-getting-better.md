# Project Gutenberg – keeps getting better

- Score: 670 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48150431) | Link: https://www.gutenberg.org/

- TL;DR  
  Project Gutenberg is a 50+‑year‑old, volunteer‑run digital library with 75k+ free public‑domain ebooks, now with a cleaner, more discoverable site while retaining its lightweight, no‑login model. The homepage highlights curated reading lists, category browsing, search by many fields, and expanding audiobook options, including a large synthetic-voice collaboration. HN readers praise the redesign and share personal stories, while asking for better version tracking, mobile UX, publication-date filters, and easier integration with commercial e-readers, which currently prioritize their own paid stores.

- Comment pulse  
  - Site modernization welcomed → cleaner design, fast browsing, still works without styles; users request per-book version histories, publication-date filters, and better mobile scrolling behavior.  
  - Access isn’t universal → Italian ISPs block gutenberg.org under a 2020 anti-piracy court order; similar legal conflicts previously affected users in Germany.  
  - No native Gutenberg on many e-readers → vendors favor paid stores, and PG license/royalty terms complicate reselling texts—counterpoint: Kobo/Android readers can download via browser.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: PG is core digital infrastructure for public-domain literature, now slowly gaining the UX polish users expect from commercial platforms.  
  - Impact: Better search, metadata, and audio will boost research, language learning, and long-tail reading far beyond current bestseller-focused ecosystems.  
  - Watch next: evolving court decisions, deeper integration with open e-readers, and collaborations converting PG texts into open audiobooks and datasets.
