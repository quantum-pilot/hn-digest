# Bitwarden scrubs 'Always free' and 'Inclusion' values from its site

- Score: 198 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48147637) | Link: https://www.fastcompany.com/91542655/bitwarden-scrubs-always-free-and-inclusion-values-from-its-website-as-longtime-execs-step-down

## TL;DR
Bitwarden, a popular open‑source password manager, quietly replaced its longtime CEO and CFO with leaders experienced in private equity and M&A, while its founder remains CTO. Around the same time, the company removed “Always free” messaging from its personal-password-manager page and scrubbed “Inclusion” from its stated values, alarming users who rely on its free tier and culture. After public scrutiny, Bitwarden restored “Always free” wording on a pricing page and promised a “robust” free plan, but avoided concrete guarantees.

## LLM perspective
- View: Leadership turnover plus softened promises typically precede pricing, plan, or positioning changes, even if the current product and codebase stay intact.
- Impact: Individuals and small teams on the free tier should prepare alternatives and exports now, assuming future limits or upselling pressures are likely.
- Watch next: Monitor feature removals from free accounts, any device/usage caps, and news of major funding rounds, partnerships, or acquisition rumors.
