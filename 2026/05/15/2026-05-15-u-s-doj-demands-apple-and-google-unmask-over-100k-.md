# U.S. DOJ demands Apple and Google unmask over 100k users of car-tinkering app

- Score: 334 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48151383) | Link: https://macdailynews.com/2026/05/15/u-s-doj-demands-apple-and-google-unmask-over-100000-users-of-popular-car-tinkering-app-in-emissions-crackdown/

### TL;DR
DOJ, suing EZ Lynk for selling diesel “defeat device” hardware and its Auto Agent app, subpoenaed Apple, Google, Amazon and Walmart for personal data on potentially 100k+ users, claiming it needs witnesses about real‑world use. EZ Lynk, EFF and EPIC call the dragnet unconstitutional overreach that chills legitimate diagnostics and tuning. HN discussion centers on privacy vs emissions enforcement, the desire to nail a vendor by statistically proving predominant illegal use, and the risks of app‑store centralization.

### Comment pulse
- Civil-liberties concern: Dragnet naming every buyer without probable cause is seen as a fishing expedition. — counterpoint: prosecutors seek statistical proof of predominant illegal use.  
- Enforcement critique: Commenters prefer targeting visible polluters and expanding periodic emissions inspections instead of mass data grabs from app platforms.  
- Slippery-slope worry: Once allowed here, similar subpoenas could help manufacturers attack mods disabling tracking or other lawful tinkering, especially given centralized app stores.  

### LLM perspective
- View: This blends environmental enforcement with bulk digital surveillance; courts must clarify limits on witness-hunting via platform metadata.  
- Impact: Outcome will shape expectations for app-store intermediaries, right-to-repair tools, and how risky it is to buy gray-area hardware.  
- Watch next: Whether Apple/Google win to narrow subpoenas, and if Congress reacts with clearer standards for mass user-identification in civil cases.
