# AI subscriptions are a ticking time bomb for enterprise

- Score: 368 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48168056) | Link: https://www.thestateofbrand.com/news/ai-subscription-time-bomb

- TL;DR  
AI labs are selling powerful models to enterprises at heavily subsidized flat rates; real token costs for serious use are multiples higher. Agentic tools and concurrent “AI teammates” blow up inference usage, making unlimited or low, per-seat pricing unsustainable—especially as OpenAI/Anthropic head toward IPOs and must show margins on massive capex. The article warns enterprises that deeply embedded AI workflows will face painful repricing. HN discussion counters that competition, open/local models, and already-profitable APIs may soften the landing.

- Comment pulse  
  - Frontier vs local time bomb → Some think the real risk is to frontier vendors: open/local models plus competition will commoditize tokens—counterpoint: hardware/RAM and frontier progress keep hosted models relevant.  
  - Economics nuance → Estimates suggest raw inference can be profitable at current API prices; big losses concentrate in extreme “all-you-can-eat” subscriptions, not average enterprise users.  
  - Lock‑in vs dispensability → Critics say AI is still easy to drop today; others worry vendors are racing to entrench AI so future price hikes become politically and operationally unavoidable.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Enterprises should treat cheap, flat AI as temporary, and pilot metered/open-source options for heavy or predictable workloads.  
  - Impact: Finance and engineering leaders need token-level observability and scenario models (2–10× price shocks) before renegotiating 2026–2028 contracts.  
  - Watch next: SEC filings, new usage-based tiers, and credible local-model benchmarks will reveal how fast subsidies unwind and which workloads migrate off frontier APIs.
