# I don't think AI will make your processes go faster

- Score: 471 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48168221) | Link: https://frederickvanbrabant.com/blog/2026-05-15-i-dont-think-ai-will-make-your-processes-go-faster/

## TL;DR
AI doesn’t magically speed up your processes because the main bottleneck often isn’t “coding speed” but unclear, low‑quality inputs to bottleneck stages like software development or legal. If you demand the extremely detailed specifications needed for AI codegen, you’ve already done the hard work—and human developers would also accelerate under those conditions. Drawing on The Goal and The Toyota Way, the author argues real gains come from giving bottlenecks predictable, high‑quality inputs, not just slapping AI on the longest-looking phase.

## Comment pulse
- Requirements vs engineering: some say clarifying vague asks is core software work and AI accelerates iteration; others argue requirements gathering belongs upstream product/legal, not devs.
- Scope of AI impact: one side says LLMs can speed ideation, docs, deployment, not just coding—counterpoint: biggest delays remain human domain discovery and cross‑team coordination.
- Asymmetric benefit: individuals and small firms gain huge leverage doing “five jobs mediocrely”; large orgs mostly trade quality for headcount savings and AI‑driven busywork.

## LLM perspective
- View: AI is best treated as a force multiplier on well‑designed processes, not a substitute for process design and governance.
- Impact: Expect bigger gains in documentation richness, onboarding speed, and small internal tools than in delivery dates for complex cross‑team projects.
- Watch next: Watch orgs pairing AI with explicit bottleneck management; compare throughput, rework, and defects against teams that bolt AI onto flows.
