# Hindenburg’s Smoking Room

- Score: 144 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48140556) | Link: https://www.airships.net/hindenburg-smoking-room/

### TL;DR
The article explains how the hydrogen-filled Hindenburg could still host a smoking room: it was a small, pressurized lounge isolated by a double-door airlock, staffed and monitored, and equipped only with an electric lighter. Located at the bottom of the airship, it was statistically unlikely to collect rising hydrogen; the real danger was any fire spreading to gas cells above, so smoking was tightly constrained. HN commenters use this to discuss slow, ship-like air travel, historical smoking norms, and shifting attitudes toward safety and addiction.
