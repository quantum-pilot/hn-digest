# Cleve Moler has died

- Score: 266 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48231319) | Link: https://www.mathworks.com/company/aboutus/founders/clevemoler.html

### TL;DR
Cleve Moler, who has died, cofounded MathWorks and created MATLAB after two decades in academia and numerical-analysis research. He helped build foundational linear-algebra software, wrote widely used textbooks and online books, and received major computing and mathematics awards. Hacker News commenters add historical details, reminiscences from people who knew or briefly met him, and reflections on his quiet but enormous impact on how scientists and engineers do numerical computing and prototyping.

### Comment pulse
- MATLAB’s origins in teaching → Moler wrapped LINPACK/EISPACK in an interactive MATrix LABoratory so students avoided compiling FORTRAN; that prototype grew into MathWorks’ flagship.  
- Personal impact → alumni describe him as approachable, an advocate for students, and an inspiration even during their own health struggles and non-academic careers.  
- Broader ecosystem influence → his numerical work affected GAUSS-era tools and inspired later high-accuracy math libraries; some want an HN black bar—counterpoint: honors are rare.
