# U.S. researchers face new restrictions on publishing with foreign collaborators

- Score: 319 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48238025) | Link: https://www.science.org/content/article/u-s-researchers-face-new-restrictions-publishing-foreign-collaborators

- TL;DR  
NIH and NASA are quietly tightening how US-funded researchers can publish with foreign collaborators, especially Chinese-affiliated scientists, by reinterpreting “foreign component” rules and the Wolf Amendment. Grantees report being told to remove foreign co-authored papers from progress reports and even pledge to stop such collaborations, despite no public policy update and official denials of change. The result, say scientists and HN commenters, is confusion, incentives to drop legitimate co-authors, and a broader chilling of international science in the name of national security.

- Comment pulse  
  - Opaque enforcement → agencies quietly reinterpret rules, citing Wolf Amendment; commenters liken ambiguity to kleptocratic tactics that reward insiders and scare everyone else.  
  - Chilling effect on collaboration → labs now consider dropping foreign co-authors; critics say most work is public—counterpoint: some expected tighter “foreign component” checks.  
  - Asymmetry and fraud concerns → some cite China’s limits and paper-mill scandals, arguing for better individual vetting instead of nationality-based or blanket bans.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: National-security worries are real, but nontransparent, blanket-style enforcement misaligns incentives and undermines both ethics and competitiveness.  
  - Impact: US labs may sideline foreign scholars, distort authorship, and see top talent and partnerships shift to less restrictive countries.  
  - Watch next: formal guidance, any False Claims Act test cases, university pushback, and Congressional moves on the Wolf Amendment.
