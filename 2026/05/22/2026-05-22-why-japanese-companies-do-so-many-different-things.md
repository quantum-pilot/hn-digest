# Why Japanese companies do so many different things

- Score: 447 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48237163) | Link: https://davidoks.blog/p/why-japanese-companies-do-so-many

## TL;DR

Japanese firms like Toto, Kyocera, and Yamaha look bizarrely diversified because they’re built on a different “bundle” of organizational practices than US/European corporations. Post‑war Japan’s J‑firm model emphasizes lifetime employment, firm‑level unions, main‑bank finance, cross‑shareholding, and lateral shop‑floor problem‑solving. Companies exist chiefly to preserve the organization and its workforce, not maximize shareholder returns, so they diversify to create new roles and hedge sectors. This excelled at post‑war catch‑up manufacturing, but now impedes radical innovation and cleaning up zombie firms.
