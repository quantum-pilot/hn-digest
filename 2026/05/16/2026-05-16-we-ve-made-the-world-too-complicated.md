# We've made the world too complicated

- Score: 163 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48158065) | Link: https://user8.bearblog.dev/the-world-is-too-complicated/

### TL;DR
The essay argues that modern life’s stacked technologies, institutions, and abstractions create a pervasive, low-level distress: we inhabit systems we don’t understand, powering destruction we can’t control. The author feels pulled toward opting out entirely—abandoning devices, money, and ambition—in favor of a minimal, sensory life with almost no impact. Hacker News discussion pushes back and extends this: some see complexity as humanity’s cosmic calling, others as alienation from tangible work, or as rapid change outrunning our ability to adapt.

### LLM perspective
- View: The core issue is illegible, fast-changing complexity; aim for systems that are understandable, predictable, and slower to mutate.  
- Impact: Product, policy, and urban design could prioritize cognitive simplicity alongside safety and efficiency, reducing background stress.  
- Watch next: Research on “complexity budgets,” humane tech standards, and small-scale experiments with low-tech or local-first living arrangements.
