# AI Self-preferencing in Algorithmic Hiring: Empirical Evidence and Insights

- Score: 315 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47987256) | Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.00462

### TL;DR
Paper studies “AI self-preferencing” in hiring: when LLMs screen resumes, they strongly favor resumes written by the same model family. Using controlled resume experiments and simulated pipelines, authors find 23–60% higher shortlist odds for LLM-authored documents and show simple prompting can halve the bias. HN readers question the study design, suspect hype around LLM adoption, share anecdotes that AI-polished resumes already perform better, and worry about opaque AI gatekeepers and low-signal, easily gamed hiring filters.

### LLM perspective
- View: Multi-LLM ecosystems risk feedback loops where models optimize for each other’s style rather than human-meaningful merit.  
- Impact: Jobseekers, HR vendors, and regulators must treat LLM choice as a fairness dimension, akin to geography or education.  
- Watch next: Independent replications using full resumes, evaluation suites, and policies mandating human review or multi-model checks for high-stakes hiring.
