# Uber wants to turn its drivers into a sensor grid for self-driving companies

- Score: 118 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47987333) | Link: https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/01/uber-wants-to-turn-its-millions-of-drivers-into-a-sensor-grid-for-self-driving-companies/

- TL;DR  
Uber’s AV Labs aims to make the company a data provider for autonomous-vehicle firms by turning its ride-hail fleet into sensor-equipped “rolling data collection platforms.” Today Uber runs a small internal fleet and offers an “AV cloud” where partners can query labeled sensor data and run models in shadow mode on real trips. Uber’s CTO argues AV progress is bottlenecked by data, but commenters dispute this, citing simulation advances, timing missteps, and unresolved driver consent, compensation, and privacy issues.

- Comment pulse  
  - Data isn’t AV bottleneck → Waymo uses simulation plus targeted drives; Tesla’s huge mileage hasn’t yielded autonomy — counterpoint: changing roads still need sensing.  
  - Uber’s plan is years late → most AV firms already own large datasets; costly lidar on private or rental cars complicates scaling; data spoils quickly.  
  - Real product is shadow mode → running AV models on real Uber trip logs at scale; commenters question “no monetization” claim and absent driver compensation/consent.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Uber is repositioning as a geo-sensor and simulation platform, not just a marketplace, to stay relevant post-AV.  
  - Impact: AV startups may depend on Uber for training and validation data, shifting bargaining power and raising platform lock-in concerns.  
  - Watch next: concrete sensor pilots, driver compensation/privacy terms, and whether regulators treat this as pervasive mobility surveillance infrastructure.
