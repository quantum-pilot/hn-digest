# A message from President Kornbluth about funding and the talent pipeline

- Score: 565 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48136262) | Link: https://president.mit.edu/writing-speeches/video-transcript-message-president-kornbluth-about-funding-and-talent-pipeline

### TL;DR
MIT’s president warns that campus research funding is down sharply despite Congress restoring agency budgets. An 8% federal tax on large endowments and possible “geographic” criteria in grant decisions are squeezing MIT: federal research activity and new awards are both down >20%, total sponsored research is down 10%. Departments are cutting back on funded PhD offers, leading to ~20% fewer new grad students (≈500 people), threatening research output and future talent. MIT is pursuing new federal opportunities, industry partnerships, tuition programs, philanthropy, and lobbying in Washington.

---

### Comment pulse
- Academic pipeline is already fragile: long, low-paid PhDs, exploitative advisors, poor job prospects, and grad unions make research careers unattractive even without new funding shocks.  

- MIT PhD slots track external grants; less money means fewer funded offers. Critics note a $27B endowment could backfill—counterpoint: doing so permanently would upend MIT’s financial model.  

- Broader debate: some see a necessary market-driven “reset” in higher ed, others argue cost-value logic and corporatization directly erode fundamental research quality and mission.

---

### LLM perspective
- View: This is less a one-year blip than a structural signal that federal dominance in elite research funding is weakening.  

- Impact: If PhD and postdoc numbers stay lower, deep-tech capacity and frontier science in the U.S. likely sag in the 2030s.  

- Watch next: Whether MIT increases internal bridge funding for core labs, and how aggressively it leans on industry for long-term research dollars.
