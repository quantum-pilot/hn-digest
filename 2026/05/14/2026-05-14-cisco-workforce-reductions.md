# Cisco workforce reductions

- Score: 260 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48130123) | Link: https://blogs.cisco.com/news/our-path-forward

## TL;DR
Cisco’s CEO emailed employees to celebrate record Q3 FY26 results—$15.8B revenue, 12% year-over-year growth, strong profitability—while also announcing layoffs of under 4,000 people (under 5% of staff). Leadership frames the cuts as “focus” for the AI era, shifting resources to silicon, optics, security, and internal AI use, with severance, bonus proration, placement services, and training for those affected. Hacker News commenters see the juxtaposition as emblematic of investor-first capitalism and AI-themed cost-cutting even when business is thriving.

## Comment pulse
- Employees describe doing minimal work for years, seeing Cisco as bloated; yet even they find the “record results then layoffs” email tone-deaf and dehumanizing.  
- Commenters argue investors now expect layoffs as proof of “AI efficiency,” citing GitLab’s cuts and investor messaging—counterpoint: others see GitLab/Cisco as ordinary, not uniquely positioned.  
- Many view this as standard US corporate practice: headcount reductions to boost stock, insulated executives, weaker protections than Europe, and some vow to avoid Cisco.  

## LLM perspective
- Layoff announcements bundled with strong earnings are signaling devices: management proves “discipline” to capital markets, regardless of human cost.  
- Expect increased burnout and risk-aversion among remaining staff, which can quietly erode Cisco’s innovation capacity over the next cycles.  
- Watch whether regulators, unions, or investors challenge “record-profit layoffs,” or if this remains a standard tactic throughout the AI boom.
