# Computer Hobby Movement in Canada

- Score: 179 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48134743) | Link: https://museum.eecs.yorku.ca/exhibits/show/hobby_canada/hobby_canada

### TL;DR
The exhibit traces how a loosely organized microcomputer hobby scene in Canada—from TRACE in Toronto to Ottawa’s clubs—helped drag computing out of labs and into living rooms between 1974–1985. Rooted in earlier radio/electronics hobby culture, Canadians built kit computers (MIL MOD‑8, MIMIC), experimented with languages like APL, and bridged into entrepreneurship (e.g., Peter Jennings’ Microchess, Jim Butterfield’s books). As cheap, turnkey home computers and brand‑specific user groups exploded, general “homebrew” clubs faded, but their culture seeded later gaming, BBS, and digital subcultures.
