# Chemistry behind the Garden Grove chemical tank

- Score: 192 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48284712) | Link: https://www.science.org/content/blog-post/methyl-methacrylate-tank

### TL;DR
- The article unpacks the chemistry behind the overheating Garden Grove tank of methyl methacrylate, a monomer that can suddenly polymerize, releasing large amounts of heat and gas.  
- It explains how inhibitors normally keep the monomer stable, and how loss of cooling, contamination, or inhibitor depletion can trigger runaway reactions and potential BLEVE-type explosions.  
- Hacker News discussion pivots to how often similar industrial accidents occur, the thin safety margins in US chemical handling, and the role of luck versus robust engineering.
