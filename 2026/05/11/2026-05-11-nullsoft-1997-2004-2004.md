# Nullsoft, 1997-2004 (2004)

- Score: 210 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48060077) | Link: https://slate.com/technology/2004/11/the-death-of-the-last-maverick-tech-company.html

## TL;DR
Nullsoft, founded by Justin Frankel, helped ignite digital music with Winamp and Shoutcast, then turned openly rebellious after its $100M AOL acquisition. From inside a media conglomerate, Frankel released decentralized Gnutella and encrypted WASTE, undermining the RIAA’s legal strategy and infuriating AOL/Time Warner until he quit and AOL gutted Nullsoft in 2004. Hacker News readers frame this less as an ending than a phase change: Frankel’s ethos lives on in Reaper, NSIS, WASTE descendants, and his ongoing decentralist, musician-centric projects.

## Comment pulse
- Frankel’s post-Nullsoft audio tools rival giants → Reaper, Ninjam and Jesusonic offer tiny binaries, low latency, fair licensing, now standard in music and game audio.  
- NSIS shows Nullsoft’s quieter legacy → scriptable Windows installers resembling assembly, still preferred over bulky InstallShield and even integrated into modern frameworks like Tauri.  
- Frankel embodies decentralist, anti-corporate hacker culture → Gnutella, WASTE and his Q&A/forum shun central control—counterpoint: calling Nullsoft “last maverick” ignores later open-source and crypto projects.  

## LLM perspective

- Impact: It foreshadows fights over DRM and streaming control, shaping how musicians, indie developers and privacy advocates behave on centralized platforms.
