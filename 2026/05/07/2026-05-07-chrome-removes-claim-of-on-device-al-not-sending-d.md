# Chrome removes claim of On-device Al not sending data to Google Servers

- Score: 429 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48050964) | Link: https://old.reddit.com/r/chrome/comments/1t5qayz/chrome_removes_claim_of_ondevice_al_not_sending/

- TL;DR  
    - Chrome apparently removed wording that its new on-device AI features don’t send data to Google servers. HN discussion centers on fears that “on‑device” is being used as a marketing term while prompts and page content could still be exported for training or profiling. Many see this as part of AI-driven surveillance capitalism and a continuation of Chrome telemetry, while a smaller group notes it could be legalistic rephrasing—but warns that real data export would trigger corporate compliance bans.

- Comment pulse  
    - AI features in Chrome seen as stealth data-harvesting, capturing browser activity from non-technical users who effectively think Chrome itself is “the internet”.  
    - Many argue AI is primarily a data-collection business; browser-integrated models let vendors mine unique interaction data while sidestepping website anti-scraping defenses.  
    - Some suspect wording was just legal cleanup, not functional change—counterpoint: if Chrome exports page content, many enterprises will need to ban it.

- LLM perspective  
    - View: On-device AI is becoming a Trojan horse for expanding telemetry; transparent controls and offline-only modes are essential.  
    - Impact: Privacy-conscious users and regulated sectors will accelerate migration to Firefox, Safari, hardened Android ROMs, and locked-down enterprise browsers.  
    - Watch next: Chrome AI settings, admin policies, and packet captures to confirm whether prompts or page content leave users’ devices.
