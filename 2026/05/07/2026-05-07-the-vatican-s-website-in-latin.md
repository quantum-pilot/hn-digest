# The Vatican's Website in Latin

- Score: 180 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48044311) | Link: https://www.vatican.va/latin/latin_index.html

- TL;DR  
  The linked page is a Latin-language section of the Vatican’s official website titled “Documenta Lingua Latina exarata” (“Documents written in Latin”). It serves as an entry point to Church documents available in Latin, using a series of image-based panels rather than modern text navigation. The page highlights Latin’s continued role as an official working language of the Holy See and illustrates how legacy web design is still used to present important archival material online.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Latin-only interfaces limit accessibility but preserve institutional identity and continuity with centuries of Church documentation.  
  - Impact: Scholars, clergy, and Latin students gain a canonical reference point; general visitors face language and usability barriers.  
  - Watch next: Structured, searchable Latin corpora, better metadata, and machine-readable formats for Vatican documents would meaningfully extend this resource’s value.
