# Motherboard sales 'collapse' amid unprecedented shortages fueled by AI

- Score: 239 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48050540) | Link: https://www.tomshardware.com/pc-components/motherboards/motherboard-sales-collapse-by-more-than-25-percent-as-chipmakers-strangle-enthusiast-pc-market-to-build-more-ai-chips-asus-projected-to-sell-5-million-fewer-boards-in-2025-gigabyte-msi-and-asrock-also-expected-to-see-reduced-sales-numbers

### TL;DR
Global motherboard shipments from Asus, Gigabyte, MSI and ASRock are projected to fall ~25–37% in 2026, as AI infrastructure demand diverts wafer capacity from consumer CPUs, DRAM and SSDs. Component shortages and price spikes, combined with few compelling platform upgrades (AM5 continuity, delayed Intel Nova Lake, no new Nvidia gaming GPUs), are making DIY builds uneconomical. Enthusiasts and even enterprises increasingly repair or reuse older systems, while board makers pivot profitable production toward AI server hardware.

### Comment pulse

- Some argue AI isn’t what made motherboards pricey; issue is component inflation and incompatibility, so average RAM/CPU specs may plateau or fall for years.
