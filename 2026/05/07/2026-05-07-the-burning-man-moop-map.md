# The Burning Man MOOP Map

- Score: 510 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48049653) | Link: https://www.not-ship.com/burning-man-moop/

### TL;DR
Burning Man’s MOOP Map turns its “Leave No Trace” principle into a quantified, public accountability system. After 70,000 people leave Black Rock City, ~150 crew members walk nearly 4,000 acres in line sweeps, logging and photographing every scrap of “Matter Out of Place.” The data drives cleanup tactics, camp placement, and compliance with strict Bureau of Land Management thresholds (≤1 sq ft debris/acre, sampled at 120 points). Over ~20 years, per-person debris has steadily fallen, a trend commenters credit to strong culture, hard cleanup work, and regulatory pressure, while also noting challenges like extreme weather and off-site trash impacts.

### Comment pulse
- MOOP crew describes photographing every item, green-screen pixel counting against a 0.00229% debris limit—physically brutal but deeply satisfying work; some wonder about computer-vision assistance.

- Heavy rain and mud can hide debris and destroy roads, turning cleanup into raking and sifting mud; veterans fear a future storm stranding tens of thousands.

- Participants highlight Burning Man’s fully attendee-created programming and unusually rigorous cleanup norms—counterpoint: some trash is effectively exported to Reno/Sparks instead of the playa.
