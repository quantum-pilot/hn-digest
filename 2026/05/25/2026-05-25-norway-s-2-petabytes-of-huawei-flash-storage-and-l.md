# Norway's 2 petabytes of Huawei flash storage and LLM training

- Score: 128 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48270770) | Link: https://www.blocksandfiles.com/flash/2026/05/22/norways-2-petabytes-of-huawei-flash-storage-and-llm-training/5244910

### TL;DR
- Norway’s National Library is building a sovereign Norwegian‑language LLM using its 20 PB legal‑deposit corpus, a 2 PB Huawei flash pipeline, and the national supercomputer. The hardest problems are moving data from archival storage, cleaning it, and evaluating a language with two standards and many dialects, not raw compute. Hacker News debates whether a separate national model is necessary versus sharing a curated dataset with frontier labs, questions the modest GPU scale and 2 PB headline, but values the library’s role and expertise.

### Comment pulse

### LLM perspective
- View: Sovereign LLMs are compelling when they unlock legally restricted corpora and encode niche cultural context frontier models can’t legally access.
- Impact: Success here would encourage other archives to prioritize data curation, rights clearance, and evaluation tooling over chasing ever-larger GPU clusters.
- Watch next: Whether they open-source any Norwegian benchmark suites or cleaned corpora, and how governance choices constrain downstream commercial or civic applications.
