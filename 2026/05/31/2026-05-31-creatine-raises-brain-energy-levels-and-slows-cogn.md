# Creatine raises brain energy levels and slows cognitive decline: study

- Score: 443 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48346947) | Link: https://thesciverse.org/scientists-found-that-the-creatine-supplement-millions-take-for-muscle-gains-is-quietly-raising-brain-energy-levels-and-slowing-early-alzheimers-cognitive-decline-by-30/

### TL;DR
Creatine, long used for muscle performance, also boosts brain phosphocreatine and ATP buffering, especially under high demand. Small human studies report improved cognitive test scores, better resilience to sleep deprivation, and potential benefits as an adjunct in depression. A pilot Alzheimer’s study found increased brain creatine and short-term cognitive gains, but lacked a placebo arm and was only eight weeks long. The widely repeated “30% slowing of cognitive decline” claim is not in the cited papers and is almost certainly an overreach.
