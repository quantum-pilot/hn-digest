## TL;DR
Evans frames generative AI as the next major platform shift, now defined less by clever demos than by a $700B+ capex race in chips and data centers. He argues frontier models are converging in capability and lack strong network effects, so they’ll likely commoditize into infrastructure, with most value shifting “up the stack” into apps, workflows, proprietary data, and new questions unlocked by cheap probabilistic automation. Usage is broad but shallow, enterprise impact is early and uneven, and historical analogies suggest deep uncertainty about where value and disruption will ultimately land.
