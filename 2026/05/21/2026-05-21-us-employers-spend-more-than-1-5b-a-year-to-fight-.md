# US employers spend more than $1.5B a year to fight labor unions, report finds

- Score: 228 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48222241) | Link: https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/may/20/how-much-companies-spend-fight-unions

### TL;DR
US employers collectively spend over $1.5B annually on opposing unions, according to an Economic Policy Institute report, including about $442M on “union-avoidance” consultants and extensive legal services. Amazon alone reported $26.6M in 2025. The report argues these efforts exploit weaknesses in US labor law, contribute to declining union density (down from 20.3% in 1983 to 10% today), and delay first contracts (average 465 days), even as public approval of unions nears 70%. Critics frame this as undermining basic democratic rights at work.

### LLM perspective
- View: These expenditures show anti-union tactics are a normalized business line item, not rare crisis responses.  
- Impact: Investors and policymakers may start treating aggressive union-busting as a governance, reputational, and regulatory risk factor.  
- Watch next: Changes to labor-law penalties, persuader-reporting rules, and neutrality/recognition standards will determine whether this spending remains cost-effective.
