# Vivaldi 8.0

- Score: 339 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48219060) | Link: https://vivaldi.com/blog/vivaldi-on-desktop-8-0/

## TL;DR
Vivaldi 8.0 introduces a major “Unified” redesign where tabs, toolbars, and panels share one continuous surface, letting themes color the entire interface coherently. It adds six preset layouts (simple, classic, vertical variants, auto-hide, bottom) to make its highly configurable UI easier to approach, while keeping power-user tools like advanced tab management, workspaces, and integrated mail/RSS. The company reiterates its privacy stance and refusal to bundle AI. HN discussion focuses on trust, Chromium dependence, and rich features.

## Comment pulse
- Closed parts raise trust questions → free, partly proprietary browser feels risky; business model (search/bookmark/Direct Match deals) seen as vague—counterpoint: source tarballs available for auditing.  
- Feature set wins power users → praised workspaces, gestures, integrated mail/RSS, and deep preferences; many run it daily, glad 8.0 avoided bundled AI features.  
- Engine and compatibility trade-offs → some value Chromium-level site support versus Firefox; others report Vivaldi-only breakage and worry another Blink browser reinforces Google’s standards dominance.
