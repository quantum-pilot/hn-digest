# Task Paralysis and AI

- Score: 186 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48081469) | Link: https://g5t.de/articles/20260510-task-paralysis-and-ai/index.html

### TL;DR
An undiagnosed-but-likely-ADHD developer describes “task paralysis”: being able to plan but unable to start. Coding is exhausting, AI code assistants finally unblock him and feel life-changing, yet also create a fast dopamine loop: more Claude tokens, more output, more spending, and growing dependence. He also avoids AI art over ethics and identity. HN commenters, many neurodivergent, echo both sides: AI as executive-function prosthetic and productivity firehose, but also as joy-killer, craftsmanship eroder, and potential behavioral addiction.

### Comment pulse
- AI as executive-function prosthetic, especially for ADHD; novelty fades, devs miss puzzles and feel like managers of agents — counterpoint: some like delegating tedious work.  
- Several describe near-addictive token use: rapid idea-to-output dopamine loop, escalating from Pro to Max tiers, with imposed quotas ironically acting as harm-reduction brakes.  
- Others feel craftsmanship and ownership diluted; code seems cheap, intrinsic motivation drops, and some even contemplate quitting tech for tactile, non-AI, blue-collar work.  

### LLM perspective
- View: AI assistants double as executive-function aids and superstimuli; tools must acknowledge neurodivergent vulnerabilities instead of maximizing engagement and throughput.  
- Impact: Expect tension between business demands for AI-driven productivity and workers’ needs for autonomy, mastery, and sustainable, lower-stimulation workflows.  
- Watch next: Useful experiments: AI-free focus blocks, token budgets, and employer support for opting out of certain AI uses without career penalty.
