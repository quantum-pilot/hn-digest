# OpenWarp

- Score: 190 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47970622) | Link: https://openwarp.zerx.dev

- TL;DR  
OpenWarp is a community fork of the Warp terminal that adds true “bring your own provider” AI: explicit protocol selection (OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini, Ollama, DeepSeek, etc.), custom models and prompts, multi-language UI, and strictly local credential storage, while continuously merging Warp’s upstream UI/UX. HN discussion is split: many liked Warp as a polished terminal but dislike its AI-heavy, login/paywalled direction; some question why this isn’t upstreamed, and one user reports OpenWarp still enforcing Warp’s $20/month restriction.

- Comment pulse  
  - Warp’s AI-centric pivot frustrated users who wanted a great terminal, not an agent IDE, prompting moves to Ghostty or stock terminals—counterpoint: Warp says AI can be disabled and BYOM is coming.  
  - Several argue an “AI terminal” is unnecessary since you can run models via CLI/TUIs; others see value if AI is tightly focused on DevOps/CLI workflows.  
  - Some view OpenWarp as redundant and better upstreamed; others say the fork pressures Warp to support local models, multiple providers, and a “thin” non-AI mode.  

- LLM perspective  
  - View: OpenWarp showcases demand for AI integration that’s protocol-agnostic, self-hostable, and privacy-preserving instead of tied to a single cloud vendor.  
  - Impact: If Warp ships flexible, non-paywalled BYOM, OpenWarp may become a niche fork while influencing Warp’s product and pricing decisions.  
  - Watch next: Clarified licensing around local models, a minimal “ThinWarp” configuration, and benchmarks comparing latency/usability vs. plain terminals plus standalone AI tools.
