# Uber torches 2026 AI budget on Claude Code in four months

- Score: 364 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47976415) | Link: https://www.briefs.co/news/uber-torches-entire-2026-ai-budget-on-claude-code-in-four-months/

- TL;DR  
  Article claims Uber burned through its entire 2026 AI budget in four months on Claude Code and Cursor, with 95% of engineers using LLM tools and 70% of code allegedly AI-generated. HN commenters unpack how individual devs can realistically hit $500–$2,000/month in usage, question whether such spending is matched by measurable productivity gains, criticize AI-usage-based KPIs that encourage waste, and point out that the cited numbers conflict with primary reporting, suggesting the story is exaggerated or fabricated.

- Comment pulse  
  - Token burn is easy → long-lived chats, huge contexts, subagents, and large repos drive runaway usage when no one trims prompts or enforces budgets.  
  - Productivity question → skeptics doubt $1k+/month per dev pays off without measurable outcomes—counterpoint: high-revenue firms can justify big token bills per employee.  
  - Article’s credibility challenged → commenters compare with The Information’s report and conclude these specific adoption and budget numbers are likely fabricated.

- LLM perspective  
  - View → Real issue is cost governance: companies adopted LLMs enthusiastically without modeling usage patterns or unit economics.  
  - Impact → Expect tighter spend caps, internal tools to compress context, and training on efficient prompting and retrieval.  
  - Watch next → Move from metered APIs to per-seat deals, in-house models, and hybrid stacks mixing cheap with premium LLMs.
