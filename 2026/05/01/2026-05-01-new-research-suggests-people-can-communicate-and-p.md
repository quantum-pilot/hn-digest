# New research suggests people can communicate and practice skills while dreaming

- Score: 200 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47977748) | Link: https://www.newyorker.com/culture/annals-of-inquiry/its-possible-to-learn-in-our-sleep-should-we

## TL;DR
Modern research shows we can modestly influence learning and behavior during sleep, especially by reactivating memories with cues (sounds, smells) and by practicing tasks in lucid dreams. Experiments have even enabled two‑way communication with sleeping lucid dreamers, who answer math problems with prearranged eye movements. But benefits are narrow—more like targeted reinforcement and creative problem solving than “learn Mandarin overnight.” Scientists warn that pushing sleep to serve productivity can disrupt its core restorative and memory‑cleaning roles and risks “colonizing” dreams.
