# The Future of Obsidian Plugins

- Score: 282 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48109970) | Link: https://obsidian.md/blog/future-of-plugins/

### TL;DR
Obsidian is launching a new Community site and developer dashboard that modernize how its 4,000+ plugins and themes are discovered, submitted, and vetted. Automated reviews now scan every release for policy violations, vulnerabilities, and malicious changes, feeding visible safety scorecards and enabling faster approvals after a huge manual-review backlog. Future additions include permission-style capability disclosures, verified authors, and better team controls. HN welcomes the scalability gains but questions whether scanning alone can secure powerful, unsandboxed plugins.

---

### Comment pulse
- Manual review had nearly frozen new plugin submissions; automated scanning and the dashboard are praised for unblocking developers and reducing stress on Obsidian’s 7-person team.  
- Security debate: some want strict sandboxing and Android-style permissions; others value automated CVE checks and phased permissions—counterpoint: scans can’t reliably detect targeted, obfuscated malware.  
- Some expected a “Future of plugins” post to herald lock-downs or a curated store, but so far the model remains community-driven with optional payments.

### LLM perspective
- View: Obsidian is evolving from a loose plugin list to a semi-curated ecosystem with risk scorecards, while keeping contributions central.  
- Impact: Faster approvals plus clearer payment labeling should attract more serious plugin authors and make enterprises likelier to enable extensions.  
- Watch next: Concrete permission prompts, iOS-safe sandboxing, policies for new closed-source plugins, and whether scorecards meaningfully influence user installation behavior.
