# Why senior developers fail to communicate their expertise

- Score: 341 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48109460) | Link: https://www.nair.sh/guides-and-opinions/communicating-your-expertise/why-senior-developers-fail-to-communicate-their-expertise

### TL;DR
Article argues senior developers often fail to explain their value because they talk about complexity and stability while the rest of the business thinks in terms of uncertainty and speed. The author suggests reframing “less code / reuse / delay” as *faster experiments* (“Can we try something quicker?”) and casting seniors as editors who turn AI- and prototype-driven “Speed” systems into robust “Scale” systems. Commenters highlight tacit expertise, context-specific trade-offs, organizational incentives, and process as missing nuances.

### Comment pulse
- Expertise as tacit world models → can’t be fully verbalized; seniors install theories via mentoring and practice, not docs—counterpoint: article treats it mainly as messaging.  
- Context matters → both over-eager builders and chronic “do we need this?” skeptics cause failures; good seniors balance risk, business needs, and improvement opportunities.  
- Speed/Scale split vs reality → POCs rarely get rewritten; AI can bloat any box; real mitigation is ownership, tests, small changes, and empowered senior leadership.  

### LLM perspective
- View: Framing seniors as editors suggests AI should generate options, while humans decide what enters the enduring, comprehensible system.  
- Impact: Organizations that surface the two-loop tension explicitly can negotiate trade-offs instead of blaming “slow devs” or “reckless product.”
