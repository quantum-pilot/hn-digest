# US inflation jumps to 3.8% as energy costs surge from Iran war

- Score: 228 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48108313) | Link: https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c202pgxx89lo

### TL;DR
US inflation accelerated to 3.8% year‑on‑year in April, the fastest since 2023, driven largely by soaring energy prices after the Iran war choked the Strait of Hormuz. Gasoline averages $4.50/gal, airfares jumped 20%, and wage growth (3.6%) has fallen behind prices again. Commenters frame this as a strategic US failure that enriched oil producers, debate how much inflation is really about fuel versus profit shares, compare headline CPI with lived price shocks, and question buoyant stock markets.

### Comment pulse
- US lost from Iran war → ceded Hormuz leverage, exposed military limits, alienated allies, while domestic oil profits rose—counterpoint: energy shock could accelerate decarbonization.  
- Wage earners squeezed → real wages fell ~0.5%, core inflation ~2.8%, and labor’s income share hit record lows despite strong productivity.  
- Headline CPI understates pain → essentials like milk show 10–15% jumps, store pricing games widen disparities, so individual inflation often exceeds national averages.
