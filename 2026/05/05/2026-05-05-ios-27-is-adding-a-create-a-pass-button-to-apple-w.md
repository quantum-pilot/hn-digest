# iOS 27 is adding a 'Create a Pass' button to Apple Wallet

- Score: 372 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48021561) | Link: https://walletwallet.alen.ro/blog/ios-27-wallet-create-pass/

- TL;DR  
  - iOS 27 will add a “Create a Pass” option in Apple Wallet, letting users scan QR codes or design basic standard/membership/event passes without any developer account or PassKit setup. This flips Wallet from merchant-issued to user-curated, especially helping gyms, clubs, libraries, and small venues that never built Wallet support. It partially undercuts pass-generator tools like WalletWallet, while HN discussion focuses on Apple’s long-neglected Wallet UX, alternative third‑party apps, and how this merely catches iOS up to Google Wallet.

- Comment pulse  
  - Wallet’s card UI is confusing with similar-looking cards; some defend its “physical wallet” metaphor as friendlier for older or less technical users.  
  - Many see 14 years of weak adoption as Apple’s UX/business failure, not developer apathy—Apple could’ve offered simpler standards or a no-code pass portal.  
  - Users already relied on photos or apps like Pass2U/Pass4Wallet; commenters note Google Wallet has long supported DIY passes, so Apple is just closing a gap.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: This shifts Wallet from brand-controlled artifacts to user-controlled storage for any scannable relationship.  
  - Impact: Small organizations gain lightweight digital passes; pass-generator startups must differentiate on cross-platform, sharing, and richer metadata.  
  - Watch next: Whether Apple enables export/sync, richer barcode formats, lock-screen behaviors, and parity or competition with Google Wallet’s existing feature set.
