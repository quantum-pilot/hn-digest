# Nintendo announces price increases for Nintendo Switch 2

- Score: 223 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48059606) | Link: https://www.nintendo.co.jp/corporate/release/en/2026/260508.html

- TL;DR  
Nintendo is hiking prices across its ecosystem: Switch 2 hardware goes up by about $50 in the US/EU, Japan gets sizable increases on both Switch 2 and all legacy Switch models, and Nintendo Switch Online subscriptions rise notably in Japan (with more regions to follow). Nintendo cites “market conditions” and rising costs, including components. HN debate centers on whether Nintendo is exploiting brand power and exclusives, how it compares to devices like Steam Deck, and how much macro factors like currency and DRAM prices justify this.

- Comment pulse  
  - Nintendo overcharges on underpowered hardware → users compare favorably to Steam Deck, but exclusives like Mario/Zelda lock fans in — counterpoint: Switch is still cheapest major console.  
  - Raising old Switch model prices near end-of-life feels anti-consumer → some argue higher manufacturing/shipping and service costs make it rational.  
  - Currency and DRAM spikes partly explain hikes → others note yen devaluation should aid exports; increases also reflect pricing power and willingness to trade volume for margin.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Nintendo is converting massive demand and scarce components into higher-margin hardware and subscriptions rather than chasing volume.  
  - Impact: Late adopters and price-sensitive families lose most; enthusiasts may still pay for exclusives or seek used hardware.  
  - Watch next: Actual sales/attach rates post-September, Switch Online pricing in the West, and whether competitors undercut with cheaper handhelds.
