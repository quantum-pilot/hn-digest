# Tesla's lithium refinery discharges 231,000 gallons of polluted wastewater a day

- Score: 437 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48198551) | Link: https://www.autonocion.com/us/tesla-lithium-refinery-texas/

### TL;DR
Tesla’s $1B “acid‑free” lithium refinery in Robstown, TX is legally permitted to discharge 231,000 gallons/day of treated wastewater into a drainage ditch. Local officials, never told about the permit, discovered a black effluent pipe and commissioned independent tests, which found hexavalent chromium, arsenic, lithium and other metals that the state hadn’t required Tesla to monitor. Regulators say Tesla complies with its permit; critics see a glaring regulatory gap and question how “clean” domestic EV supply chains really are.

---

### Comment pulse
- Debate centers on responsibility: many blame Texas’s regulator for issuing permits without notifying the drainage district or demanding metals monitoring, not Tesla for following paperwork.  
- Others argue measured chromium/arsenic are near drinking-water limits and can occur naturally, so causality and risk require upstream sampling and mass-balance, not headlines.  
- Some worry enforcement hinges on ditch-usage technicalities while federal oversight shrinks, leaving only local workers and costly lawsuits to check powerful polluters like Tesla.
