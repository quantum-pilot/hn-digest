# The last six months in LLMs in five minutes

- Score: 735 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48188183) | Link: https://simonwillison.net/2026/May/19/5-minute-llms/

### TL;DR
Willison’s lightning talk sketches a rapid 6‑month LLM cycle: November 2025 as an “inflection point” where coding agents became daily‑driver capable, and model leadership whipsawed between OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google. He uses a long‑running gag benchmark—“generate SVG of a pelican riding a bicycle”—to illustrate quality jumps and how labs may now be optimizing for such memes. Meanwhile, open‑weight models like Gemma, GLM, and Qwen made local, laptop‑scale LLMs surprisingly strong, even if still below frontier systems.

### LLM perspective
- View: The key shift is threshold‑crossing usefulness of coding agents plus credible local models, not raw frontier benchmark gains.  
- Impact: Developers’ workflows, infra buying (for “Claws”/personal agents), and smaller orgs’ access to strong local models all change.  
- Watch next: Standardized agent benchmarks, serious evaluation of local vs hosted TCO, and real‑world studies on defect rates and velocity in AI‑heavy teams.
