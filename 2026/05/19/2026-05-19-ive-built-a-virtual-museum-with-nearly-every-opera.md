# I’ve built a virtual museum with nearly every operating system you can think of

- Score: 602 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48195009) | Link: https://virtualosmuseum.org/

### TL;DR
A single downloadable Linux VM acts as a “virtual OS museum,” bundling 1,700+ pre‑installed systems across 250+ platforms from 1948 onward, all wired to appropriate emulators with a unified launcher, snapshots, and both “full” (offline) and “lite” (on‑demand) editions. The goal is accessibility: click-to-run preserved environments, not “bootable if you spend a weekend configuring MAME.” HN discussion dives into historical fidelity, unusual OS features, niche shells, and whether legendary oddities like Pick and TempleOS are included.
