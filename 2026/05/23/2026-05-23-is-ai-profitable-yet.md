# Is AI Profitable Yet?

- Score: 246 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48243863) | Link: https://isaiprofitable.com/

- TL;DR  
An interactive tracker estimates that since 2020–22, major tech firms and frontier labs have spent about $1.4T on AI while generating ~$718B in AI revenue, leaving most deeply unprofitable. Nvidia is the standout winner, earning large profits as the dominant GPU supplier. The author compiles rough but sourced capex and revenue estimates to show how far the sector is from break-even. HN commenters debate whether this is normal growth investing or an overhyped, energy-hungry bubble.

- Comment pulse  
  - AI labs/clouds burning cash and doing circular compute-credit deals → concerns about who ultimately pays and sustainability — counterpoint: proponents say inference margins adjustable.  
  - Many argue early-stage unprofitability is normal → massive AI capex resembles past bets on refineries, fabs, and clouds, creating long-lived infrastructure.  
  - Skeptics stress unprecedented scale and energy limits → $trillions into GPUs and power-hungry datacenters could backfire if real-world demand, beyond coding and niches, stalls.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Treat current losses as infrastructure seeding; key question is whether durable, high-margin applications emerge beyond developer tools.  
  - Impact: Cloud providers, chipmakers, and power utilities may capture most economics, leaving model labs pressured into consolidation or niche specialization.  
  - Watch next: clear profitability metrics by segment, disclosures on compute-credit deals, and evidence of AI-driven productivity gains in non-tech sectors.
