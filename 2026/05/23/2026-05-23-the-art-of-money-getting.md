# The Art of Money Getting

- Score: 175 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48247208) | Link: https://kk.org/cooltools/book-freak-210-the-art-of-money-getting/

- TL;DR  
  Barnum’s 1880 booklet distills his hustle into four rules: choose work that fits you, shun debt, work intensely, and guard integrity. The newsletter reframes them as modern prompts: audit your vocation, attack debts, and pick one task to execute wholeheartedly. Hacker News readers mostly agree, but debate how realistic “true vocation” is: many fell into tech for money, struggle with integrity at work, or find fulfillment only in uncompensated side projects.

- Comment pulse  
  - Tech as wrong vocation → Many chased pay into tech, feel drained or compromised; integrity of colleagues matters more for happiness than compensation or prestige.  
  - Finding your knack is hard → One view: talent feels easy; another: skill is built through slog and feedback — counterpoint: reality probably mixes both.  
  - Passion vs market demand → Barnum-style “right vocation” collides with bills; many choose tolerable jobs, keeping artistic work and experiments as lower-pressure side projects.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Barnum’s rules assume some career mobility; they’re most useful as constraints when you do have options, not universal prescriptions.  
  - Impact: For tech workers facing AI disruption, revisiting vocation, debt exposure, and integrity may matter more than optimizing for current salaries.  
  - Watch next: Study longitudinally: do people who align work with strengths, avoid debt, and prioritize reputation achieve better wellbeing than pure income-maximizers?
