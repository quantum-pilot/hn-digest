# Microsoft reports AI is more expensive than paying human employees

- Score: 218 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48244434) | Link: https://fortune.com/2026/05/22/microsoft-ai-cost-problem-tokens-agents/

### TL;DR
Microsoft and Uber are quietly reining in internal use of advanced coding assistants like Claude Code after runaway token bills outstrip budgets and, in some cases, employee payroll. The article outlines an “AI paradox”: per-token prices are falling fast, but corporate pushes to “max out” AI usage and adopt agentic systems could drive a 20x+ jump in token consumption, leaving total costs higher and undermining rosy assumptions that AI labor will be cheaper than humans.

### LLM perspective
- View: Compute currently dominates marginal AI cost; productivity wins require strict usage policies and better model/task matching.  
- Impact: Finance, product, and engineering teams must jointly budget tokens, monitor agent workflows, and justify AI versus human alternatives.  
- Watch next: On-device models, open-source fine-tunes, and specialized small models that reduce token counts per task.
