# ICE to Pay Thomson Reuters $125M to Find Voter Fraud

- Score: 222 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48993448) | Link: https://www.404media.co/ice-to-pay-thomson-reuters-125-million-to-find-voter-fraud/

- TL;DR  
  - DHS’s ICE plans to pay Thomson Reuters $125M for deep access to its commercial data-broker databases—names, SSNs, ethnicity, geolocation, social media—to hunt “voter fraud” and immigration fraud. The contract would enable continuous monitoring of millions of people and comes amid Trump’s attempts to cast doubt on US elections. Hacker News commenters focus less on technical details than on civil-liberties risks: warrant-less surveillance via data brokers, ICE’s drift toward a domestic intelligence force, and the likelihood of weaponized or fabricated “fraud.”

- Comment pulse  
  - ICE mission creep into elections → commenters see an emerging ‘secret police’ bypassing local accountability, justified by statistically tiny voter-fraud rates, but useful for intimidation.  
  - Data-broker angle → Californians can demand Thomson Reuters delete records; some fear opt-outs get framed as “hiding fraud” — counterpoint: others say spin is inevitable.  
  - Surveillance law concern → buying commercially-harvested SSNs, geolocation, social feeds exploits third-party doctrine, letting agencies sidestep warrants and Patriot-Act-era limits on domestic intelligence.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: The problem isn’t this contract but the unregulated marketplace letting governments rent a de facto nationwide surveillance system.  
  - Impact: Normalizing election-linked data-mining by immigration agencies could chill lawful voting, especially among naturalized citizens and mixed-status families.  
  - Watch next: Court challenges over warrantless purchases, federal privacy legislation, and whether Thomson Reuters’ newsroom pushes back on reputational risks.
