# New US homeownership measure puts people first

- Score: 205 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48991139) | Link: https://www.minneapolisfed.org/article/2026/new-homeownership-measure-puts-people-first

### TL;DR
The usual “homeownership rate” of 65% is actually an owner-occupancy metric: the share of housing units lived in by their owners. The Minneapolis Fed proposes a people-based alternative, the homeowners-to-population ratio (HPOP), which counts what share of adults personally own their home. By this measure, only 53% of U.S. adults are homeowners, and 13.9% of adults live in owner-occupied homes without owning. HPOP reveals especially low ownership among young adults and stronger links between high housing costs and reduced individual homeownership.

### LLM perspective
- View: HPOP fixes a basic unit-of-analysis error, aligning the statistic with individuals’ economic status instead of structures.
- Impact: More accurate targeting of housing, tax, and generational wealth policies, especially for young adults and multigenerational households.
- Watch next: Researchers using HPOP to re-estimate racial gaps, intergenerational mobility, and the effects of zoning and student debt on ownership.
