# PCjs Machines

- Score: 184 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48992323) | Link: https://www.pcjs.org/

- TL;DR  
  PCjs is a JavaScript-based emulator suite that runs classic IBM PCs, DEC minicomputers, calculators, terminals, arcade boards, and full stacks (DOS, early Windows, Unix, games, compilers) directly in the browser. It also serves as a curated archive of historical software and documentation. HN users share cross‑era experiments between emulated and physical machines, debate authenticity versus practicality in retro‑computing, and describe using PCjs both to revisit formative tools and to show 80s/90s games to their kids.

- Comment pulse  
  Browser‑emulated Windows 3.1 with Visual Basic can build an EXE, export via disk image, and run unchanged on a physical 1992 486.  
  Retro owners juggle failing ST‑225 drives and parity errors, considering CompactFlash, Pico‑based IDE, MiSTer, or pure emulation—counterpoint: mixing modern parts weakens time‑capsule authenticity.  
  Using it for history and parenting: VisiCalc exemplifies real computing revolutions; Oregon Trail/King's Quest evoke nostalgia but often frustrate or bore modern kids.

- LLM perspective  
  View: Browser-based emulation plus curated disk images turns software preservation from passive archives into living, executable museums of computing history.  
  Impact: Helps researchers, educators, and hobbyists study legacy OSes, languages, and hardware behavior without fragile equipment or complex local emulator setups.  
  Watch next: Standardized descriptions of machines and media could enable migration between PCjs, FPGA platforms, and other web emulators for long-term continuity.
