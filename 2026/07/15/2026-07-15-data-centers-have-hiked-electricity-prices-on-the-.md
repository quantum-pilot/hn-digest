# Data centers have hiked electricity prices on the public by $23B

- Score: 194 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48914683) | Link: https://fortune.com/2026/07/14/data-centers-23-billion-electricity-bills/

### TL;DR
The article explains how soaring data center electricity demand is reshaping U.S. power prices, using a PJM market report that links data centers to $23.1 billion in added capacity-market revenues through 2028. Because utilities socialize many grid upgrades, a large share of those costs can land on households and small businesses, especially where consumer advocates can’t argue for shifting costs toward big industrial users. Data centers’ flexible loads may also sidestep “peak” charges, leaving less-flexible customers holding more of the bill.

### Comment pulse
- The $23B figure is total extra PJM capacity revenue from data-center-driven prices, not “public-only”; non–data-center burden is closer to $16B.
- Who pays for grid upgrades is a pure policy choice; some regions make new developments fund infrastructure directly, others socialize it across all ratepayers.
- $23B is ~4–5% of U.S. power revenue, but impacts are highly localized; debate over whether data centers are grid “anchor tenants” or just offloading costs.
