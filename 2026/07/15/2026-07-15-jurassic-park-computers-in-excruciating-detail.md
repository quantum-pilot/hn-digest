# Jurassic Park computers in excruciating detail

- Score: 865 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48915709) | Link: https://fabiensanglard.net/jurrasic_park_computers/index.html

## TL;DR
Fabien Sanglard re-watches Jurassic Park and forensically catalogs every on-screen computer: PowerBooks, Quadras, SGI Indigo/Crimson workstations, PLI disk arrays, a Motorola Envoy prototype, and rows of Thinking Machines CM-5 supercomputers. He digs into specs, pricing, storage capacities, continuity errors, and how graphics were live-driven on set. HN comments add first-hand anecdotes about loaner supercomputers, CRT sync engineering, and how much of Nedry’s “Nedryland” UI was real Mac/IRIX software rather than random screen gibberish.
