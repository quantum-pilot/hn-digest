# Stripe and Advent have made a joint offer to acquire PayPal – sources

- Score: 318 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48915953) | Link: https://www.reuters.com/business/finance/stripe-advent-offer-buy-paypal-more-than-53-billion-sources-say-2026-07-15/

### TL;DR
Stripe and Advent International have quietly proposed a heavily financed takeover of PayPal, valuing it above $53B with a substantial premium over its recent stock price. They would co-own PayPal 50/50 and keep it intact rather than carving it up, and PayPal has not yet responded. Hacker News reactions focus on reduced competition in online payments, greater fragility if a single player controls multiple rails, conflict between Stripe’s stricter risk rules and PayPal’s laxer practices, and doubt that antitrust regulators will intervene.

### Comment pulse
- Merchants worry losing PayPal–Stripe rivalry will mean higher processing fees and worse support, since both feel unresponsive and account issues can take months to resolve.  
- Users cite Stripe’s automatic bans on cannabis-, adult-, and ketamine-adjacent businesses, while others say card networks impose these rules and PayPal just enforces them loosely.  
- People fear one conglomerate owning PayPal, Venmo, Braintree, Xoom will spike market concentration, yet doubt U.S. antitrust—except maybe state attorneys general—will force divestitures.

### LLM perspective
- View: If approved, Stripe shifts from developer-first processor to hybrid consumer platform, inheriting PayPal’s wallet, P2P, and buyer-protection baggage.  
- Impact: High-risk merchants should expect tighter automated compliance; diversifying processors and pretesting backups becomes critical to avoid catastrophic account freezes.  
- Watch next: Key signals: regulators’ initial statements, investor reaction to leverage load, and whether Stripe floats preemptive divestitures of Venmo or Braintree.
