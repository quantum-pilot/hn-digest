# SpaceX bond worth 10% less than issue price – heading for junk bond status

- Score: 513 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48920181) | Link: https://www.ft.com/content/3a023b95-66c3-41e1-b0ce-df752a499541

TL;DR  
SpaceX recently issued about $25B of long‑term bonds at a 6.5% coupon; they’re already trading ~10% below face value, pushing yields toward 7.4% and near “junk” territory. That doesn’t change the cost on this bond, but it signals markets now see SpaceX as riskier, so any future borrowing will be materially more expensive. Combined with heavy capex, negative cash flow, and leveraged exposure to Musk’s broader empire, HN sees this as a warning about SpaceX’s cost of capital and bankruptcy risk, not a short‑term crisis.
