# Sleep regularity is a stronger predictor of mortality risk than sleep duration (2023)

- Score: 643 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48919363) | Link: https://academic.oup.com/sleep/article/47/1/zsad253/7280269

- TL;DR  
Researchers analyzed about 100k UK adults with wrist-worn accelerometers and found that how consistent your sleep-wake timing is across days predicted all-cause mortality better than total sleep duration. Irregular sleepers had elevated risks of death from cardiovascular, cancer, and other causes, even after adjusting for shift work, income, and lifestyle factors. Hacker News comments debate causality and confounding, while swapping practical advice: prioritize regular bed/wake times, consider magnesium or melatonin cautiously, and remember observational studies guide hypotheses, not prescriptions.
