# Claude Fable produced a counterexample to the Jacobian Conjecture

- Score: 649 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48973869) | Link: https://xcancel.com/__alpoge__/status/2079028340955197566

## TL;DR
- An explicit polynomial map ℂ³→ℂ³ has been found with constant nonzero Jacobian determinant (−2) but three distinct preimages of one point, so it is not injective. This directly disproves the Jacobian Conjecture, which claimed such maps must always be invertible. The example was discovered with help from Anthropic’s Claude “Fable” model, stunning mathematicians who expected any counterexample to be extremely high‑degree. Discussion explores implications for related Dixmier/Poisson conjectures, human stories around failed attempts, and how AI‑assisted search will reshape pure math.
