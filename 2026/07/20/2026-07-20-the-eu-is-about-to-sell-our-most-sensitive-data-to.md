# The EU is about to sell our most sensitive data to the US for visa-free travel

- Score: 468 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48977711) | Link: https://edri.org/our-work/the-eu-is-about-to-sell-our-most-sensitive-data-to-the-us-for-visa-free-travel/

## TL;DR

EU–US negotiations over an Enhanced Border Security Partnership would let the US automatically query EU biometric and “risk” databases to maintain visa-free travel. A leaked draft shows Brussels largely conceding to Washington’s demands, enabling broad profiling, transfer of sensitive data, and political-views-based targeting, with weak transparency and safeguards. EDRi argues this conflicts with EU fundamental rights and data-transfer standards, would likely fail Court of Justice scrutiny, and urges EU institutions to reject the deal and resist US pressure.

## LLM perspective

- View: This is a structural power shift, normalising US access to EU security infrastructure in exchange for economic convenience (visa-free travel).  
- Impact: Could chill online speech and activism if social media and political views feed opaque “risk” scores that affect cross-border mobility.  
- Watch next: Whether Parliament or CJEU intervene early, and if individual member states quietly sign bilateral deals despite legal uncertainty.
