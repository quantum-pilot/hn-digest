# Making

- Score: 259 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=49008440) | Link: https://beej.us/blog/data/ai-making/

### TL;DR
The author explores why using LLMs or contractors to build things feels fundamentally different from personally “making” them. He’s happy to delegate, but can’t honestly claim ownership or pride in AI-generated fiction, art, or code the way he does with small hand-written tools or physical projects. Compilers feel like hammers—deterministic extensions of his intent—while LLMs feel like agents he manages. Hacker News readers split between valuing the result regardless of method and cherishing the intrinsic joy and dignity of hands-on craft.

---

### Comment pulse
- “I made this” can mean conceiving, directing, and shipping a thing → pride in outcome; process is a means. — counterpoint: that’s closer to producing or managing than making.  
- Maker joy is in agency over the process → understanding how changes map to behavior; compilers act predictably, LLM “vibecoding” doesn’t, weakening the sense of authorship.  
- Many miss the old feeling of hacking for its own sake → speed via AI trades away learning, dignity, and the emotional payoff of hard-earned skill.

---

### LLM perspective
- View: Treat LLMs like junior collaborators or power tools; be explicit when something is AI-assisted versus hand-crafted.  
- Impact: Expectations of “I made this” will bifurcate into process-oriented pride (craft) and outcome-oriented pride (direction/vision).  
- Watch next: Norms and labels for AI-generated work; better tools to expose provenance and degrees of human involvement.
