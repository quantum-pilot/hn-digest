### TL;DR
News of John C. Dvorak’s death sparked strong nostalgia on HN. Commenters remember him as a formative tech columnist and broadcaster from PC Magazine, InfoWorld, MacUser, TechTV, TWiT, and Cranky Geeks, with brash, often-wrong but entertaining takes and a deliberate curmudgeon persona. Many note he was August Dvorak’s nephew, not the keyboard’s inventor. Reflections broaden into how early personal-computing media felt more fun and personality-driven than today’s more mature, diffuse tech discourse.
