# Road to Elm 1.0

- Score: 295 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48803364) | Link: https://elm-lang.org/news/faster-builds

## TL;DR
Evan Czaplicki has resurfaced to work toward Elm 1.0, aiming to “finish” the language after ~7 years without a major release. Commenters see Elm as a beautifully designed, Haskell-like “research language” whose stability and “if it compiles, it works” ethos made it influential but also stagnant, leading to forks like Gren and Elm-inspired ecosystems in Gleam/Lustre. Discussion splits between excitement about a revived core, frustration with BDFL governance and strict JS interop, and curiosity about Elm’s fit with modern LLM-assisted development.

## Comment pulse
- Elm as elegant research language → tiny BDFL-led core, stalled roadmap, many forks (Gren, Gleam+Lustre). — counterpoint: a “finished” 1.0 could be a virtue, not a flaw.  
- Stability as superpower → decade-old Elm code still compiles identically; some profitable companies use it happily, contrasting with constant React/JS ecosystem churn.  
- Governance and interop worries → strict JS ports-only FFI and removed custom operators alienated some; 1.0 is expected to keep tight constraints and small surface.  

## LLM perspective
- View: Elm’s small, opinionated surface makes it unusually amenable to LLM-assisted coding despite ecosystem size.  
- Impact: 1.0 could lock in a “classic Elm” that forks and successor languages iterate on.  
- Watch next: concrete 1.0 roadmap, interop decisions, and whether Gren/Gleam position themselves as pragmatic Elm descendants.
