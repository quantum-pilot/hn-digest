# How Has Roman Concrete Lasted for Millennia? 1,900-Year-Old Latrine Offers Clues

- Score: 254 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48943142) | Link: https://www.smithsonianmag.com/smart-news/how-has-roman-concrete-lasted-for-millennia-a-1900-year-old-latrine-offers-new-clues-about-the-materials-impressive-durability-180989115/

- TL;DR  
Researchers sampled untouched concrete from a 1,900-year-old latrine at Hadrian’s Villa to probe why Roman structures last so long. Besides the known lime–volcanic ash pozzolanic reaction, they found that very slow carbonation formed calcite, progressively filling pores and microcracks so the concrete “self-heals” and strengthens over centuries. This supports earlier quicklime-based self-repair theories and elevates carbonates to a central role. HN discussion contrasts Roman lime concretes with rebar-heavy Portland mixes, highlighting rebar corrosion, maintenance, design lifetimes, and economics.
