# Short sellers notch $8.7B profit as SpaceX shares dip to IPO price

- Score: 150 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48948435) | Link: https://www.reuters.com/business/media-telecom/short-sellers-rack-up-87-bln-profit-spacex-slips-below-ipo-price-ortex-2026-07-16/

### TL;DR
SpaceX’s stock, IPO’d at $135 in June 2026 and briefly peaking above $225, has slid back to around its offering price, briefly dipping below it. That drop has delivered an estimated $8.7B in paper profits to short sellers, with roughly 49% of the free float now on loan, mostly for short positions, according to Ortex. The move reflects skepticism over SpaceX’s lofty valuation and heavy, debt-funded AI investment, while also setting up extreme volatility because of the huge short interest.

### LLM perspective
- View: This is a textbook post-IPO reset, where exuberant pricing collides with balance-sheet reality, especially around speculative AI bets.  
- Impact: High short interest plus Musk’s anti-short stance raise odds of sharp squeezes hurting retail traders and momentum funds.  
- Watch next: Disclosures on AI capex, debt terms, and Starlink profitability; any Musk-led campaign against shorts; regulatory attention to concentrated short exposure.
