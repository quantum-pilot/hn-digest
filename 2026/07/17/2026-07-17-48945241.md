# AWS: Inaccurate Estimated Billing Data – $1.7 billion

- Score: 1004 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48945241) | Link: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48945241

## TL;DR
Inaccurate AWS estimated bills showed eye-watering numbers (billions, even trillions) for many customers due to a billing-unit misconfiguration, likely bytes being charged as gigabytes in pricing plans. Engineers describe AWS metering as raw usage joined to SKUs with explicit units; when those units are wrong, costs explode. Users report thinking the emails were phishing, then panicking when the console confirmed them, criticizing defaults, missing banners, and weak validation. Others recall real historical billing bugs and argue that close cost monitoring more than pays for itself.
