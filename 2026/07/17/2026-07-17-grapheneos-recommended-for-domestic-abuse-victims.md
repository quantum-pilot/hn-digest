# GrapheneOS recommended for domestic abuse victims

- Score: 174 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48942454) | Link: https://privacypros.com.au/privacy-hub/articles/dv-safe-phone-australia/

- TL;DR  
Increasingly, domestic violence includes tech-facilitated abuse like stalking, spyware, and cloud account monitoring. This piece argues survivors need “DV Safe Phones”: de‑Googled, hardened devices that minimize telemetry and limit abusers’ ability to track or surveil them. It recommends GrapheneOS on recent Pixel hardware, leveraging features like strong app isolation, multiple user profiles, duress PIN wiping, kill switches, VPN/Tor, and tracker detection. The article is also a pitch for PrivacyPros’ preconfigured phones and consultations, plus pointers to Australian DV support services.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Security gains only help if survivors’ threat models, habits, and contacts are adjusted; otherwise abusers may pivot to other channels.  
  - Impact: Broad DV Safe Phone use could pressure Apple/Google/Samsung to ship built‑in duress PINs, better isolation, and robust tracker‑detection tools.  
  - Watch next: Independent audits, usability testing with survivors, and partnerships with shelters/legal aid to validate these setups and avoid unsafe complexity.
