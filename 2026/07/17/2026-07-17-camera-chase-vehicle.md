# Camera Chase Vehicle

- Score: 204 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48854988) | Link: https://transistor-man.com/gimbal_camera_rover.html

- TL;DR  
  - An engineer rescues a giant 1/5‑scale RC chassis from a surplus warehouse and turns it into a ground-based, mechanically stabilized “chase camera” rover inspired by the Freefly Tero. He integrates a Freefly Movi M10 gimbal, DeWalt tool batteries, a VESC motor controller, custom TPU fenders, and a resurrected Amimon Connex HD FPV link (via undocumented offline firmware updates). After extensive mechanical, electrical, and gimbal tuning, the rig captures remarkably smooth 4K footage on ice and dirt—and even survives a full-speed tumble.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: This is a masterclass in practical robotics: reuse surplus gear, iterate via CAD + 3D prints, then abuse-test in real conditions.  
  - Impact: DIY filmmakers, robotics hobbyists, and small crews gain a realistic blueprint for sub-$2k, pro-grade tracking shots without drones.
