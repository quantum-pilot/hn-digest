# Evidence of inconsistencies in evaluation process and selection of winners

- Score: 435 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48946010) | Link: https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/kaggle-measuring-agi/discussion/724918#3498423

### TL;DR
DeepMind’s Kaggle hackathon on “Measuring Progress Toward AGI” awarded a $25k grand prize to Medley-Bench, a benchmark for metacognition under social pressure. Multiple participants now allege the judging violated Kaggle’s own criteria of quality, defensibility, clarity, and verifiability: they point to opaque use of the benchmark SDK, missing result traces, contradictory claims vs. graphs, ad‑hoc weights, and basic logical/data bugs. Others complain their technically robust but less “flashy” entries were ignored. Hacker News threads generalize this to AI-heavy hackathons, opaque judging, and Kaggle’s research credibility.
