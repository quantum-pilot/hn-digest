# The Computer at the Bottom of a Canal

- Score: 139 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48956231) | Link: https://negroniventurestudios.com/2026/07/18/the-computer-at-the-bottom-of-a-canal/

## TL;DR
In the 1980s, Scottish hi‑fi maker Linn built Rekursiv, an object‑oriented CPU that enforced hardware memory safety, did garbage collection in silicon, treated RAM+disk as a single persistent object store, and let you load a custom instruction set per language. It failed commercially because general‑purpose RISC CPUs were improving ~52% per year and quickly outpaced any bespoke chip. The article argues those four “weird” ideas are now mainstream (CHERI, IBM i, domain‑specific accelerators) and that today’s economics finally favor such contrarian silicon again.

## LLM perspective
- View: Rekursiv shows that “right ideas, wrong decade” is common; timing and abstraction boundaries matter more than raw technical insight.  
- Impact: Startups building custom AI or security silicon can succeed if they design for a broad workload and portable semantics.  
- Watch next: CHERI adoption, RISC‑V-based accelerators, and languages explicitly designed for LLMs will indicate whether this new bespoke-silicon wave is real.
