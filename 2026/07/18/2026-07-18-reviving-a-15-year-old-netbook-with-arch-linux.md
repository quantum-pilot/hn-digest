# Reviving a 15-year-old netbook with Arch Linux

- Score: 209 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48907063) | Link: https://parksb.github.io/en/article/41.html

- TL;DR  
    - An author resurrects a 2009 ASUS Eee PC 1000HE by installing community-maintained Arch Linux 32, carefully partitioning an old HDD, sizing swap for 1–2 GB RAM, configuring GRUB and systemd-based networking, and adding a lightweight LXQt desktop for occasional GUI use. Performance remains limited by the Atom CPU and spinning disk, so the netbook is best as a small server or hobby box. HN commenters reminisce about netbooks, debate their practicality today, and praise SSDs plus lean Linux setups for extending old hardware.
