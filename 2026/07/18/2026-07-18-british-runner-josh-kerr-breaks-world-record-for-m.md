# British runner Josh Kerr breaks world record for mile which stood for 27 years

- Score: 105 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48958814) | Link: https://news.sky.com/story/british-runner-josh-kerr-breaks-world-record-for-mile-which-had-stood-for-27-years-13564688

- TL;DR  
  - Scottish middle-distance runner Josh Kerr broke Hicham El Guerrouj’s 27‑year men’s mile world record at the London Diamond League, running 3:42.66 before 60,000 fans. He executed his “Project 222” plan almost perfectly, passing 1500m in 3:27.7 and finishing strongly after early pacers dropped away. The record returns the iconic mile mark to Britain, and discussion centres on his precise pacing, near-sprint intensity, and how much modern shoes, tracks, pacers and training methods contribute versus raw human ability.

- Comment pulse  
  - Kerr’s pacing and performance were extraordinary → sustained ~13.7s per 100m for almost the whole race, essentially sprinting for four minutes.  
  - Tech and training, not genetics, drive gains → carbon shoes, pacers, nutrition and supplements stack advantages — counterpoint: why then a 27‑year world-record drought?  
  - Pacer use is allowed but constrained → pacers must start normally and can’t rotate runners in, unlike banned marathon projects with relay pacer squads.
