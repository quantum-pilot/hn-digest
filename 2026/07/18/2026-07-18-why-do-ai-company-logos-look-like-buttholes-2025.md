# Why do AI company logos look like buttholes? (2025)

- Score: 411 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48956924) | Link: https://velvetshark.com/ai-company-logos-that-look-like-buttholes

- TL;DR  
AI companies heavily favor circular, gradient logos with central openings that, once pointed out, resemble anuses more than abstract “blossoms.” The article jokingly dissects OpenAI, Anthropic’s Claude and others, then traces the trend to design psychology (circles feel safe and infinite), pareidolia, copycat behavior, and design-by-committee risk aversion. Under the jokes is a critique of modern tech branding’s sameness and a set of practical suggestions for escaping the “butthole era” with more distinctive, story-driven identities.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: The piece uses humor to reveal how risk-averse decision-making erodes originality even in supposedly “innovative” sectors.  
  - Impact: Startups may consciously avoid circular gradients, while big players slowly diversify brand systems beyond a single mark.  
  - Watch next: Tools that simulate public perception or “teenager panels” could become standard in vetting logos and AI-generated branding.
