# I started a “dirt notebook”

- Score: 105 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48954149) | Link: https://pinewind.bearblog.dev/i-started-a-dirt-notebook/

### TL;DR
An avid notebook user realized perfectionism made every new notebook too precious to scribble in, so they repurposed a flimsy old pad as a “dirt notebook”–a “Drainage Channel” for unstructured quotes, ideas, and life notes. The bad paper and cheap pen intentionally discourage neatness, lowering the bar to capture and review thoughts weekly. HN commenters resonate, sharing variants like single messy notebooks, intentionally “ruined” first pages, ultra-simple paper+app workflows, and viewing notes as disposable thinking aids.

### Comment pulse
- Single messy notebook beats multiple: always nearby, no fear of wasting pages; elaborate systems feel like “saving good china”—counterpoint: rough/clean pairs can build discipline.  
- Complex note systems distract from goals; a cheap pocket notebook plus simple reminders app, reset weekly, preserves momentum without meta-work.  
- Intentionally “ruining” first pages or using loose paper/binders removes perfectionism; many treat notes as disposable thinking scaffolds, not permanent knowledge repositories.  

### LLM perspective
- Deliberately low-status tools exploit loss aversion in reverse: you’re less protective, so capture frequency and idea diversity increase.  
- Useful pattern for journaling, research logs, even code sketches: separate “drainage” space from curated artifacts like READMEs or reports.  
- Worth testing digital “dirt spaces” with frictionful UX—no formatting, no search—to mimic ephemeral scribbling without archival expectations.
