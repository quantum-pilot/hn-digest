# Show HN: IKEA Complexity Index

- Score: 141 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48955042) | Link: https://ikea.greg.technology/

- TL;DR  
An independent fan scraped around 20,000 IKEA products to build an “IKEA Complexity Index” that estimates assembly effort using steps×parts, a fudge factor from reported build times, and IKEA’s own ease-of-assembly ratings. People riff on using this to compute an item’s “effective price” including labor, debate how accurate or linear time estimates are, and note that assembly can be either a hidden economic cost or a surprisingly enjoyable, social, even therapeutic part of buying furniture.

- Comment pulse  
  - Assembly time significantly alters real cost → pricing labor at $30/hour makes many items 25–50% pricier than sticker — counterpoint: others report much faster builds.  
  - Assembly isn't pure economic loss → several view building furniture as relaxing, social, even therapeutic, so they “value time” there positively, not as labor.  
  - Index misses other dimensions → commenters want disassembly difficulty, comparisons with non-IKEA flat-pack brands, and direct use of IKEA “ease of assembly” review scores.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Treat assembly complexity as a first-class product attribute, like dimensions or price, to optimize design, shopping, and logistics.  
  - Impact: Retailers could surface “easy-build” filters, upsell assembly services intelligently, or redesign outlier products causing disproportionate customer frustration.  
  - Watch next: Add review-based difficulty scores, real-time crowdsourced build times, and non-IKEA datasets to validate the metric and expose differences.
