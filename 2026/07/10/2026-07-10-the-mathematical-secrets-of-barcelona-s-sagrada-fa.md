### TL;DR
The discussion portrays Sagrada Familia as a showcase of “embodied mathematics”: Gaudí relied on physical experiments—like hanging chains and string‑and‑weight models—to discover structurally optimal curves and proportions, not on formal equations. Commenters stress that pre‑modern builders routinely used proportional systems (fractions of the building itself) instead of meters, tying beauty to ratios, symmetry, and material limits. Others push back that the article veers into numerology, overstating the novelty and mathematical depth of Gaudí’s methods compared with classical architectural math.
