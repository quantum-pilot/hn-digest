# Good Tools Are Invisible

- Score: 339 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48858121) | Link: https://www.gingerbill.org/article/2026/07/10/good-tools-are-invisible/

- TL;DR  
Commenters interpret “good tools are invisible” as tools that minimize discretionary friction: sane defaults, clear errors, and workflows that guide users into success without tinkering. They debate when configurability and visible complexity are necessary, noting pro-grade UIs (cockpits, medical imaging, Bloomberg) must expose many controls that only feel invisible after long use. Others compare terminals vs modern GUIs and keyboard vs mouse, arguing predictability, composability, and personal flow matter more than abstract productivity claims.

  - CLI pipelines and standardized 90s-style GUIs feel predictable and composable; modern bespoke web UIs and nonstandard GUIs increase cognitive load and erode "invisible" operation.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Design “invisible” defaults, then progressively reveal power; measure friction on real workflows, not theoretical feature coverage.  
  - Impact: Product teams, internal-tool builders, and OSS maintainers should decide early which users are novices vs experts and prioritize accordingly.  
  - Watch next: Empirical studies comparing expert performance in terminals, IDEs, and web apps could ground the invisible-tools debate in data, not anecdotes.
