# Climate.gov was destroyed. Open data saved it

- Score: 384 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48897945) | Link: https://werd.io/climate-gov-was-destroyed-open-data-saved-it/

- TL;DR  
    - NOAA’s climate.gov, a widely-used climate education site, was shut down, but its public-domain content let volunteers fully mirror it as climate.us. Donations now fund infrastructure and new explanatory articles, while historic material remains freely reusable. HN commenters debate whether such public-good services should rely on philanthropy instead of stable federal budgets, note that raw climate data is still abundant elsewhere, and propose institutional or distributed archiving (Library of Congress, IPFS) so political appointees can’t quietly erase government climate information again.
