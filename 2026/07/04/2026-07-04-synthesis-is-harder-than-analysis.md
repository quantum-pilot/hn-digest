# Synthesis is harder than analysis

- Score: 142 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48782219) | Link: https://surfingcomplexity.blog/2026/07/03/synthesis-is-harder-than-analysis/

- TL;DR  
    The piece argues that “synthesis” (forming a coherent picture of the whole) is fundamentally harder than “analysis” (reasoning about parts), yet our tools, training, and culture bias us toward analysis. Using differentiation vs integration as metaphor, it claims operators of complex systems—like SREs—struggle most with synthesis: predicting global behavior, integrating partial signals, and designing resilient architectures. Commenters link this to emergence in physics, NP vs P in CS, and working across abstraction layers, while questioning the tightness of the calculus mapping.
