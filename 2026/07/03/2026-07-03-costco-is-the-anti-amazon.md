# Costco is the anti-Amazon

- Score: 260 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48776044) | Link: https://phenomenalworld.org/analysis/the-anti-amazon/

- TL;DR  
  Costco is framed as the anti-Amazon: a low-friction, no-frills warehouse club that monetizes memberships, limits choice, pays relatively well, and largely sidesteps last‑mile delivery. Commenters debate whether customer driving is environmentally worse than parcel delivery, noting it depends on distance, basket size, and errand-chaining. Others see Costco’s strategy as “wisely” avoiding a brutally hard logistics problem, though they point out Instacart fills the gap. Several discuss how car-centric suburbs and US housing norms make Costco powerful yet hard to replicate abroad.
