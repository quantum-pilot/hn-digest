# An American Privacy Emergency

- Score: 390 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48768992) | Link: https://scottaaronson.blog/?p=9902

## TL;DR
A new US Commerce Department directive (DAO 216-26) bans “noise infusion” and related privacy techniques like differential privacy, allowing only crude coarsening of released data. Critics say this forces agencies into a bad tradeoff: either release far less data or release re-identifiable data, undermining both research and personal privacy. Commenters argue over motives (authoritarian control, partisan census fights, corporate influence) and over whether this truly constitutes a “privacy emergency” versus merely clumsy, regressive policy.
