# Guerrilla London bus ads mock Kylie Jenner’s Meta glasses campaign

- Score: 197 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48930011) | Link: https://hyperallergic.com/guerrilla-london-bus-ads-mock-kylie-jenners-meta-glasses-campaign/

### TL;DR
UK activist group Everyone Hates Elon installed a lenticular spoof ad at a London bus stop near Meta’s HQ, transforming Kylie Jenner’s Meta smart-glasses promo image into an X‑ray skeleton with “We’re always watching.” The piece attacks camera‑equipped glasses as tools for voyeurism and real‑world surveillance, citing extended recording times, harassment videos, and facial-recognition experiments that re-identified passersby. Hyperallergic frames it as protest art highlighting how law, ethics, and billionaires’ priorities lag behind invasive consumer tech.

---

### Comment pulse
- Ad as artwork → Commenters praise the lenticular execution and They Live homage; some estimate costs at roughly $10–20 per poster, not hundreds.  
- Product design and norms → Debate over why Meta centers cameras instead of audio, HUD, and translation; some foresee normalized constant recording, others want distinctive, cameraless smart glasses.  
- Power, policy, and agency → One camp calls for state-level restrictions on such tech; others argue public education and respecting individual choice beat coercive regulation—counterpoint: most users underestimate surveillance harms.
