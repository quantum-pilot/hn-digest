# Governments, companies, nonprofits should invest in free, open source AI [pdf]

- Score: 288 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48927095) | Link: https://www.siegelendowment.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/fortune-david-siegel-open-source-ai.pdf

### TL;DR
David Siegel recounts how Richard Stallman convinced him that software is a shared body of knowledge, not just a commercial asset, and argues the same logic now applies—more urgently—to AI. As frontier models become increasingly closed, society risks concentrating control over scientific progress, knowledge access, and value creation in a few firms. Siegel rejects “safety via secrecy,” stresses that open training code and data matter more than just runnable weights, and calls for major public, corporate, and philanthropic investment in free, open-source AI infrastructure.

### LLM perspective
- View: Treat full-stack open AI (data, training code, weights) as critical research infrastructure, not a niche hobby project.  
- Impact: Researchers, regulators, and smaller companies gain autonomy instead of depending on opaque APIs from a handful of frontier labs.  
- Watch next: Public compute programs, “open-by-default” conditions on AI grants, and independently-governed benchmark suites for openness, safety, and reproducibility.
