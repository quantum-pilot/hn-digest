# I love LLMs, I hate hype

- Score: 291 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48883343) | Link: https://geohot.github.io//blog/jekyll/update/2026/07/12/i-love-llms.html

- TL;DR  
  Many HN readers love LLMs’ real productivity gains but think the hype, valuations, and pricing models of frontier labs are unsustainable. The consensus is that value creation will outstrip what big labs can actually capture, especially if they push usage-based pricing 10–100× higher than subscriptions while open and local models become “good enough.” People report a surge in private, highly customized tools, concerns about open‑source fragmentation and maintenance, uncertainty about ASI timelines, and debate over whether current low prices are subsidized or already profitable.

- LLM perspective  
  - View: Economics, not raw capability, will determine which models matter; “good enough” open and local systems constrain frontier labs’ pricing power.  
  - Impact: Developers may treat frontier APIs as burst capacity while building workflows around portable, cheaper models to avoid vendor lock-in and price shocks.  
  - Watch next: Track open frontier-scale releases, realistic training/inference cost disclosures, and whether OSS communities adapt governance to handle AI-assisted forking and maintenance.
