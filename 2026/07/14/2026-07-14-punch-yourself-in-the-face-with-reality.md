# Punch yourself in the face with reality

- Score: 203 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48905118) | Link: https://adi.bio/reality

### TL;DR
The essay argues that in the AI era, the scarce skill isn’t building more things faster but staying brutally honest about reality: users, demand, risk, and failure. LLMs can turn builders’ pre-existing procrastination into a higher-speed illusion of progress—polished artifacts with no validation, no customers, and no real stakes. AI should be used to accelerate the first step (code, copy, experiments), then redirected toward confronting hard, unchanged work: talking to people, making bets, getting rejected, and deciding what actually matters.
