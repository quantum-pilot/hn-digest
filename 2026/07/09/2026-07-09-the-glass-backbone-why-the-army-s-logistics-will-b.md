# The glass backbone: Why the Army's logistics will break in the next war

- Score: 265 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48845442) | Link: https://mwi.westpoint.edu/the-glass-backbone-why-the-armys-logistics-will-break-in-the-next-war/

TL;DR
- The piece argues the US Army’s logistics are a fragile “glass backbone”: optimized for efficiency and peacetime cost, not survival in a drone‑ and missile‑saturated peer war. Traditional tooth‑to‑tail thinking underfunds supply, transport, and maintenance even as those nodes become primary targets; once the tail is cut, combat units are useless. HN commenters link this to Ukraine, Iran, and historical campaigns, stressing long-range strikes, industrial capacity, and even speculative space delivery as reshaping what “rear area” safety means.
