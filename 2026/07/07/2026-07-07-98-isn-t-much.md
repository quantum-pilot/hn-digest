# 98% isn't much

- Score: 460 | [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48816959) | Link: https://whynothugo.nl/journal/2026/07/03/98-isnt-very-much/

### TL;DR
Discussion centers on how a “98% success rate” can be deeply misleading. For mass systems, 2% failure can lock out millions, damage reputation, or break network effects, so contexts like payments, immigration, or infrastructure often demand near-100% reliability and broader compatibility. Others note small businesses can’t afford to support every legacy browser or device and may rationally accept exclusions. Commenters also argue our language should shift from percentages to odds or explicit “1 in N” failure framing.

- For many products, 98% coverage is fine → supporting legacy setups costs time and money. — counterpoint: excluded users and network effects can hurt revenue.
